Engineering Education Scheme - Parents Needed
Grade 11 Physics have been accepted into the EES (Engineering Education Scheme). This is a unique and exciting opportunity for Halcyon’s keen engineers. The EES organisation will find a company to work with 4-6 students. Students will meet company engineer representatives  at their headquarters or a nearby university for an introductory workshop. The students will then be assigned an engineering project by the company to complete over the next six months. Upon  completion, students will present to senior engineers and if the project passes evaluation it will be incorporated into company practice.

This is an amazing opportunity, but the number of schools applying outnumber the quantity of companies who know to participate. The organisation has advised that students are permitted to source their own company. 

Grade 11 physics students are looking for a company that would welcome their participation in an engineering project and  is interested in helping educate the next generation of engineers. 

Please email Lori Fritz if you know a company that you think would be suited to this project.
Grade 7 Theatre Trip
Before the end of term, Grade 7 went to the Gielgud Theatre to see the production of The Curious Incident of The Dog in The Night Time. In their English lessons, Grade 7 are currently studying the novel upon which the production is based. The trip aimed to help students understand the characters and themes. Following the trip, students were asked to write a review of the performance for a summative assessment. It was a great production enjoyed by students and staff.
Grade 8 Field Trip
Location: Dickens Museum, 48 Doughty St, London WC1N 2LX
Date: Friday 11 November
Time: 1245-1600
Students will have the opportunity to step back in time and walk in the footsteps of Charles Dickens and his former home. The museum houses the world's finest and most comprehensive collection of material relating to Dickens with over 100,000 items including furniture, personal effects, paintings, prints, photographs, letters, manuscripts, and rare editions. 

Students will leave school at 1245 and return by 1600.  

Students should bring: 
  • A topped-up Oyster card
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Filled water bottle
  • Charged iPad
If you have any questions regarding this trip, please email Gabriel English.

HPC: Exhibition The Vulgar, Fashion Redefined
Location: Barbican Art Gallery
Date: Wednesday 23 November
Time: 1000-1200
Meeting Point: the Barbican (more details to follow upon confirmation)
Cost: £36 (including exhibition entrance ticket)
This new exhibition conceived by exhibition-maker Judith Clark and psychoanalyst Adam Phillip will look at the notion of vulgarity in fashion inviting the viewer to think again about exactly what makes something vulgar and why it is such a sensitive and contested term. Drawn from major public and private collections worldwide, the show will include contributions from leading modern and contemporary designers such as Walter van Beirendonck, Manolo Blahnik, Christian Dior, Marc Jacobs for Louis Vuitton, Stephen Jones, Christian Lacroix, Karl Lagerfeld for Chloé and Chanel, Alexander McQueen for Givenchy, Prada and Vivienne Westwood.
