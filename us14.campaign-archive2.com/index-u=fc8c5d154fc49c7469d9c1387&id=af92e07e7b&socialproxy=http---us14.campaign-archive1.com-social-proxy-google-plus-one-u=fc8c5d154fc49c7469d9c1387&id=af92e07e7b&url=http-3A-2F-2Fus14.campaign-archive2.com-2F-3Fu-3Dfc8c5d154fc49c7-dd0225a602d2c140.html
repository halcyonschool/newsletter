Exchange Programme: ¡¡Bienvenidos Mati y Dioni!! 
On Tuesday 17 January we were thrilled to welcome two Argentinian exchange students to Halcyon - Mati and Dioni! This visit is part of our student Language Exchange Programme with St. Ignatius College in Argentina - some of you might remember our first exchange programme student, Pauli, from last year. This is an international experience we are proud to offer Halcyon students and students from St Ignatius!

We hope to immerse Mati and Dioni fully in Halcyon and London life during their one month visit: from academic to social opportunities. We want to extend a big thank you to their generous host families - the Bodaghi’s and the Schmidt-Das’. We are sure Halcyon students Matis and Kieran will be the perfect 'cicerone mates' for them, along with the rest of students from Grade 10 and 11.

A number of Halcyon students (Eric, Kieran and Nate from G11 and Beckett, Lucia, Matis, Maggie, and Olivia from G10) went to Gatwick on Tuesday to give them a warm welcome after their long trip (see the photo below). On Wednesday Halcyon put on a delicious spread as a welcome breakfast after which our Argentinian peers were given their lesson plan and had a tour of the school. 

On Thursday we held an assembly where Dioni and Mati discussed their school in Argentina and Rio Cuarto, their home town. Eric, Kieran and Nate who went to Argentina in the summer with the exchange programme also shared their experience of the exchange programme. The whole school then participated in an online quiz which tested vocabulary differences between Spanish language in Argentina with Spain. 

We are positive Mati and Dioni will fully integrate themselves in Halcyon life and look forward to supporting them during this fantastic journey.
#BETT2017: Halcyon Presentations
Date: Friday 27 January
Time: 1030
Location: ExCel London
Like Halcyon, BETT believes in creating a better future by transforming education. Their mission is to bring together people, ideas, practices and technologies so that educators and learners can fulfil their potential. Every year, at the ExCel Arena, they host BETTshow - the world’s largest education technology event.  

Tens of thousands of people from all over the world attend the show; this year promises to be bigger and better than ever before. It is therefore a huge honour that Halcyon has been asked to present on the Google Stage at 1030 on Friday 27 January. 

Students and staff will be explaining how the new Google Sites platform has been revolutionising the way that things are done at Halcyon; both in and out of the classroom. Student representatives will be joining from the Halcyonite (Halcyon’s student-led e-magazine) and from the Student Council to talk about some of the innovative ways they are using this awesome interactive tool! If you would like to attend BETT, register at bettshow.com. Follow @HalcyonSchool on Twitter to keep apprised of #BETT2017 news and updates!

Hyde Park at Lunch for Grades 6, 7 and 8
At Halcyon we recognise that promoting an active lifestyle is important to being fit and healthy. Longitudinal research in the British Journal of Sports Medicine shows that greater participation in physical activity has a positive impact on academic achievement (Booth et al., 2013). 

Starting next week, Halcyon will introduce Hyde Park lunch sessions for Grades 6, 7 and 8. Once a week, each grade will have a full hour for lunch - beginning 15 minutes earlier than normal at 1200 - to provide them with the opportunity to go to Hyde Park with Fit for Sport. Once at Hyde Park students will be able to participate in activities such as ultimate frisbee and football. The Hyde Park lunch time activity for each grade will take place on a different day each week and will be weather dependent. If it is raining students will remain on campus. The day of each grade’s Hyde Park visit will be communicated to students in the Morning Notes. 

On those days, students should bring a change of trainers just in case they get muddy in the park. 

We will monitor the success of the programme over the coming weeks with a view to extending this opportunity to Grades 9 and 10. Ultimately, we wish to offer all students, Grades 6 - 10, greater access to outdoor activities.

At lunchtime,we will also be developing a more diverse range activities within the school. In the Stern Hall, Fit for Sport will run a weekly rotation of activities such as dodgeball and hockey for students to participate in. In the Second Floor Space, students can relax with friends, read a book or get support from teachers with their homework.

The overall aim of the increased lunch time provision is to provide more opportunities for students to have an active and social lunch time at Halcyon. This in turn will support a positive impact on our children’s health and learning.
