Director's Message, Half Term Break
 
Dear Students, Staff and Parents,

I hope that everyone has enjoyed the first half-term of the school year. I say ‘enjoyed’ very deliberately because we know we all learn better when we are enthused and engaged; I know there has been fabulous learning taking place - from our week-long residential trips, through field trips around London during our Community Days, to great IB learning across our classes, and teacher professional learning days. We have all been engaged, given our best and made progress as individuals and as a learning community. We aim for learning to give us a sense of success and help us feel fulfilled.

This year we have established Exploration classes and started a one-to-one mentoring programme for all students. We have also sought to expand the time available for personal and service learning, through our vertical mentor groups. These programmes aim to provide students with an opportunity to shape (some of) their learning. It also allows more time to reflect on their experiences and be supported in making positive changes. By having a greater sense of ownership of their learning, students should find learning more enjoyable and satisfying.

I trust our parents have enjoyed their learning too, coming to our workshops and orientation evenings. I hope that we have communicated our enjoyment of being a part of the Halcyon experience, and presented our school as a place that prizes inquiry, discovery and innovation. I know that our parents are always there to support both the school and their own learning - thank you! 

Finally, and not by any means last, our staff have supported the changes we have made this year and been wonderful ambassadors for innovation. They, too, have taken on new ideas and different ways of learning, and I believe this is one of the many reasons why Halcyon is such a great place to be; as staff members we all enjoy learning together and then sharing that with the students and parents.

Wherever you may be over the coming vacation - enjoy!

Best wishes,
This Week’s Community Day
 
Grade 6
Grade 6 students visited the British Museum and looked at ancient civilisations, focussing on works of art and artefacts. Students gained an understanding and appreciation of the cultures the artwork represented.

They studied the types of symbols used and how these express the identity of their culture. Students discussed how the British Museum housed a wide range of artworks representing different cultures. The group mostly focussed on the Aztec, North American, Inuit, Sri-Lankan and African works of art. Students had the opportunity to make many drawings for inspiration. Their  research  unearthed how the masks were used and how they were made.
Grade 7
While at the Olympic Park, Grade 7 students completed this mathematics worksheet which helped them appreciate the legacy of the Olympic site. For instance, one of the facts they discovered was that East Village of the site was the athletes village during the Olympic and Paralympic Games. Close to 16,000 athletes and team officials resided there during the Olympic Games, and 7,200 during the Paralympic Games.
Grade 8
Grade 8 students visited the Thames Explorer Trust in Chiswick. They collected data on the physical processes and ecology of the River Thames, and learnt about the role of the river in London’s history and the ways London protects itself from flooding.
Grade 9
Grade 9 visited the Royal Courts of Justice, where they took part in a mock murder trial. Luckily, the accused was freed, due to insufficient evidence. After lunch, students had a wonderful tour of the Inns of Court, called "Discovering Legal London". They learned all about the legal system of England and Wales.
Grade 10
Grade 10 students visited the Clink Prison Museum and the Museum of London. They investigated the question "What does the history of crime and punishment tell us about changing views of individual liberty and how society should deal with crime?". This visit supported part of their PSHE unit on liberty, equality and democracy. The day started with a look at modern justice as students participated in a simulated jury trial in the classroom. Then students visited the Clink Prison museum and the Museum of London gathering information for their own documentaries about crime and punishment.
DP Students (Grades 11 & 12)
The field trip to the Tate Modern and Tate Britain (aka "Tate to Tate") was planned for IB Diploma students with a focus on Visual Arts and Theory of Knowledge (ToK). The art works displayed evoked some interesting connections to epistemological categories of understanding and enabled students to engage in a detailed comparative analysis, where students looked for cultural, contextual, formal, and conceptual links. Students also had the opportunity to see iconic British artworks such as the paintings of John Constable and Joseph Turner.

2016-2017 Junior Intern Programme, USC Shoah Foundation Institute for Visual History
Congratulations to Evy, Grade 10, who has been chosen to participate in the 2016-2017 Junior Intern Programme for the USC (University of Southern California) Shoah Foundation Institute for Visual History.

Evy was one of only three students selected from outside of southern California and so will meet virtually with the other participants. 

"Through this programme, participants will have the opportunity to work in a university environment while developing an understanding of different types of memory, deepen proficiency in digital literacy and improve communication skills. There will also be community service opportunities and field trips. In addition, participants will contribute to the Institute's development of IWitness educational resources that will be available to teachers and students worldwide. Through these activities, interns will think about what it means to be a responsible participant in civil society as they develop the knowledge, skills and confidence to believe they have a role in creating positive change in their own lives and communities."

What a fantastic opportunity and we wish Evy all the best in her endeavour.

Engineering Education Scheme - Parents Needed
Grade 11 Physics have been accepted into the EES (Engineering Education Scheme). This is a unique and exciting opportunity for Halcyon’s keen engineers. The EES organisation will find a company to work with 4-6 students. Students will meet company engineer representatives  at their headquarters or a nearby university for an introductory workshop. The students will then be assigned an engineering project by the company to complete over the next six months. Once completed, students will present to senior engineers and, if the project passes evaluation it will be incorporated into company practice.

This is an amazing and unique opportunity, but the number of schools applying outnumber the quantity of interested companies. The organisation has advised that students are permitted to source their own company from the school community. 

Grade 11 physics students are looking for a company that either would welcome their participation in an engineering project and  is interested in helping educate the next generation of engineers. 

Please email Lori Fritz if you know a company that you think would be suited to this project.

HPC: Exhibition The Vulgar, Fashion Redefined
Location: Barbican Art Gallery
Date: Wednesday 23 November
Time: 1000-1200
Meeting Point: the Barbican (more details to follow upon confirmation)
Cost: £36 (including exhibition entrance ticket)
This new exhibition conceived by exhibition-maker Judith Clark and psychoanalyst Adam Phillip will look at the notion of vulgarity in fashion inviting the viewer to think again about exactly what makes something vulgar and why it is such a sensitive and contested term. Drawn from major public and private collections worldwide, the show will include contributions from leading modern and contemporary designers such as Walter van Beirendonck, Manolo Blahnik, Christian Dior, Marc Jacobs for Louis Vuitton, Stephen Jones, Christian Lacroix, Karl Lagerfeld for Chloé and Chanel, Alexander McQueen for Givenchy, Prada and Vivienne Westwood.
