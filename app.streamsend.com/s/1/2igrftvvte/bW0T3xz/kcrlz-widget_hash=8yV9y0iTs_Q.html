3. 
Director search update
Following the successful first round of interviews for Halcyon’s new Director, the Board is delighted to inform you that the shortlisted candidates will be visiting Halcyon on 18, 19 and 23 March.

During the first round of interviews, the Board met with the candidates and interviewed them on their educational philosophy, management style, understanding of Halcyon and experience of implementing the IBMYP and IBDP in a young, small school, as well as many other topics. The candidates who made it through this round of interviews all impressed the Board with their experience, thoughtfulness, intelligence and awareness of, and appreciation for, Halcyon.

The school visits will now offer the candidates the opportunity to gain a better understanding of Halcyon by meeting with and asking their own questions of small groups of representatives of various stakeholder groups. These groups include students, parents, teaching and administrative staff and the Board. Each candidate will also have a chance to visit and observe a couple of classes.

We will conclude the recruitment process by Monday 23 March and will make an announcement shortly thereafter.
4. The IB selects Halcyon staff for important roles
Halcyon is delighted to announce that more of our teachers have been chosen for the IB to work in important roles. These appointments show how experienced and capable our teachers are as they have been chosen from applicants all over the world.
  • Elisabet Aleu will receive training next week to become an IBMYP Consultant and School Visitor.
  • Lenny Dutton is now part of the team developing the MYP eportfolio for Design Technology.
  • Noelia Zago will be trained as a Workshop Leader for the IB Diploma Programme.
At Halcyon, we support and encourage our teachers to experience working for the IB as we believe that teachers are essential for an excellent education and their professional development is key.

Having teachers who collaborate with the IB also enables Halcyon to keep up-to-date with the IB Programmes and helps us in our goal to become a leading school in the IB world.
5. Grade 10 Personal Project & Grade 8 Community Project Celebration of Learning
Date: Monday 20 April
Time: 1530-1720
Location: Stern Hall

Grade 10 students will be showing the Personal Projects that they have been working on since the beginning of the year. Here students work independently on a project of their choosing, which links to a Global Context. Students have been doing a broad range of projects from creating presentations to help teenagers better understand issues with mental health, through to creating videos about Halcyon.

The Grade 8 students will be presenting the projects they have completed as part of the IBMYP Community Project. The IBMYP Community Project is a group project involving service to the community.

Students and parents in all Grades are encouraged to attend, both to celebrate the achievements of students presently in Grades 8 and 10 and to get inspiration for their own projects in coming years.

If you have any questions, please get in touch with Lenny Dutton or Julia Watson.

    8. John Wilson, experienced educator, visits Grade 9 & 7
    John Wilson, an experienced American educator and Vietnam War veteran, came to speak to Grade 9 on Monday about the Cold War and his own experiences in the Vietnam War. One of our Grade 9's described him as "awesome" and John Wilson was equally impressed with our Grade 9s, describing them as "very strong history students". He was particularly impressed with their detailed contextual knowledge. Last week, John spoke to Grade 7 students on the theme of the American Civil War and the whole class was engrossed in his detailed expertise on the topic.

    viii. The Radio Play - Look What A Wonder
    Thursday 23 April 1545-1645
    Halcyon Room of Prayer

    This event is brought to us by Drama teacher, Julia Watson. This is a rare opportunity to listen to a radio play that may one day become a West End musical. Look What A Wonder is a one-hour adaptation of a gospel folk opera, based on the life of the ex-slave and free Black, Denmark Vesey. Look What A Wonder has already been immensely successful as a narrated concert performance. For reviews, along with pictures, synopsis and music clips, please go to www.lookwhatawonder.com.
