6. Social Media/Apps Age Requirements
Halcyon London International School respects and recognises the age requirement to access social media sites and apps. Examples of this are the 13+ rule for Facebook and Instagram and gaming apps have a 17+ rating meaning that, like a movie's guidance rating, the content is not suitable for those below that age. Younger children having access to these accounts and apps is against the sites' user agreements and the student's account may be shut down. We therefore ask that students do not have access to them on their devices at school. Visit http://www.commonsensemedia.org/reviews to find out more about ratings and find age-appropriate movies, books, apps, TV shows, video games, websites, and music that you and your kids will love.
7. Halcyon Takes on the Poetry Games
“The Poetry Games” is a new creative writing competition from Young Writers Publishers for students aged 11-18.

Each year, students compete against thousands of entries to become one of 12 finalists chosen to have their piece published. Only one student will be crowned “Poetry Games Champion”, winning an iPad Air and £1000 for their school.

This year, Halcyon is submitting four entries:

Siri Cervin - “The Stars In The Sky”
Kate Crannell - “My Traveling Life”

Noah Melman - “Where I’m From”

These poems have been published on the Halcyon Blog for you to enjoy (see links above).

We wish our students the best of luck in the competition and applaud their creative, bold spirit!

12. Upcoming Trips
GRADES 6-9 (Chinese classes): The British Museum and Chinatown
Thursday 27 February
Depart: 1245 (by Tube) | Return: 1600 (students will be dismissed from school)

The purpose of this trip is for the students to discover the culture and history of China by looking at the Museum’s China collection and experience the Chinese New Year festive atmosphere in Chinatown.

While at the British Museum, the students will have a one hour guided tour led by James Trapp, the China Education Manager at the Museum. They will study the writings on different objects and identify the differences between traditional and simplified Chinese characters. They will then go out to Chinatown for a fun character trail activity.

Students will need to bring:
  • Oyster Card
  • iPad
  • A warm, waterproof coat
  • Sensible walking shoes.
Trip leader: Mei-Ling Lin
Accompanying parent: Anja Beck.
