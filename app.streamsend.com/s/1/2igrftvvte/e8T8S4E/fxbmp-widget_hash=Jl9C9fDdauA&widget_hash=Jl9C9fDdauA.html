2. C&S and IB Learner Profile Final Presentations
The main goal for Halcyon’s C&S is for our students to develop a sense of responsible citizenship, reflecting on the key questions of:
  • How do we live in relation to each other?
  • How I can contribute to the community?
  • How I can help others?
These key questions are reflected in the four strands of the Halcyon C&S programme: Halcyon Community; Halcyon in the Wider Community; Helping Others; and Helping the Environment.

To close this C&S programme this year, Halcyon’s students have to prepare a presentation to their advisory and a committee of Halcyon teachers. This will take place on the morning of Thursday 26 June. In the presentation they will need to link the IB Learner Profile with their individual C&S pathway. To do this they may use a digital tool of their choice. As with the Learner Profile, the C&S programme aims to extend our students' learning beyond intellectual achievement, in order to help each other to develop a personal value system through which to guide every student life as thoughtful members of local communities and the larger world. They will record their presentations and upload them to vimeo or youtube as a private video and place the link in the report reflections on ManageBac.

    9. Chinese Cinema Study Day at the BFI
    Our Grade 8 & 9 Mandarin class had a fruitful trip to the British Film Institute (BFI) last week, where they joined over 50 students from other UK schools for a full day of Chinese film and culture study.    

    In the morning session, students watched a selection of movie clips set in different periods, each reflecting the political and social change of the time in China. They were given a study pack with Chinese transcripts and discussion questions. The afternoon session concluded with the appreciation of an internationally-acclaimed movie, Not One Less, which highlights the issue of poverty and education.

    It was a valuable experience for the students to spend a day listening to the rhythm of Mandarin spoken continuously and in different accents. The BFI is currently hosting a Chinese movie festival, entitled Electric Shadow (the Chinese term for movies or “dian ying”). Join in if you would like to watch some Chinese movies and participate in this unprecedented event!
