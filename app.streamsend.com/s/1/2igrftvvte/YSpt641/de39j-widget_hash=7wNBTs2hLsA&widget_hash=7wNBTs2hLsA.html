3. Gong Xi Fa Cai! Happy Chinese New Year!
Chinese New Year, or the Lunar New Year, is also known as the Spring Festival which celebrates the start of new life, the season for sowing and family reunion. The Chinese New Year festivities last for 15 days from Friday, 31 January but the festive spirit lasts for the whole month!

4. Halcyon's IBDP Consultant, Bill Roberts
On 23 and 24 January, Halcyon was visited by Bill Roberts, our appointed IB Diploma Programme consultant. This is the first step in the process for IBDP verification and we are well ahead of schedule. Bill began with a tour around our facilities and held meetings with the Grade 9 students, all teachers and members of the Board. Bill was thoroughly impressed with how well our Grade 9 students already understand the requirements of the Diploma Programme! During his final briefing, Bill mentioned that it was a great pleasure for him to have profound IB conversations with the teachers at such an early stage of the authorisation process thanks to our experienced IB educators. He also highlighted Halcyon as a unique type of school. Bill’s visit was a very important milestone in our journey towards becoming the first not-for-profit IB world school in London.

9. Grade 6 Gothic Mapping Field Trip
This week, the Grade 6 class headed out on a journey to some of London’s top sites. Being in the midst of a unit on Gothic Fiction in English, and a Mapping Unit in Humanities, the students drew on their knowledge and skills to create a map of “Gothic London”. The maps were created using iPad applications and an e-book on Gothic elements; all feature colour-marked routes and small captions at each site. Aside from gaining greater knowledge of the Gothic Fiction genre, and navigating its many sites around London, the students have created a worthwhile project that can now be used by others wishing to explore our wonderful city. 11. Upcoming Trips
GRADE 9: “Wicked” Theatre Trip (Matinee Show)
The Apollo Victoria Theatre
Wednesday, 5 February
Depart: 1300 | Return: 1800

The Grade 9 class has, for the past five weeks, been engaged in an English unit focusing on the Fantasy Fiction genre. This has been an extensive study that has taken them from 4th Century Fantasy Fiction pieces to more contemporary, populist versions. An important aspect of this unit has been a discussion of the ethical considerations of the respective texts, with a view to composing a final literary essay.

One work studied during this unit was L. Frank Baum’s famous text, The Wonderful Wizard of Oz which has been marvellously adapted, and re-envisioned, for the stage in the form of the West End Show, “Wicked,” based on the novel by the same name written by Gregory Maquire.

The educational value of the “Wicked” theatre production extends beyond the reinforcement of literary concepts studied in the text. The plot draws on historical events and ideologies, as well as provokes thought related to the nature of friendship, personal values, and the consequence of choices. Additionally, it provides students with an opportunity to experience varied forms of artistic expression, ranging from visual choices in costumes to design technology used in constructing the elaborate stage sets.

Trip leader: Michelle Jones.
