2. Halcyon Field Studies Day
On Thursday, 26 September all Halcyon students will be participating in field studies related to their units of work on London Diversity. As the school building is closed on Thursday, students should meet at Marble Arch Monument (directly opposite Marble Arch tube station) at 0900.

Grades 6 and 7 will visit the London Wetlands Centre in Barnes, South West London, to explore and collect data in this special London ecosystem. The group will depart by bus from Marble Arch and will be dismissed from Marble Arch at 1535 after the trip.

Students need to bring: 
  • Oyster card
  • Packed lunch
  • Raincoat. 
Trip Leader: Lori Fritz

Grades 8 and 9 will visit one of the most interesting and vibrant areas of London while exploring the diversity of the city apparent in its history, literature and art. The group will depart by tube from Marble Arch and be dismissed from Marble Arch at 1535 after the trip.

They will visit an exhibition at Tate Modern, followed by a tour and lunch of the Brick Lane area in East London. The day will culminate with a visit to the Museum of London.

Students need to bring:
  • Oyster card
  • £5 for lunch in Brick Lane
  • Raincoat.
Trip Leader: Stephanie Cooke
