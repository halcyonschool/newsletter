Director’s Message
Dear Parents, Students and Colleagues,

As we reach the end of our first term, I would like to thank everyone in our community for their support for learning at Halcyon. Our wonderful students have been supported by dedicated and tireless staff, and we are all very grateful for the help and generosity of our parent community.

There have been many highlights this term - our bright, thoughtful, committed MUN and GIN groups; the fantastic student-led Halloween catwalk; the many, many field trips around London, and our successful residential trips way back in September; the excellent support we always receive from the HPC; the student showcase, and the International Family Showcase; and our first IB Diploma class, taking-on new challenges and leading Halcyon - our first graduating class. There’s much more to celebrate, and I hope all of our students will have time over the vacation to reflect on their achievements, and consider how they can continue to challenge themselves next term.

When we return after the vacation, we will continue our strategic planning process. This will culminate in a new three-year strategic plan for the school, designed to capture the spirit of our school mission and our commitment to innovation, while helping us provide solid institutional foundations to manage our growing school. Thank you to everyone who participated in the focus groups and completed the climate survey; we very much value your feedback.

Finally, I would like to thank our teaching team for their creativity, passion and expertise; it is a pleasure to lead such a thoughtful and committed team.

I’d like to wish you all a restful and enjoyable vacation, and thank you for your support for our learning community.

Best wishes,

REMINDER: ACT Test for Grades 10 and 11
Please note, if your child(ren) would like to take the ACT test please confirm to Barry Mansfield by Friday 15 December 2015.

The ACT is used for entrance to U.S. universities (please be advised to check your child(ren)’s chosen colleges for admissions requirements), and offers an alternative to the SAT. It is better suited to students following IB programmes, but is still an acquired skill and we would recommend some additional coaching to prepare for the test.

Halcyon is an ACT test centre, and we are scheduled to administer a test on Saturday 11 June 2016. Please note that all test centres are open to all applicants - we cannot reserve places for our students - so if your student is interested in taking this test, you will need to register with ACT and then sign-up for a test centre. Please note that Halcyon is full, so you will need to look for a suitable alternate centre in London.

To prepare for the tests, we have teamed up with UES London who will offer our school discounted tuition if we have a minimum group of five students. The starting point is a proposed fee of £700 for 20 hours tuition. UES are situated close to the school - a two minute walk in Crawford Street. The proposal would be to have the courses directly after school on Mondays, broadly speaking from February through April - a commitment of ten weekly 2-hour sessions finishing by 1800.

If you are interested in the ACT course, please contact Barry. If we have more than five students, the school will facilitate the programme with UES.

New Staff Announcement!
Kelly Foster will be stepping in as Drama Teacher while Julia Watson is on maternity leave. Kelly has studied theatre and performance as an undergraduate student and has a masters degree in theatre and philosophy from Queen Mary University of London where she specialised in late medieval and early modern performance practice. Part of this study involved exploring the effects of the second schism on narrative forms during the period.

Kelly trained as an actor at RADA and LAMDA and spent 10 years working in London as an actor before moving to Paris to teach theatre and performance to international students.

During her time as an actor Kelly attended the Institute of Education, University of London to complete a PGCE in further education where she specialised in drama and performance.

Kelly’s main focus is directing for performance, actor training and the theatre of the postdramatic which currently forms part of her research for a future practice based PhD which she hopes to complete while living and working in London.

Kelly is committed to bringing a rigorous actor training approach to her work in the drama classroom in order to allow students to develop the tools they need in order to produce challenging, original and creative performance work.

Kelly is looking forward to the opportunity of collaborating with the students and teachers of Halcyon. We look forward to welcoming Kelly to the school!

How to be a Supportive Parent of an Adolescent?
A new Halcyon Discussion Group is forming to share our collective knowledge of how to be effective parents for our London teenagers. Discussions will pool experiences, ration-out advice and share stories of raising adolescents. The group will be facilitated by a qualified Life Counsellor and meet once a month with January’s first theme being “Open Communication”. Please sign-up for this Parent Discussion Group – couples or singles welcome. Group size will determine the first location and date. For more information please contact Gabi.Vollenweider@gmail.com.

Introducing Kensington Chelsea Women’s Club (KCWC)
The HPC would like to introduce organisations in London that may be useful to our parent community. The KCWC is an organisation of international and British women who want to enjoy everything London has to offer. They have an activity based club where members can attend events organised by fellow members who follow their passion and use their skills to organise over 30 activities including: history, culture, art, design, sports, crafts and hobbies, special events, languages, book and lecture groups, leisure pastimes, travel and tours in the UK and Europe as well as volunteering. Daytime and evening events are available. If you are interested in learning more about the KCWC or becoming a member, please see here. This is a great opportunity to meet people if you are new to London, or just to get involved in activities you enjoy!


The HPC is an all-volunteer group that works to support community life at Halcyon for all students, parents and staff. We welcome any and all support.
