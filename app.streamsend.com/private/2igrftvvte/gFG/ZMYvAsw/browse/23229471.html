3. Trial of ManageBac for recording homework tasks in Grade 6
At Halcyon, all major "summative" assessment tasks used to determine report grades are recorded in ManageBac. Information about these tasks are in orange on the ManageBac calendar as shown in the screenshot above. Major "formative" tasks that students are doing for practice but do not contribute to report grades are also shown - in green - on the calendar. Usually, these tasks are completed in school over longer periods and so do not necessarily give parents information about homework on a particular night.

Up until now, homework tasks have been recorded individually by students in their homework diary rather than in ManageBac. Following feedback from Grade 6 parents, we are trialling a system of recording all homework tasks in ManageBac. They appear in blue in the calendar on the day that they are due. Students will continue to record their homework in their individual diaries but we are investigating whether home-school communication will be improved by making this extra information available in ManageBac for Grade 6 parents. To help us with our investigation, we would appreciate your response to this Grade 6 Parent survey.   

Of course we are interested in feedback about homework and other academic issues from parents in all Grades and the student council has formed a teaching and learning committee who will be leading a further study involving the wider school community in the near future.
4. The benefits of being a IBDP student
by Noelia Zago, IBDP Co-ordinator

Acceptance rates: IBDP students have a higher rate of university and college acceptance.

Interdisciplinary learning: The IBDP develops candidates as internationally-minded individuals with a commitment to lifelong learning.

Self-confidence: IBDP students are confident in their abilities as learners, do not shy away from taking risks where necessary and learn to apply this in their everyday lives.

Ingenuity: IBDP students are creative and imaginative in dealing with everyday challenges and issues.

Understanding: The IBDP develops students’ empathetic skills and encourages them to consider multiple perspectives.

Well-informed: IBDP students explore concepts and issues that have both local and global significance. This helps them to acquire in-depth knowledge and an understanding of various disciplines.

These are only some of the advantages!

9. Disobedient Objects
Last Wednesday Grade 10 visited the Disobedient Objects exhibition at the V&A. Students are currently studying social justice campaigns in Individuals & Societies and Design. The main focus is on the Civil Rights Movement in America, drawing comparisons between the movement in the mid-20th Century and the issues today involving police brutality against African Americans. The class also studied the troubling case of Stephen Lawrence, a young Afro-Carribean man who was killed in 1993 in London. The exhibition was a great way to learn about the range of civil disobedience campaigns and to take inspiration for the creation of their own disobedient objects in Design.

Finding clarity in a time of transition
Date: 25 February
Time: 1030 - 1230

The familiar highs and lows in life can be exacerbated when going through periods of change, for example moving to a different country. This seminar provides an opportunity for you to take stock of your life at the moment, making an honest assessment of what is going well and what needs to improve. The next step is to get clear about what you want. You should leave this seminar with a clear idea of what is getting in the way of you feeling happy and fulfilled at the moment and what you want from your life. Sometimes you find out it's not what you think it is!
