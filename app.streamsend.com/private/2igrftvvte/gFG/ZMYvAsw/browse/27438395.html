        1. Director's Note
        Dear Parents and Students,

        I wanted to check-in with you at the end of our first full week in school, and ensure that everyone has found the back-to-school process successful. This can mean slightly different things for parents and students, but I do hope there is a shared sense that Halcyon continues to meet its mission of appreciating every individual’s needs. The first few weeks of school inevitably include sign-up for courses and activities, discussions about subject options in Grades 10-12 and language choices across the school. Some of these choices - especially Mother-Tongue programmes - can take longer to be realised. Most importantly, I hope that everyone feels positive about their classes. 

        For staff and myself, it’s always a privilege to be sharing our day with such wonderful, engaged students and we are all very grateful for the support we have from our Halcyon parents. We have very much enjoyed the start of year and look forward to the many learning opportunities that lie ahead.

        Next week, we have our residential trips and I am sure everyone will enjoy them. Please do review the final trip reminders provided below. Thank you!

        Finally, our extracurricular activities programme started this week and when we return, the Swim Club will begin on Friday 23 September. I am also hoping to confirm our Mindfulness class, but am looking for a couple of additional students to make this activity viable. If you would like to join this class, please email me directly.

        Have a great weekend, and we look forward to hearing all your stories from the residential trips!

        Best wishes,

              4. University and Careers Guidance
              University Counselling Activities
              Mark Bowen, University Guidance Counsellor, is organising visits and presentations from a variety of universities so that students can learn more about the process and offerings. This will usually involve visits from university representatives giving presentations on their institutions and application processes. This will usually involve more American universities as British universities typically rely upon Open Days and Taster Courses rather than individual secondary school visits. The visits are intended for Grade 11 and Grade 12 students and students are encouraged to attend as many presentations as possible to broaden their awareness. Additionally, Mark is happy to support students interested in universities from other countries as well.

              The first four sessions will all occur at Halcyon as follows:

              Monday 19 September
              At 1300 Manhattanville College will present

              Thursday 22 September
              At 1300 Drexel University will present

              Friday 23 September
              At 0800 Notre Dame University will present in the Hub
              At 1300 Fordham University and the University of Georgia will present in the Hub

              These are great opportunities for students to learn in depth about these institutions and gain familiarity with the process.

              Fulbright Commission USA College Day
              The Fulbright Commission USA College Day is being held in London on Friday / Saturday 23-24 September. Students and parents should make reservations for various time slots in advance at their website. This is recommended for all Grade 11 and 12 families who might make an application to a US university, as frequently these representatives will be reading Halcyon students’ applications. If you and / or your children are interested in attending, do be sure to book your tickets as soon as you can as the event is very popular. Plan to be there for a few hours to make sure you see and speak to many universities. Information about the venue and location are available on the website.

              More university visits and taster day information are in the planning stages so stay tuned!
              5. Development Activities
              After a kick off in May at the last Halcyon Big Picture evening, the Development Team have been busy over the summer with planning for this academic year. The Development Committee - a combination of the Development Team (Rita Halbright and Eilidh MacIver) and parents will continue working together to support exciting projects in the Halcyon community such as the Second Floor renovation and our Bursary Programme.

              Development Committee Meeting
              Date: Friday 30 September
              Time: From 0800 - 0900
              Location: Halcyon London International School, 33 Seymour Place
              The first Development Committee meeting of this school year will be the perfect opportunity for last year’s members to reconvene and new parents to join in the activity. We will be reviewing the Development Timeline for this year. Your input is welcome! We also want to move ahead in fine-tuning ideas and forming subcommittees to support activities and events. Please RSVP here if you would like to attend the meeting to pick up from last year or learn more about what is involved. If you have any questions in the meantime, please feel free to contact Rita Halbright.

              Development Updates
              Last term, we had the launch of our renovation campaign, where we are working toward raising at least £250,000 to support the school in renovating the 2nd floor space into state of the art, innovative learning spaces to accommodate several groups of learners in flexible ways.
              We are delighted to announce that after our soft launch in the spring we have already raised over £50,000 toward this goal thanks to the generosity of current and past Halcyon families.

              Thanks to Gift Aid calculations being done as we speak this figure will increase. More information about this exciting project and our development goals will be discussed at the Halcyon Big Picture on 29 September (see above).

              We look forward to working together with the whole community to reach our fundraising goal so we can carry out the renovations over next summer.

              Additionally, Halcyon’s Bursary Fund has grown thanks to the generosity of a Halcyon family. We have our first bursary student this year and are working hard to continue to broaden access to a Halcyon education for more eligible students.

              If you have any questions about these projects, please contact Rita Halbright.
