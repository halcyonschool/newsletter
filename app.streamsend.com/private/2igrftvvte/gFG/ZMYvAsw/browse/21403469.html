MaKey MaKey
Last week, Grade 6 was visited by Ed Charlwood, a Design Technology Consultant, in their science class. To enrich their unit about electricity and conductivity, Mr Charlwood introduced the students to MaKey MaKey kits. Once connected to a computer, the MaKey MaKey becomes a touch pad; the computer thinks that the MaKey MaKey is a keyboard. The kids found out that using the MaKey MaKey allowed them to control a computer with things like copper, water and even bananas! They were amazed by what the MaKey MaKey allowed them to do. Through trial and error, they discovered the difference between conductors and insulators. Mr Charlwood put them to the challenge of working in groups to make something with the MaKey MaKey that controlled the computer in any way they wanted with any materials they choose. They all took initiative, and became very inventive; exploring something that was brand new to them. End products ranged from fruit controlled pac-man, to creating music by sticking hands in coloured water. Through doing, they ultimately taught themselves about material conductivity. The class was a great example of how powerful experiential learning is, and how Halcyon students are successful independent learners.

Grade 8 student enters the Holocaust Youth Commission Competition
The Prime Minister’s Holocaust Commission is to convene a Youth Forum in Downing Street in the autumn this year. They are seeking six youth representatives to play a role in this commission.

One winner will be chosen by Holocaust survivor Elie Wiesel to join the Commission as a youth member to feed in the views of the forum and help decide the final recommendations to be submitted to the Prime Minister.

In order to place himself in the running for this position, Halcyon's very own, Ethan Reich was required to write a 1500 word essay based on the topic, "Why is it so important that we remember the Holocaust and how can we make sure future generations never forget?"

Working tirelessly during his English lessons, as well as after school, Ethan has now completed his final submission. His piece draws on his own family's experiences, as well as the view that "Education is key - The Holocaust is not just a Jewish memory; it is inherently a human memory and everyone should take a moment to reflect on yom hashoah".

Ethan's piece is highly-persuasive piece and tackles the current issues facing British youth.

As a community, we wish you the best of luck, Ethan!

Upcoming Trip: Grades 7&8 Kew Gardens
Date: Monday 2 June
Depart: 0900
Return: 1535

Halcyon science students are busy growing their own plants and experimenting with growing conditions and we expect to be inspired and informed by our visit to the impressive and historical botanical gardens at Kew. As we enjoy the beauty and variety of these amazing gardens, Grade 8 students will consider in particular how Kew uses greenhouses to control the heat and light radiation to grow plants from around the world and Grade 7 students will consider the variety of plants and their life cycles as part of their study of earth systems.
