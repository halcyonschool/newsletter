    7. Halcyon’s Student Council introduces: International Student ID Cards
    What are student IDs?
    ISIC (International School ID Cards) is a company which distributes IDs for international students. The cards are distributed in over 130 countries for students aged 12 and up. The cards contains a photo, first name, last name and date of birth as well as the school’s name.

    What are the benefits?
    There are many benefits of the ID card. For example, discounts in local restaurants, museums, cinemas, clothes and office supplies shops, bookstores, public transport, and more. They are also good for general ID too.

    If you would like your student to have a card, you can apply here.

    You will also need a “proof of enrollment letter” from the school. To request this, please fill out this form. Once completed, you will be issued with a letter via email within 3-5 days.
    8. Grade 8 & 9 Chinese Cinema Study Day at BFI
    Grade 8 and 9 Mandarin class had an enjoyable trip to the British Film Institute, where they were introduced to a brief history of cinematography development in Mainland China through watching a selection of movie clips set in different periods, each reflecting the political and social change of the time. It was a valuable experience for the students to have the opportunity to listen to the rhythm of Mandarin spoken in different regional accents and to learn about how traditional Chinese values were expressed in each film.
      9. Child-to-Child Assembly
      Allegra and Lucy organised for Bella Tristram from Child-to-Child to present at this week’s assembly about the charity’s inspiring work. They have a number of projects all over the world which support their premise that “when children work together they can change their world”. Child-to-Child go into areas where young people need a variety of support - one example being forming small teams of students to support a blind student so s/he can have assistance at home to get ready for school, get to school and support them in the classroom.

      Having young people see how they can positively affect other people’s lives helps develop their leadership skills, improve their self-confidence and develop other useful skills while they help others do the same.

          12. Mandarin made fun
          Learning Mandarin can be difficult, but our enthusiastic Mandarin students are making it an interesting and stimulating learning experience.

          Recently in our Mandarin classroom, we have used Lego bricks to build Chinese characters. Thanks to the feature of Chinese writing, the components of each character more or less fit in a square block. This feature fits in perfectly with the design of Lego and through building the characters, students were able to reflect on how each component of the writing is positioned and structured.

          We have also used traditional Chinese puppets to create some interesting role-play. Grade 8 students worked in pairs to write up scripts of conversation between doctor and patient as the revision activity of the current unit. They’ve produced some really lovely work! See our blog for more photos and an example of the puppet play video.
