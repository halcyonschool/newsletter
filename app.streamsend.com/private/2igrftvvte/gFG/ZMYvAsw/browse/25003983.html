1. Mark Your Calendar: Community Day Monday 5 October
Community days are an opportunity for Halcyon students to explore resources outside of the classroom as part of their learning. Grade level trips and visits also occur at other times in the term but on community days there is a wonderful synergy when all students are engaged in exploring London and sharing their stories on return to school. 

Students should wear comfortable walking shoes and bring along a waterproof, an Oyster card, a charged iPad and money to buy lunch and a snack or a packed lunch and snack. All students should arrive at school at the normal time and all trips will return to school before the end of the normal school day at 1535 and after school activities will run as usual.

Grade 6 will visit the London Zoo to study animal adaptations and participate in a hands-on workshop as part of their science unit on urban ecosystems. 

Grade 7 will be visiting a school for the deaf and working with students there on a project about 'seeing sound' as part of their science unit on sound and hearing.

As part of their study of Victorian London, Grade 8 students will explore the life and times of Charles Dickens at the Charles Dickens Museum and see what inspired the writers of the time.

Grades 9 and 10 science students are going to the Wellcome Collection Museum to look at the work of inspirational scientist Henry Wellcome and consider how scientific and technical innovation has helped develop practices in medicine and space research. 

Grades 10 and 11 art students will visit the Tate Britain to research inspirational sculptors to understand their use of balance, proportion, weight and interpretation of identities within the figure. Grade 11 students will also study art works linked to assessment tasks in English literature and mathematics.

A snapshot from Monday 28 September community day.

10. Mark Your Calendar: Study in Canada Fair
Date: Saturday 10 October
Time: 0930 - 1500
Location: Canada House, Trafalgar Square, London, SW1Y 5BJ

Students and their families are invited to Canada House to meet with Canadian universities and colleges, and learn about studying opportunities in Canada. The seminar programme will include presentations on studying in Canada, the benefits and differences of a Canadian education, and employment opportunities. Canadian alumni will be present as well to share their experiences. 

The event will be held in the newly renovated Canada House on Trafalgar Square, a beautifully restored heritage building that now houses an impressive collection of Canadian art and design. Lunch and reference materials will be provided for attendees. Free to attend.

Shoreditch Street Art
Tuesday 13 October 1000 - 1230
Meeting Place: Urban Street Experience, outside exit 3 of Old Street station
£30.00 plues £1.26 surcharge
Shoreditch is the epitome of London's creative community and has been home to street art from all over the world. This is a great tour to experience a part of London that is constantly brimming with creative talents and just a funky part of town. Even if you have been on a street art tour in the past, it's worth going again as the art is constantly changing. This tour is organised by From My City which is a trusted tour organiser and we have had great tour guides from them in the past. Please sign-up directly on this page for Halcyon so that we can keep track of who's coming. For any questions, please contact Stacey Ciauri.

Digital Photography and the Selfie
Tuesday 10 November 0945 - 1200
Art Workers Guild, 6 Queen Square, WC1N 3AT
£25.00 per person incl. coffee and pastries
The London Art Salon is offering a lecture on the invention and rise of self-portraiture from humble beginnings as hidden painted faces to the obsessive documenting and displaying of our own digital images. While the "selfie" is being billed as a massive contemporary phenomenon, photographers did not wait for the invention of digital smart phones or selfie sticks in order to turn the camera on themselves. The London Art Salon brings together expert speakers in a series of talks and discussions that cover London's current art exhibitions, events and art market. Registration is essential here. For any questions, please contact Anja Beck.

City of London Architecture Tour
Wednesday 18 November 1030 - 1230
Meeting Place: One New Change EC4M 9AF
£30.00 plus £1.26 surcharge
This is an amazing tour with the former head of planning for the City of London. On our walk around The City of London, we will look first at the immediate setting of St Paul's Cathedral and its transformation over recent decades. Our route will continue via Cheapside, the City's revitalised High Street, towards Bank and the Royal Exchange. The tour will continue through the medieval alleyways off Cornhill and take in Leadenhall Market and the Lloyds Building before visiting the cluster of new office towers which house London's insurance sector. Our walk will then turn north to visit Broadgate and Liverpool Station, finishing on the northeastern corner of the City adjacent to Spitalfields. This tour is organised by From My City. Please sign-up directly on this page for Halcyon so that we can keep track of who's coming. For any questions, please contact Stacey Ciauri.
