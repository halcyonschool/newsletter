    8.
    The Education Show: Lenny Dutton & Elani McDonald present
    Lenny Dutton and Elani McDonald give successful talks at this year’s The Education Show in Birmingham.

    Lenny presented “How might we begin to teach coding” - a session giving the non-coder the tools they need to teach coding in a fun and engaging way. The session showcased Lenny’s first coding unit at Halcyon where she taught Grade 6 to code and they designed lessons to teach others.

    Elani’s talk explored the different aspects of gaming in the classroom and the impact gaming has on engagement, differentiation, and how it can be used to foster a love of learning.
      9. Halcyon’s Mathematics Achievements
      We are very excited to once again be competing in the United Kingdom Mathematics Trust (UKMT) tournament which consists of different levels and competitions. Most participating schools have several hundred students per year group from which to choose, including streamed sets of which only the top mathematicians are allowed to compete. As a young, small school, Halcyon can be very proud of its achievements. Last year, with roughly 40 students, we had four medal winners. This year with roughly 75 students we had seven medal winners. Our younger competitors, Grade 7 and Grade 8 students, just competed in a region-wide competition with 40 schools and came in 30th. Considering that we only sent four students from our school, this was a good achievement and we plan to do better next year. We are also very proud of our two Olympiads (one this year and one last year) who competed with the top 500 students in their age group in the country.

        10. Grade 10 English
        Michelle Jones’ Grade 10 English class had visitors from Facing History and Ourselves, Roger Brooks, CEO/President; Anne-Marie Fitzgerald, Chief Officer of Regional Operations; and Michael McIntyre, Sr. Programme Coordinator, UK. 

        The visitors thoroughly enjoyed the class’ analysis and comparison of individual chapters from “The Great Gatsby” and “To Kill a Mockingbird”, looking at how the authors used different elements of class to develop context.           11. "Always Learning" Together
          Our Humanities teacher, Stephanie Cooke, and our Digital Learning Co-ordinator, Cindy Crannell, went to the TedxAmsterdamEd conference on Thursday to "meet the people who are planting the seeds of the education revolution". They heard from inspiring speakers who are paving the way to develop an education system that meets the needs of children of the 21st Century. Recurring themes included encouraging children to express and develop their personal creativity, calling for schools to catch up with the workplace in integrating new technology into the classroom, making the products of their labour meaningful and encouraging collaboration. Some of the talks seemed so obvious, such as Sandra Van Aalderen wondering why teachers are not trained in understanding how the developing brain works in order to maximise student learning.
            12. Digital Corner with Cindy Crannell
            While at TEDx AmsterdamEd event this week I had the privilege to listen to a Ted Talk by Yoram Mosenzon. The focus of his talk was on how we as humans communicate and the power it has to either create conflict or collaboration: "One that tends to create distance, conflict and misunderstanding and the other that creates connection and genuine compassion." In relation to our most recent parenting talks this Ted Talk supported many of the same ideas and strategies around communicating with our teens. Take some time to visit Yoram's website Connecting2lifeThe Art of Dialogue which has many workshops and courses.
            13. Birgit Maass visits Halcyon
            Birgit Maass, Linus and Jonah's mother, is a TV reporter for Deutsche Welle, the German international public broadcaster. She has been involved with the Alexander Litvinenko case and has had access to prolonged interviews with his widow, Marina Litvinenko. Birgit came to Halcyon to talk about her work.


            Recently Ms Birgit Maaß gave a talk on what is happening in the Russia crisis particularly looking at a Russian spy who went undercover in London named Alexander Litvinenko. Her talk was entered on the way that he fled and only a few years later was poisoned in an upscale hotel bar. The case has come that he was drinking green tea when some radioactive polonium was slipped into it. Only a few days later he was sick and lay dying for a few weeks.The suspects of the murder, one of them being politician Andrey Lugovoy, were said to have left a radioactive trail behind them wherever they went and so were identified. But many Russia experts say the murderers will not come to justice due to the fact that the Russian government had wanted Litvinenko to be killed.
