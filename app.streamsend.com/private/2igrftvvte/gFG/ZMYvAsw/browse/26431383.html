        1. Student Achievements
        Fourth Plinth Schools Awards 2016
        The Fourth Plinth Programme is the most talked about contemporary art prize in the UK. Funded by the Mayor of London and supported by Arts Council England, the programme invites world class artists to make astonishing new works for the centre of the capital city.

        As part of the programme, London’s school children from all London boroughs are asked to come up with brilliant ideas about what it means to live in the capital. This year’s annual Schools Awards attracted more than 1,800 entries of creative works inspired by the Fourth Plinth in Trafalgar Square.

        14 of Halcyon’s Grade 7 students have had their work accepted by the London Mayor Fourth Plinth Schools Awards! We would like to wish a massive well done to the following students, and thank you to Art Teacher Sue Dance for guiding the students and submitting their work:
        • Tallulah and Annmarie (joint piece)
        • Linus
        • Darrien
        • Mary Iris
        • Davide
        • Alexia
        • Jeffrey
        • Lucca
        • Jonah
        • Leyli and Noemi (joint piece)
        • Ethan
        • Javier
        Grade 7 created sculptures from recycled materials, which they collected over the term, as part of their Service and Action commitment. The students then used these materials to construct their imaginative sculptures in response to British culture.

        See the Fourth Plinth Schools Award 2016 gallery here. Halcyon’s students feature on pages 104-118.

        2. Adventures in Learning
        Grade 10 Trip, Water Treatment Facility 
        Date: Wednesday 13 April
        Time: Depart Halcyon at 0900, Return to Halcyon at 1500
        Location: The Water Treatment Plant, Slough
        Grade 10 have currently been studying the chemistry of water and have been focusing on how water is recycled. The students have a great opportunity to visit a Water Treatment Facility in Slough where they will tour and observe the recycling process. They will also be able to participate in experiments which focus on water purification on a small scale and how the purification process can generate electricity for the facility.

        G10 will be travelling by coach to and from the facility. They will leave at 0900 and will return back to school at 1500.

        Students will need to bring:
        • a fully charged iPad
        • comfortable walking shoes
        • warm jacket
        A packed lunch will be provided. If you need any further information then please contact Mrs Khan or Mr Alcantud.


        Grade 10 Trip, Serpentine Galleries
        Date: Thursday 14 April
        Time: Depart Halcyon at 0915, Return to Halcyon at 1315
        Location: The Serpentine Galleries, Kensington Gardens, London
        The Art Trip for all G10 Students on Thursday 14 April is now to the Serpentine Galleries in Kensington Gardens.

        Students will have the opportunity to view an exhibition called "painting the unseen" by the Swedish pioneer abstract artist, Hilma da Klint, who painted hidden worlds found within nature and the spiritual realm.

        The second exhibition called "Das Institute" is by a group of artists working collaboratively with installations, neon signs and mixed media.

        This will allow students to study the various themes and explore any connections to the play that they are studying in English and use them to experiment with creative writing. Art students will use connections for ideas in their art-works.

        G10 will walk from school to Kensington Gardens.

        Students will need to bring:
        • a fully charged iPad
        • a topped up Oyster card
        • comfortable walking shoes
        • warm jacket
        A packed lunch will be provided, but students may wish to bring money for an extra snack at the cafe.

        Students will be accompanied by Sue Dance (Art), Jo Ranson and Hannah Shillito (English).

          Grade 6 Trip, British Museum
          Date: Friday 15 April
          Time: Depart Halcyon at 0915, Return to Halcyon at 1315
          Location: The British Museum, Great Russell St, London WC1B 3DG
          Grade 6 will be visiting The British Museum on Friday 15 April for the Art and Drama department, being accompanied by Sue Dance and Kelly Foster. The students will have the opportunity to observe and investigate ceremonial or tribal masks from various cultures. Students will be able to make notes, take photographs and draw shapes and patterns, to give them inspiration for their own masks that they will be creating over Term 3. These "character" masks will then be used in their Drama classes for their performance, exploring the concept of transformation.

          Students will be travelling from Halcyon at 0915 to the Museum. The group will arrive back to Halcyon at 1315.

          Students will need to bring:
          • a fully charged iPad
          • a topped up Oyster card
          • comfortable walking shoes
          • warm jacket
          The school will provide a packed lunch, but students may bring money to buy an extra snack.


          Grade 7 Trip, National Gallery
          Date: Thursday 21 April
          Time: Depart Halcyon at 0915, Return to Halcyon at 1230
          Location: National Gallery, Trafalgar Square, London WC2N 5DN
          Grade 7 will be visiting the National Gallery on Thursday 21 April to study the Impressionist painters. This is part of their interdisciplinary unit "Colour and Light" with Science and Art, whereby they will have the opportunity to investigate the scientific colour theory used by these artists.

          Students will use their iPads to photograph and make annotations on the characteristics found in the paintings, which will they will continue to analyse in both classes.

          Students will be accompanied by Sue Dance and Lori Fritz. The group will leave Halcyon at 0915 and return by lunch, 1230.

          Students will need to bring:
          • a topped up Oyster card
          • fully charged iPad
          • warm jacket
          • comfortable walking shoes

          Grade 9 Trip, Imperial War Museum

          New Staff
          Miguel Alcantud - Integrated Science & Chemistry Teacher
          Miguel Alcantud has 16 years of experience teaching Integrated Science in Middle and High School, plus IB Biology, Chemistry and ESS, as well as some Maths, English for adults and Spanish as a second language. Miguel has worked for several schools in Spain with most of his teaching experience at the American School of Valencia, where he enjoyed engaging in the IBDP.

          Earning a university degree in Biochemistry as well as two Diplomas for teaching Spanish and English as a second language, Miguel has also completed training in IB Biology and Chemistry.

          Miguel developed a passion for education as soon as he understood that life itself was an everlasting learning process and was also inspired by some of his own teachers.

          Coming from the Mediterranean side of Spain, he arrived in London last August with his American-Spanish multicultural family. Miguel considers himself a citizen of the world with deep mediterranean roots, who enjoys bringing this international perspective into his classes.  He loves sharing the learning process with students, as he believes that the role of a teacher is to guide and accompany them throughout that process.  

          Miguel speaks Spanish, English, Catalan and some Italian. He loves music (rock, Brit-pop, mediterranean folk), poetry, cooking, cycling or hiking.

          Please join us in welcoming Miguel to our team!

          Street Art As a Growing International Art Business: Talk for Parents and Students
          Date: Thursday 21 April
          There are two sessions available please sign-up here for one: 1545 - 1715 OR 1830 - 2000 that same evening
          Have you ever wondered about the art on London's city streets? The spray painted walls of East and South London? How street art is an international art business? Then come to our latest special parent, student, teacher education evening where we will be hosting a street art expert on the topic and one of London's own street artists, Rich Simmons who will do an actual street art demo for us. We will cover all these areas and a lot more. Rich's demo is of one of his most recognised art pieces. If you have any questions about the talks please email Stacey Ciauri.

          Halcyon Bookclub
          Date: Wednesday 13 April
          Book: ‘How to be Both’ by Ali Smith
          Time: 1330

          Date: Tuesday 17 May
          Book: ‘The Sense of an Ending’ by Julian Barnes
          Time: 1400

          ‘How to be Both’ is a novel about art’s versatility. It is a fast-moving, genre-bending conversation about 2 tales, set 500 years apart. The book was shortlisted for the Man Booker Prize in 2014.

          ‘The Sense of an Ending’ is a novel about the imprecision of memory and how it constructs people, stories and histories...it was the winner of the Man Booker Prize in 2011.
