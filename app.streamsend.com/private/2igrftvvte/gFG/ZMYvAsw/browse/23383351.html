1. Director Search Update
Now that the deadline for applications has passed, the Board would like to provide an update on the Director search.
  
Firstly, we are sad to inform you that our current Head, Duncan Partridge, after much deliberation, had decided not to apply for the post. As Duncan told staff earlier this week: “This decision is by no means a reflection of my appreciation of working with ... the whole school community. I believe that the vision of the school and the talent and commitment of the team working to realise that vision is truly outstanding. It has been and will continue to be my privilege to work with you all.

“At the moment, my personal and professional plans do not fit with a long term contractual commitment and both myself and the Board feel that having in place a Director who will be able to make such a commitment will be important in leading the school into its next stage of development. I have made it clear to the Board that I am more than happy to support the school going forward in any way that I can and both they and I hope that our paths will continue to cross in the coming exciting years.”

Secondly, Odgers Berndtson continued a robust Director search. After worldwide efforts during the last several weeks, our search firm team reviewed over 100 interesting applicants. They compiled a list of the top 30 applicants which they and the Board discussed in detail this week. The Board was delighted with the quality of the candidates and has selected seven candidates which they will interview in the next three weeks. Thereafter, the shortlisted candidates will meet with representatives of other stakeholder groups including parents, staff and students. The Board aims to conclude the process over the Term break or shortly thereafter.
2. Punctuality
We note that some students are arriving at school after the start of the school day of 0845. Advisory periods from 0845 to 0900 are mandatory and very important parts of the school programme. All students are expected to arrive at school on time in order to participate in these sessions. Students arriving after 0850 are marked as late. These statistics are reported on the student’s academic report. Please may we ask that parents and carers do all they can to ensure students arrive at school punctually.

      12. Grade 8 World War One Interdisciplinary Unit
      Just before the February half term, Grade 8 completed their Unit on World War One. This was an Inter-Disciplinary Unit (IDU) between Drama and Humanities. Students were asked to produce a written monologue and perform it in front of the class. The written monologue had to be historically accurate (in terms of names, phrasing, factual details) and be set in a true historical context. This meant that students had to really synthesise their knowledge of both Drama and Humanities in order to succeed.

      Students had a lot of fun and very high quality work was produced (a fair number of students gaining full marks on some Criteria!). The depth of the historical research was highly impressive. 

      Next year, we hope to also include English and Art in this unit, and cannot wait to plan more IDUs in the future.
        13. Dove Campaign for Real Beauty came to visit Halcyon
        A representative from the Dove Self-Esteem Project conducted a workshop with Halcyon’s Grade 6 and Grade 7 to help them understand the role of media and the power it has over how young people (and others) view themselves and others.

        The session was organised by Jackie in Grade 10 as part of her IBMYP Personal Project which examines the effect of digital body modification in advertising.

        The workshop included content on self-esteem and body image. The session discussed the challenging issues of peer and media pressure that children face every day and made particular reference to the use of airbrushing in magazines and music videos and discussed being happy with one’s own appearance. Other issues covered included safe internet practice and appropriate use of technology in relation to photography.

        Photographs of people that had been digitally modified were examined and the students were asked what they noticed about the photos. They remarked that some of the so-called “flaws” had been reduced or omitted. However these “flaws” are part of who we are so what happens when the media tries to “erase” them? Students were amazed by how much people’s appearance could be changed through these modifications. Jackie was really happy with the workshop and felt everyone who attended got something out of it.

          14. Global Issues Network
          This year, Global Issues Network (GIN) students have been working in partnership with Forrester Court Care Home in Paddington. Students have been working in two areas, developing a community garden at the home and a social project by spending time with the residents at the Home. The project has been named Seeds of Growth. In late March, students will present their work to other students from all over the globe at the annual GIN conference, which will be held in Milan.

          As part of the social project, 7th and 8th Grade made Valentine's Day cards to give to the Forrester Court residents. A couple of days before Valentine's Day, some of the GIN Club members went to the Home to deliver the cards. The residents were happy and excited when they received them. On previous trips to the Home, we have been talking with them and getting to know some of the residents there.

          In the second aspect of the project, GIN members have come up with an idea to grow crops for the Care Home's cook to use. We think this is a good idea because the residents can have natural, free and organic herbs and vegetables. We first cleaned their garden of weeds and then we started to make a patch of compost to grow our crops. We currently have placed a fence around the compost to make everything look aesthetically pleasing. Next we will plant seeds in the spring and we will be excited to see our crops completely grown in the summer.
