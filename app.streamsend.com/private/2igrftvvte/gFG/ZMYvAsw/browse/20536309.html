5. Step One Career Testing
Halcyon is very pleased to announce that Step One Career Testing for our Grade 10 students will take place during Term 1 next academic year. Step One Career Testing will be an integral part of our Careers programme and our subject selection counselling process for students selecting their IB Diploma Programme subjects. Each student will complete an online questionnaire (scheduled for 2 ½ hours) during the week commencing 17 November 2014. The questionnaire attempts to identify students’ strengths, weaknesses, and main areas of interest. Thereafter, students have a 40 minute individual meeting with trained and experienced career counsellors during the week commencing 8 December 2014. Students then receive a detailed written report which can be used by the family as a discussion point leading into the IB Diploma Programme course selection later in the year.

10. Grade 9 Theatre Trip: “Wicked” at Apollo 
"It’s looking at things another way…" - Fiyero

This line, from the popular West-End Musical “Wicked”, resonated with the Grade 9 Class as they headed to the Apollo Theatre last week. Throughout their recent Fantasy Fiction Unit in English, students had explored different ways of thinking about literature, as well as the ethical parameters of the genre as a whole. In-depth studies into the early origins of this genre through Aesop’s Fables, the classic perspective in the text The Wonderful Wizard of Oz, and the more contemporary take in Markus Zusak’s The Book Thief, had prepared the class to critique this stage spin-off. 12. Humanities at Halcyon
Grade 6 have just finished a unit on digital mapping, which culminated in their gothic mapping assessment. They used Google Maps and Google Earth to create routes with information, pictures and the points of latitude and longitude of gothic sites in London to tie in with their new Gothic Literature unit in English. Grade 6 have now moved on to the concept of settlement. They are using the satellite imagery in Google Maps to try to establish which geographical features led to London becoming the capital city and have drawn their own ideal settlement.

Grade 7 have been studying plate tectonics, particularly earthquakes and volcanoes. They have studied the different types of plate boundaries and created a presentation on a case study of their choice of a recent tectonic event. Grade 7 produced a series of posters to instruct others on what to do during an earthquake.

Grade 8 have just started studying the Enlightenment. All students are completing a biography of a key Enlightenment figure and today they taught Grade 6 some of the key concepts and ideas.

Grade 9 have been studying development and are currently writing a newspaper report which answers the question: "Is globalisation beneficial to development?" They have analysed some clips from the Facing History resource Reporter to help them think about some of the issues journalists face in their reporting on development issues.
