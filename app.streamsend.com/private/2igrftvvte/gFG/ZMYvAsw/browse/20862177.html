1. Play your part in developing Halcyon’s online presence
You, your family, friends and colleagues can help build and develop Halcyon’s online presence with a few simple clicks. The more connections there are to Halcyon on the internet through visits, likes, reviews, follows, etc. the better placed we will be in search listings. Help us move up the virtual ranks so that new families and fellow educators can find us more easily.

In the next few newsletters, we will list one way in which you can help improve our virtual school profile. This week, it’s all about Twitter. Twitter is a network through which to communicate and stay connected via exchanges of short (140 characters or fewer), quick, frequent messages. It’s a great way to discover the latest news related to subjects you care about. We use Twitter to share interesting school news and engage with people. Go here to see our homepage: https://twitter.com/HalcyonSchool

Do you tweet? If so, please “Follow” us: @HalcyonSchool If you know others who tweet, please ask them to follow us too. We’ll let you know how our Twitter family grows!
2. Halcyon's first international conference
Well done to Halcyon Global Issues Network (GIN) club, who on Thursday participated in their first international conference. They presented in front of students and teachers from all over the globe. The students-led 45-minute sessions educated and raised awareness of sanitation issues. The students have participated in sessions by other schools and listened to a range of keynote speakers. We will report more next week, but if you would like to get a taste of the inspirational speakers here at Euro GIN you can watch this TED talk by Cassandra Lin, who at 10 years old, started to provide biofuel to struggling families in Rhode Island.
3. Global Issues Network Backpacks' Donation
The backpacks donated by Halcyon students were picked up this week and are ready to be sent to Malawi. This effort was co-ordinated by the GIN club. Thanks to all students who participated in the campaign.
4. Maths Competitions
A big congratulations to Katie and Eric who participated in the UKMT Olympiad and Kangaroo competitions on Thursday. The competitions have not only the best young mathematicians in the UK competing, but the best mathematicians globally. We look forward to hearing the results.
5. Google Science Fair
Last week in Personal Learning Period (PLP), students were introduced to the Google Science Fair. This is a fantastic competition where students conduct their own research projects and have the chance to win some great prizes. Please visit the Google Science Fair website, which has useful sections to help students get inspired and also gives advice for parents to support their child’s projects. Students can get support after school or may choose to do their projects during PLP time. They can work by themselves or in groups or two or three. Good Luck!
6. Art Update
Grade 6 is currently taking part in a printing unit dealing with patterns on a collaborative wall-hanging. The students explored the idea of repetitive patterns communicating motion.

After having studied Islamic designs using the circle as the basis of Grade 7’s "Design with Geometry" unit, students created ceramic tiles and coloured pencil drawings of geometric and arabesque design work. Their recent visit to the V&A Museum fuelled their inspiration.
7. National Gallery Trip
Grade 7 had a lovely morning at the National Gallery observing the Impressionist painters this week. The trip was helpful to the students, as they are investigating the scientific methods that the artists used to capture light and portray a technique of optical blending. Students are studying the "way we see" in Science; and in Art, they are experimenting and understanding the theory of colour and how this enhanced the way the artists perceived light and colour.
8. Sports Update: Our First Football Tournament
After several individual fixtures, Halcyon has been invited to participate in our first sports tournament. This is a boy's 6-a-side football event and will take place on 14 May at King Fahad Academy. The tournament will involve six other international schools from the London area. We hope to take part in a similar tournament for the Halcyon girls' football team in the Summer Term.
9. Grade 6 Community Project
Grade 6 has started their Community Project. During workshops this week, students explored identity and communication skills and tools, building towards maintaining and strengthening a positive community. The class worked hard together to design their Class Agreements with much thoughtful and insightful discussion.
