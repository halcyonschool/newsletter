        UKMT Team Challenge
        This week at The City of London School, 30 London schools each sent a team of 4 students to "do maths".​ Halcyon’s “Twin Twin Team” was made up of Alexia, Christina, Linus and Jonah, all in Grade 7.​

        ​The morning started with Round One which ​was​ the​ only round where the​ four students work​ed​ together to answer 10 questions. For ​Round Two the team split into two ​pairs and each pair completed​ either the down or the across clues to answer.

        After lunch, Round ​Three saw​ the team work in pairs with a series of questions where the answer​ to one question was needed to answer the next question. Lots of concentration was required! The final round was slightly more energetic. The pairs were split across the large hall for a relay where instead of a baton they transferred questions to each other.

        Unfortunately our team didn't win but they had a great time and a tremendous effort was demonstrated! They had lots of heated discussions about maths and got their highest score on round three. Well done Twin Twin Team!

        Next up for Grades 6 and 7 will be the UKMT individual mathematics challenge in April. Watch this space!

        2. Student Voice
        UN on Trial Trip
        On the evening of Friday 26 February 2016, some Halcyon students attended the LSE Lecture "The UN on Trial". This mock trial was part of the LSE Law Literary Festival and saw the United Nations having to defend its record of:
        • protecting human rights
        • keeping peace around the world
        • developing friendly relations among nations
        • conquering hunger, disease and illiteracy
        These things were the 'intentions' of the UN when it was first set up and the mock trial was to see if the UN has fulfilled these original aims.

        The 'trial' was presided over by The Hon. Mr Justice R. M. Jay. Some of the main arguments for the prosecution were:
        • due to the United Nations having no real authority and being governed by the nations itself; it has not defended human rights properly
        • nations only act in their self-interest
        • peacekeeping missions around the world have failed
        The defence argued:
        • even though the United Nations did have many flaws, it is vital to the survival of millions of people
        • the United Nations food initiatives, medical initiatives and peacekeeping missions save millions of lives around the world
        Although not all countries have friendly relations, at least they are talking to each other.

        It was an interesting and informative event and we enjoyed it very much.

        Thank you,
        Kieran, Grade 10

          5. Tech Insider
          Oh No! My iPad or mobile phone screen is broken!
          When a mobile phone or iPad screen cracks, it is compromised. This means that it does not take much for the rest of the screen to shatter. The screen is made out of glass and it can cause cuts as well as shards of glass entering the skin.

          If you have a cracked screen, it is time to get it repaired and it should not be used or brought to school again until it is fixed.

          6. Inspiring SA and CAS
          What is CAS?
          What is CAS? Creativity (how we are creative) Activity (how we keep physically active) and Service (how we help others) is a core part of the IB Diploma Programme and what all students in the school are working towards, starting with the Service Action (SA) programme in the MYP. It is a pathway to living a balanced and fulfilling life. We want the whole community to be inspired, so are asking staff, parents and students to share their experiences. We will run a four week feature in the newsletter and morning notes with a snapshot of Halcyon community CAS participation.

          Please contribute your experiences here to help us all inspire each other.


          What is your name? Claire Rose
          What is your position? Teacher
          What do you do to be creative? I study online courses. At the moment, I am studying an online short course from the University of Oxford, called "Archaeology in Practice". The other people on the course are from all over the world and from many different backgrounds. Some are studying to help their career, but most are doing it for fun (like me!). I also take part in weekly tap dancing lessons (I've put this under creativity, rather than activity because it's not really exercise to me!).
          What physical activities do you do to maintain a healthy lifestyle? I go to the gym three or four times a week, but I don't like to use the equipment. I like Pilates, Yoga, Zumba and other fun classes. Although it is good for my health, I also like to go swimming - but just to clear my head and relax, rather than keep fit. I'm currently thinking of training for the "Muddy Dog Challenge" this year, ran by Battersea Dog's home, which is where I have to do an assault course with my dog.
          What service activities do you do or have you done in the past? I am part of a local volunteer group called 'Time and Talents'. We have a "Timebank" database, which is where you can volunteer one hour of your time (when someone needs help with something). So far, I have cooked a meal, visited an elderly person, collected shopping and painted a wall. The concept behind it is that it doesn't matter what your 'talent' is - everyone's time is of equal worth. It also means that I get to know members of my local community a bit better.
          What are your CAS goals for the future? I may progress onto a more advanced online course (like an MA); I also would like to learn something else. For example, I used to do language lessons at evening classes. I'd love to take art classes one day. I think my CAS goals in general are to be balanced in what I'm involved in (some physical, some relaxing and some mentally challenging).
