6. Halcyon’s Mother Tongue 2014-15 programme
We are pleased to announce that our Mother Tongue Programme (MTP) has started. We are already offering, Swedish, German and Italian with nine students participating, and we are just about to add more languages as required by our students.

The Halcyon MTP is giving our students the opportunity to improve their literacy in their first language and with that their level of linguistic competence in other languages will improve as well.

If you are interested in your child participating in our Mother Tongue Programme or require further information, please contact Noelia Zago.
7. Halcyon Guest Speakers Programme
Halcyon is honoured to offer the opportunity to hear an amazing story of survival. On Tuesday 30 September, Susan Pollack, a Hungarian Jew who survived Auschwitz concentration camp, will be coming to speak at Halcyon. The opportunity to hear her talk is open to students in Grades 9 and 10, Halcyon staff and Halcyon parents. The talk will take place in the Hub from 1545. Please contact Stephanie Cooke if you have any questions.

We are also delighted that as part of the Halcyon Guest Speakers programme, Javier Sanchez, father of Celia and Andres, will be addressing students on the subject of marketing and advertising. Javier speaks from his experience in working for Coca-Cola. This talk is open to all students at lunchtime on Friday 3 October in the Hub.

If you, or anyone you know, would like to address our students, please complete this Google Form or get in touch with Stephanie Cooke.

12. Grade 9 Trip to BP Portrait Award Exhibition
Grade 9 had a wonderful opportunity last week to see the BP Portrait Awards exhibition before it ended this weekend. The students are doing portrait work this Term in both paint and collage under the Global Context of "Orientation in time and Space". This allowed them to explore the various artists who depicted the sitter in a particular time or a space, therefore giving the students ideas through which they can experiment on their own portraits. It also allowed the students to observe the different styles of contemporary painting, giving them ideas for their interpretation.
