2. Halcyon’s Growing Community
We would like to officially share some exciting news with you regarding the very promising growth of Halcyon London International School.

As of last week, we enrolled our 67th student for next term. This is exciting, because our projections for September 2014 were based on 68 students and we are more or less already there. For those of you who like numbers:
  • Having started with 34 students in September 2013, we are almost exactly at 100% growth already.
  • Now being at 43 students, we grew almost 30% over the first school year including losing two students mid-year.
  • We have well over 20 nationalities for Sept 2014 (updated stats coming).
  • We can easily grow a bit more for next year!

International, local and expatriate families from various backgrounds are looking to join our growing community so their children can enjoy a co-ed, progressive, rigourous IB education. Many inquiries are coming thanks to leading educational and relocation professionals. A growing number of London’s well-established prep schools are also recommending Halcyon to their internationally-minded families.

Many thanks to Halcyon parents and colleagues who have been also spreading the word and have sparked immense interest. We also are grateful to our wonderful staff who have contributed greatly to building our growing reputation.

4. London's first Model United Nations (MUN) Conference - at Halcyon!
In the spirit of Halcyon’s commitment to international-mindedness and civic engagement, we will be hosting a Model United Nations (MUN) conference on Friday 6 June 2014. Organized by our very own maths and biology teacher, Gareth Jones, this will be the first ever Model United Nations (MUN) Conference for international schools in London. MUN conferences strive to teach diplomacy between multiple groups, improve debating and leadership skills and develop an understanding of current affairs and global issues. Students will represent different member states of the United Nations and delegations will debate to work towards the development of resolutions for global conflicts. These issues will be similar to those debated in the actual United Nations. The conference will bring various international and state schools to Halcyon, exposing students to more people from various backgrounds and nationalities. By linking international and state schools through MUN education, the conference will also serve to build partnerships between the participating schools. This will be an opportunity to enhance the students’ global mindsets and understanding of the world they live in. The conference at Halcyon also provides an opportunity for students to gain experience and confidence in MUN before attending larger conferences such as THIMUN in The Hague or PAMUN in Paris. The theme of this first conference: The stability of North Africa and the Middle East.

8. Grade 6 Exploring Myths and Legends
Grade 6 went to the British Museum with Sue Dance and Michelle Jones, as they are combining their new unit with Art and English. They are studying "myths and legends" with Michelle Jones and together with these amazing stories, the students went the British Museum to research and get ideas from all the incredible masks representing the many cultures. Students spent time finding out what the masks were used for and whether some of their "Myths" connected with any of the imaginative masks we saw. They also spent time drawing them, collecting ideas for shapes, textures and patterns, which might influence them for their own masks that they will be constructing this term.
9. Halcyon Students Assist at Facing History and Ourselves Benefit Dinner
Halcyon students were a great support to the charity, Facing History and Ourselves as their first UK Benefit Dinner at RIBA last week. As part of this year C&S programme, seven Halcyon’s students: Gwen and Charlie Sears, Celia Sanchez Ylla, Eric Och, Isla Mathieson, Rachel Green and Alex Wiklund volunteered to help at this interesting event. They were outstanding representatives of our school, contributing to Halcyon’s positive reputation for being community-minded.

Charlie and Celia posed with guest speaker John Amaechi and guest, Razia Iqbal, BBC Special Correspondent. Mr Amaechi OBE, the New York Times best-selling author, human rights advocate and former NBA player, gave a moving talk about his own life story and how racism and prejudicial behaviour can limit a person and the importance of speaking out against bigotry and learning to shape your own informed decisions without relying on stereotypes.
