1. Attention G9, G10 & G11 PSAT Offering - 14 October 2015
As part of Halcyon's ongoing commitment to provide students with opportunities to pursue their learning in higher education, we will be offering the Preliminary Scholastic Aptitude Test (PSAT) to any interested G9-11 students who are considering university study in the USA. The PSAT is the precursor to the SAT's. The PSAT offers students additional exposure before taking the SATs. The SATs or ACTs are often a required application component for many universities in the USA. 

More information about the PSAT can be found here. If you have any additional questions, please contact Elani McDonald.

Additionally, if this is the first time your child(ren) is (are) taking a SAT-related test, we would highly recommend students further familiarise themselves with the format and content of the test. Students can do so by attempting questions on the web or picking up an old copy of the PSSS (Preliminary SAT Scoring Service) test from Elani McDonald. 

For PSAT sign-up please complete this form.

6. Mark Your Calendars: University Admissions Visit
Date: Thursday 24 September
Time: 1545 - 1645 to meet with Trinity College, University of Virginia and University of North Carolina
Location: Halcyon

On Thursday 24 September, Halcyon will be visited by admissions officers from three US universities. 

We would like to invite parents and students (G9-11) to come along to these visits, if they are interested in the universities and what they have to offer. It's also a great way to begin to inform yourselves about the US university process. 

Trinity College is a private Liberal Arts College located in Hartford, Connecticut with fewer than 2,300 undergraduates. This is a city-campus where all freshman (first year students) are required to live on campus. Trinity is also known for students' ability to work closely with faculty and take advantage of internships thanks to being located in the state's capital. The school has a beautiful 100 acre campus in an urban setting. Trinity has a campus in Rome, Italy and more than half of Trinity students study abroad during their time in college. 

University of Virginia is a state (public) university that has approximately 16,000 undergraduates. This suburban campus was founded by Thomas Jefferson and is located in Charlottesville. Only first-year students are required to live on campus, and Greek life (sorority and fraternity) is prominent at UVA with a membership that includes approximately 30% of the student body. The Cavaliers, are members of the NCAA Division I Atlantic Coast Conference. UVA's graduate programmes include the highly ranked Darden Graduate School of Business Administration, Curry School of Education, School of Engineering and Applied Science, School of Law and School of Medicine. 

The University of North Carolina (UNC) at Chapel Hill is one of America's "Public Ivy" schools. UNC has just over 18,000 undergraduates and is divided into several schools and colleges. Chapel Hill's schools of medicine, law and business all have excellent reputations. They are a member of the AAU (Association of American Universities) and Phi Beta Kappa.

Please RSVP to reception@halcyonschool.com.

The London Art Salon is offering a lecture on the invention and rise of self-portraiture from humble beginnings as hidden painted faces to the obsessive documenting and displaying of our own digital images. While the "selfie" is being billed as a massive contemporary phenomenon, photographers did not wait for the invention of digital smart phones or selfie sticks in order to turn the camera on themselves. The London Art Salon brings together expert speakers in a series of talks and discussion that cover London's current art exhibitions, events and art market. Registration is essential here. Westminster Historical Highlights Tours
Tuesday 29 September 1000-1200
Meeting Place: Outside the front of St Margaret's Westminster 1000
Finish: Green Park Tube Station 1200
15 people max
£12 per person

Westminster is the seat of monarchy and government in Britain and has been for nearly 1,000 years. The monarchs have moved their palaces several times and we shall see where they all are or were. Plus we will see the building from which Charles I was executed in 1649 and understand how constitutional government evolved over the centuries, and learn how the office of Prime Minister emerged. We will amble our way along important streets and through arguably, London's most beautiful park, St James. Tour led by London historian/Blue Badge Guide Philippa Owen. Sign up is essential here. For any questions, please contact Stacey Ciauri.

Shoreditch Street Art
Tuesday 13 October 1000-1230
Meeting Place: Urban Street Experience, Outside exit 3 of Old Street station
£30 plus £1.26 surcharge

Shoreditch is the epitome of London's creative community and has been home to street art from all over the world. This is a great tour to experience a part of London that is constantly brimming with creative talents and just a funky part of town. Even if you have been on a street art tour in the past, it's worth going again as the art is constantly changing. This tour is organised by From My City which is a trusted tour organiser and we have had great tour guides from them in the past. Please sign up directly on this page for Halcyon so that we can keep track of who's coming. For any questions, please contact Stacey Ciauri.

City of London Architecture Tour
Wednesday 18 November 1030-1230
Meeting Place: One New Change EC4M 9AF
£30 plus £1.26 surcharge

This is an amazing tour with the former head of planning for the City of London. On our walk around the City of London, we will look first at the immediate setting of St Paul's Cathedral and its transformation over recent decades. Our route will continue via Cheapside, the City's revitalised High Street, towards Bank and the Royal Exchange. The tour will continue through the medieval alleyways off Cornhill and take in Leadenhall Market and the Lloyds Building before visiting the cluster of new office towers which house London's insurance sector. Our walk will then turn north to visit Broadgate and Liverpool Station, finishing on the northeastern corner of the City adjacent to Spitalfields. This tour is organised by From My City. Please sign up directly on this page for Halcyon so that we can keep track of who's coming. For any questions, please contact Stacey Ciauri.
