3. Halcyon Eco-venture June 2014
Before the Winter holiday we announced that all Halcyon students will have the opportunity to come face-to-face with nature and develop their IB Learner Profile attributes. Students will spend five days from 16-20 June at the Outward Bounds Centre in Aberdovey, Snowdonia National Park. During this time they will participate in an Eco and Sustainability Programme and work towards achieving the John Muir Award. Students will discover and explore the wilderness and take part in a conservation project such as building bat boxes and dry stone walling. Students will then share their findings with others. Please contact Mia Rosengren if you have any questions about the trip.

6. EAL Podcast Walking Trip
As part of their ongoing English Additional Language (EAL) studies, six Halcyon students will be going on a self-designed podcast walk of the Marble Arch/Baker Street area next week.

This podcast walk is the culmination of weeks of intensive study into grammatical concepts, comprehension readings based on our local area, and communicative tasks.

Using Google Maps, our EAL students have designed a walk around the local area. The digital captions for this walk demonstrate the students' growing understanding of grammatical concepts such as nouns and verbs. The verbal commentary (which will include directions from one site to the next, as well as descriptions for each site) will demonstrate the students' understanding of prepositions, nouns of state and being, as well as vocabulary specific to their chosen site.

The goal is that by working in our local environment, our EAL students will gain a greater understanding of their new city while using this interactive way to learn the grammatical concepts needed to communicate confidently and successfully.

For more information about this exciting project, please contact Michelle Jones or Stephanie Cooke.
7. World Book Day Celebration
Celebrated for the first time in 1995, UNESCO’s World Book Day was introduced to promote reading, publishing and copyrighting.

Thursday saw the UK’s celebration of World Book Day, and Halcyon was quick to embrace this event as a community. In the usual Halcyon way, technology was an integral part of the event’s preparations and activities: Google Forms allowed Halcyon students, parents and teachers to collaboratively propose their literary-inspired breakfast foods; Goodreads, RM Books, and the World Book Day apps afforded students an opportunity to browse through thousands of digital books prior to the event; and a virtual event with author Robert Muchamore (Cherub Series, Rock War) brought his literary world into our very own Hub.

The celebration was made even more festive thanks to a community breakfast where a number of families brought in yummy treats to support our voracious reading appetites! >> Read the full blog post here.
