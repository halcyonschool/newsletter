2. REMINDER: Halcyon Official Opening Ceremony Date
Come and help us celebrate the Halcyon Official Opening Ceremony at 1800-2000 on 30 January 2014! All parents are invited. You will enjoy seeing how far the school has come and get to meet the broader Halcyon community.
3. After-school Activities - Term 2
On Monday, students will have the chance to sign up to after-school activities in their advisory group. Please see the list below for current options. Once students have indicated their preferences, we will contact you to get permission for their participation in after-school activities. After-school activities are usually scheduled to run from 1545-1645 in various locations.
String quartet - Mondays commencing 6 January
Ms Virtanen will lead a four (or more) string group (violin, viola, cello). Students can develop their classical musical talent and have some fun! Private lessons are available after the String Quartet Club. Please contact Laura Virtanen to find out more information and express interest. 

Cost: same as Term 1.
Drama Club – Mondays commencing 13 January
The Halcyon Drama Club, led by Mr Proctor, is a space for students to engage in fun and creative drama games and activities. Open to all abilities, the club aims to build a team of budding actors, directors, script-writers (perhaps even the odd singer and dancer) to produce exciting dramatic work. 

Cost: To be determined (approximately £10-15 per session, calculated according to demand).
Table Tennis Club - Tuesdays commencing 14 January till end of February
*Limited spaces left*
Table Tennis coaching will be offered by a former Table Tennis pro, Natasha from TopSpin. The sessions will take place in Stern Hall from 1600-1700 on Tuesdays. If you are interested in improving your Table Tennis skills, please express interest to Mr Byrne. 

Cost: £50 per student for the 5 lessons.
Football Club - Tuesdays commencing 25 February - end of May
The popular Football club running as an after-school club from Term 1 will continue during the second part of Term 2. The fun sessions will focus on fitness, skills, shooting, formations and tactical awareness during a match. 

Cost: same as Term 1.
Debate Club - Tuesdays or Thursdays commencing 14 January
Following a successful whole school debate last Term, Debate Club will continue this Term for those students who are interested in developing and practicing their public speaking (and arguing!) skills. Students will debate a broad range of topics, including education, technology and politics. This will be a fun and lively activity, with students thinking about and discussing complicated issues.

Cost: free.
GIN Club - Wednesdays commencing 15 January
Global Issues Network will continue this Term and allow students to engage with current events on a global scale. Students will develop their own ideas for how to tackle global issues. The club will concentrate on small projects and the emphasis will be on action. In time, GIN will become a student-led activity, which will aim to enable students in the Halcyon community to effect positive change.

Cost: free.
Choir Club & Private Singing Lessons - Thursdays commencing 16 January
We will be running a school choir led by choir mistress Lucinda Allen from Singing Nation every Thursday afternoon from 1545-1630 in the canteen. All students who might be interested in joining are invited to participate in a Choir Taster Session after school on Thursday, 9 January. 

If your child is then interested in joining Choir Club, the activity will run every Thursday.

Cost: to be determined (approximately £10-15 per session, calculated according to demand). 

In addition to the after-school choir, Lucinda will be available Thursday at lunchtime and afternoons (after the choir practice) to run individual and small group singing lessons for 45 minutes. 

Cost: from £37.50 for a single student lesson, £30 for a two-student group lesson and £25 for a three-student group lesson.

Please contact reception@halcyonschool.com to express interest.
Mandarin Club - Thursdays and one lunchtime (TBC)
As part of our commitment to multi-lingualism, we would very much like to start a Mandarin club in the school so that students not taking Mandarin as their Language B can enjoy the opportunity to engage with an additional language. We are delighted that Mandarin Club will be taught by Halcyon's part-time Mandarin teacher, Ms Mei-Ling Lin who is an experienced classroom and private instructor.

The club will meet twice a week, after school on Thursdays and one lunchtime to be determined by the students.

Cost: To be determined (calculated according to demand).

Please contact mandarin@halcyonschool.com to express interest.
Homework Club - Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Thursdays
Students are always welcome to complete homework at school in the Hub at the end of the day. This term, Homework club is open on Tuesday, Wednesday and Thursday in the Hub between 1545 and 1645 (as well as every lunchtime Monday-Friday). Teachers will be available to support students with their work during this time.

Cost: free.
4. Lunchtime Activities - Term 2
On Monday, students will also have the chance to sign up to free lunchtime activities in their advisory group. Please see the list below for current options.
Halcyon TV - Mondays with Mr Jones
Calling all budding anchor men or women! Halcyon TV Club is an opportunity for those who want to develop their presenting, writing, creating, investigating and/or technological skills, and is open to everyone. 
C&S Club - Mondays and Thursdays with Ms Zago
Are you interested in building a stronger Halcyon community; environmental issues; or helping people in the wider community? For example, do you want to become a Halcyon Ambassador? If so, please come along to Halcyon's Community and Service Club with Ms Zago to see how we can work together on exciting projects for the community.
Art Club - Tuesdays with Mrs Dance
Come to the Art Club and use this time to find out how to use your own sketchbook (separate from the one in class) in a fun, colourful, exciting and meaningful way, so that it becomes a work of art. Bring your own pictures or photographs (anything you like) to give you ideas for your own work. You will be using the book for drawings, painting, collage and mixed media. You can set your own themes for the Term.
Science Club - Wednesdays with Ms Fritz
The Term will start with a crystal growing competition but students are also encouraged to develop their own science projects either individually or in groups.
Film Club - Fridays with Ms Cooke & Mrs Jones
Students will be shown a variety of films during the Term, some of which may link in with subjects they are studying at the time. Types of film will range from factual documentaries to creative fictional visions. Students will be invited to discuss and reflect on the merit, content and style of films.
