6. Personal Project News
As part of the IBMYP, students are required to complete a Personal Project (PP). This PP is an important part of each student’s graduation from the IBMYP and it offers an exciting opportunity for students to create a truly creative piece of work and to use the skills they have learnt during their time in the IBMYP. It is an opportunity for students to do something they really want to do, something they really like, and hence that they feel is personal. The Personal Project is also an excellent preparation for the rigours of Higher Education, as the PP is often considered the younger sibling of the IB Diploma Programme Extended Essay.

Each student is appointed a Personal Project supervisor. The role of the supervisor is to guide the students on how to complete the PP successfully. Supervisor and student must meet regularly and the supervisor will comment on each stage of the PP, giving positive and constructive feedback to the student and offering advice and encouragement. This means that much of the work involved in completing the PP is done outside of normal classes and so it requires from the student a high level of autonomy and responsibility. Apart from guiding the student, the supervisor plays a role in assessing the project as well. As we are a small school, teachers will be assigned one project each, so will be able to give your child lots of time and attention to guide them through this process.

At the end of the PP process, students present their PP in an interview before a panel of senior staff and prepare an exhibition where they share their work with the school community. This will be a joint exhibition with the Grade 8s, who will be completing their Community Project at the same time.

Due to the varying nature of projects, Halcyon cannot guarantee provision of the equipment and materials needed to complete each project. We shall help where we can; however students and their families should bear in mind the implications of choosing projects that may end up costing a great deal or requiring complex equipment. There is a lot of information contained within the guide; however, if you do have any further questions, please do not hesitate to contact Lenny Dutton, Personal Project Co-ordinator.

Finally, please complete the acknowledgement form, so that we know you have received and read this Guide.

A journey now begins and we would like to take the opportunity to wish each student good luck!

8. Javier Sanchez talks to us about how to be happy
Halcyon was pleased to host our first speaker in Stephanie Cooke’s careers’ series on 3 October.

Speaking in the Hub packed with students and staff, Javier Sanchez’s message was clear: the purpose of our lives is to be happy. Helping us understand that our well-being is determined by the sum of the emotional, physical and material in our lives, he also explained that each of us is an important resource to be valued and invested in throughout the years. The analogy of a bank account was very clear. A very amusing graph demonstrated what can happen if you live a hedonistic lifestyle early on - you may deplete your "savings" and be less happy in the long run, but if you invest in education and delay gratification this can lead to lifelong happiness. The video of the marshmallow experiment illustrated this very well. Children were put in a room with a marshmallow and told if they didn’t eat it for a certain amount of time, they would get two marshmallows. Studies have shown that those children who are able to delay gratification go on to be more successful and happier. 

We are hoping to welcome Javier back later in the year for a talk on marketing and advertising drawing on his experience at Coca-Cola.

10. The IB Diploma Programme (IBDP) - a brief explanation
We will shortly be publishing a programme of events which will guide and support Grade 10 students and their families in the transition from the IBMYP to IBDP.

The IBDP is a challenging and balanced programme of education with final examinations that prepares students, aged 16 to 19, for success at university and life beyond. It has been designed to address the intellectual, social, emotional and physical well-being of students. The programme, has gained recognition and respect from the world’s leading universities.

The figure above represents the curriculum’s requirements:

In essence, students must study one course from each of groups 1 to 5. A sixth subject must be chosen either from group 6, or a second subject from one of the other five groups.

Three subjects are offered at higher level - HL - (requiring 240 hours of teaching time), and three at standard level  - SL - (requiring 150 hours of teaching time).

Halcyon is already preparing the following options, which, subject to demand, we plan to make available to Grade 11 students next year:

Group 1: Studies in Language and Literature – English or other Mother Tongue Language focusing on Language and Literature Study, HL and SL.
Group 2: Language Acquisition – French, and Spanish B subjects providing a foreign language course for students with a number of years of previous experience in a language. HL and SL
or Spanish and Mandarin Ab Initio – providing a language for students with little experience of learning. SL
Group 3: Study of Individuals in Society - History and Geography HL and SL.
Group 4: Experimental Sciences – Biology HL and SL and Environmental Systems SL
Group 5: Mathematics – SL and Studies (SL)
Group 6: Electives/Arts – Visual Arts and Performing Arts HL and SL or a second subject from one of the others five groups.

In addition to six subjects, students must successfully complete the three elements of the core: an Extended Essay (EE) of around 4000 words, a course in Theory of Knowledge (TOK), and a programme of activities entitled Creativity, Action and Service (CAS).

Grades are awarded for each of the six subjects on a scale of 1 (lowest) to 7 (highest), up to three further points can be awarded for the combined results of TOK and EE.

The maximum number of points that a students can be awarded is 45. The Diploma is awarded to those who achieve at least 24 points, subject to certain minimum level of performance across the whole programme and a satisfactory complexion of CAS.

References:

  • Mary Hyden and Jeff Thompson Taking the Diploma Programme Forward, A John Catt Publication, 2011.
11. University Preparation
Many thanks to Jason Smith of UES London who made a very useful presentation to Grade 9 and 10 students and parents last week on the North American College admissions process and the standardized ACTs & SAT tests used for applications.

This talk was offered as part of Halcyon’s commitment to providing a comprehensive university counselling service, which, going forward, will provide students and families with information and support in applying to universities around the world.

As an early preparation for applying for US universities it is common for students around the world to be entered for PSSS (Premlinary SAT Scoring Service). In line with this, we will be offering current Grade 10 students the possibility of sitting these tests at Halcyon early in 2015.

This test is regarded as excellent practice for the SAT; educators are able to review the questions and answers with students after the test and students with diagnosed learning disabilities can experiment with accommodations such as time extensions.

In addition to the PSSS, Halcyon students in Grade 10 and Grade 11 will have the opportunity to sit the PSAT in October 2015 here at school. The PSAT allows for application to the National Merit Scholarship Program for qualified students.
