6. Information about Grade 10 end of year exams
In Grade 10, the final year of the IBMYP, students will take examinations in English, Language Acquisition, Humanities, Science and Mathematics. These exams will cover the years work in each course. The exams will take place in regularly scheduled class time between 28 May and 1 June.

The exams are an opportunity for students to practice revision and exam-taking skills as well as to consolidate subject knowledge. Teachers in each subject will provided structured revision in class and in homework assignments in preparation for the exams. There will be no additional assessments or homework in the exam week.

Exams will be assessed on one or more IBMYP criteria in the same way as other tests. The results will contribute to the final grades on the third Term report card in those criteria which are assessed.

Accommodations will be offered to students with a documented need from an educational psychologist.

It is important to note that the exams are just one of several assessments that will contribute to the final IBMYP grades. However, it is very important that students make the most of the opportunity to practice revision and examination skills. Students have made a revision schedule and we hope parents will encourage and support their child in practicing good study habits over the next few weeks.

8. Standing Desk 
This week, Halcyon received its first standing desk. Standing desks have been shown to help students stay more active during the day, decrease the health risks of sitting, which can include diabetes and colon cancer, and increase student focus and productivity. In order to trial them for our school, we have ordered three desks, the first of which arrived this past week. It is located in the Humanities classroom, S02, where it has been well received. “They make me feel more focused and engaged,” says a Grade 9 student who used the desk on the first day. “Every class I’ve got more than one student wanting to use it…I’ve noticed that students who use it are more focused and fidget less,” says Ms Cooke, the Humanities teacher, “I’d welcome a couple more!” Two more standing desks are arriving in the month. 
by Nate, Grade 9

For questions or comments, email hello@halcyonschool.com and we’ll pass your message on to Nate.

    13. What do lawyers do?
    During lunchtime on Thursday last week, students had the opportunity to hear from guest speaker, Simon Camilleri. Mr Camilleri is a lawyer (solicitor), working as a corporate litigator for a US firm in London. Mr Camilleri gave a very fascinating speech about what lawyers do, the different types of lawyers, what types of things solicitors can specialise in and what it is like to work as a solicitor. Having attended King’s College, London and University of Oxford (St Edmund Hall), students were also interested in hearing about his experience applying to study Law at these institutions. The talk was very interesting and Halcyon students would like to thank Mr Camilleri for his informative presentation and Q&A session.
