5. IB World School Status
We are very excited to announce that Halcyon London International School is now an International Baccalaureate World School!

In the last few days, we have received notification that we have been fully authorised as an IB Middle Years Programme and an IB Diploma Programme school. This is a very important achievement, of which everyone in our community should be proud. Following the recent Verification Visits, the IB informed us that we comply with all required standards and practices and and commended us for many elements of our programme. Special congratulations to our Programme Coordinators, Lori Fritz and Noelia Zago, who led us so expertly through the authorisation process.

We now look forward to building on this achievement and continuing to develop our outstanding educational provision, as a member of the IB World School community.

          15. Grade 6 at The British Museum
          Grade 6 went exploring in the British Museum this week, discovering what different types of cultural masks were used for, where they came from and what they were made out of. The students collected this information after having looked at Inuit, Sri Lankan and African Masks and have used this knowledge to give them an understanding of the purpose of traditional masks and how they can change a performer’s identity. Grade 7 plan to create their own masks based on a story in English and make connections to the wonderful patterns, textures and shapes they saw in the museum.
            16. Grade 8 at Tate Modern
            Grade 8 went on an exploratory visit to the Tate Modern to investigate the strange characteristics that the Surrealist artists used in order to express their sub-conscious. The students discovered the element of surprise, objects in strange situations, and dream-like fantasy images in the various paintings within the Poetry and Dream Gallery. Grade 8 plan on making connections to these artists through their own paintings, based on dreams.
