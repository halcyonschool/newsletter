1. Halcyon Bursary Programme to start for Sept 2015
Halcyon is delighted to announce that we have secured funding to begin our IBDP Bursary Programme commencing September 2015. We wanted to let our own community know first as we are about to go live and publicise this exciting news.

It has been the school’s goal to broaden access for motivated, talented students wishing to benefit from an IB Diploma Programme education who require financial assistance. Thanks to a generous “seed” donation we can begin with a few partial bursaries (up to 50% support of fees) in the IBDP for new students who wish to apply for next year.

Here are some of the many reasons the Halcyon Bursary Programme is very important for the school:
  • Broaden access
  • Further diversify our community
  • Fulfil one of Halcyon’s charitable goals
  • Be true to the IB philosophy and Halcyon's mission to support community.
How the Halcyon community can help make our Bursary Programme a great success:
  1. Contact Rita Halbright if you would like to get involved in fundraising efforts as we would like to put together an active parent committee to support the programme
  2. Contact Mia Rosengren if you have a new student/family in mind
  3. Tell any friends/colleagues you think may be interested in applying to contact Mia - students sitting their GCSEs this year or born between Sept 98-Aug 99 are eligible
  4. Tell Rita about any friends/colleagues you think may be interested in donating to support Halcyon’s bursary programme.
Please look for more information here as the Halcyon Bursary Programme grows.

3. Security at Halcyon
Security of Halcyon’s students and staff has always been our top priority since founding the school. Following last week's tragic events in Paris, and the continued high threat level in the UK, we have reviewed our security procedures with our landlord. ​Halcyon has a strong security profile with two trained guards on duty during school hours; there are more than 20 monitored CCTV cameras around the building; and through our landlord we work closely with a highly regarded security consultancy, CST. Additionally, we have met this week with a US Embassy Security Advisor to review our security measures. They will be observing the school in the next several days at the busier times to offer any recommendations and observations. We also receive updates on any new developments from our landlord, CST and the US Embassy. 

Please contact Duncan if you have any questions.
4. 
Halcyon attends IBSCA Conference
Halcyon takes every opportunity to connect with the IB community. For this important reason last week our leadership team joined the IBSCA conference in London. IBSCA (IB Schools and Colleges Association) is one of the Associations of IB World Schools that offers support and advice to IB schools in the UK and Ireland. On this occasion, Duncan Partridge, our Head, Lori Fritz our IBMYP Co-ordinator, and Noelia Zago, our IBDP Co-ordinator, attended the event and met other schools’ administrators and IB co-ordinators to discuss the implementation of the IBMYP “Next Chapter” (or new IBMYP curriculum). It was a day of sharing experiences and good practices with representatives from many different types of IB schools.

    Upcoming Trip:
    Grade 6 to visit one of England's greatest treasures

    Date: Wednesday 21 January
    Depart: 0930
    Return: 1535

    Location: Highgate Cemetery

    Grade 6 are currently in the throes of an exciting English and Design inter-disciplinary unit on the Gothic genre. Additionally, they have just begun learning about mapping in Geography and will be given an opportunity to apply these skills in an upcoming Field Trip to Highgate Cemetery.

    Highgate Cemetery is considered one of England's "greatest treasures", providing students with an opportunity to explore Victorian architecture, Gothic-style catacombs, and an abundance of natural forest.

    Additionally, the West Cemetery, which is only accessible through special guided tours, will be used as our interactive mapping ground where students will plan, refine, and map areas using special tools. We will also be given a private tour of the cemetery by one of Highgate's school group specialists.

    The East Cemetery will give our students a chance to delve into the lives of some very famous figures who are buried there such as Karl Marx, George Eliot and Patrick Caulfield.

    The learning outcomes of this trip include creative writing (particularly using natural surroundings to develop a setting), identifying and modeling design choices (focusing on the materials, colours, and style of Gothic/Victorian architecture), and refining mapping skills.

    Students must bring:
    • Topped-up Oyster card
    • A warm, waterproof jacket
    • Comfortable walking shoes
    • Fully charged iPad.
    A packed snack, lunch, and water will be provided by Halcyon.

    Trip Leader: Michelle Jones
    Chaperones: Lenny Dutton, Stephanie Cooke.
