    6. REMINDER: mark your calendar - June Residential Trip for all Grades to Loch Eil, Scotland 
    Date: 08-12 June

    In June, our students will be travelling to the gateway to the last wilderness in the UK. Students will be crossing the border to the North and venturing to where the Romans would not dare!

    Students will be developing their IB core and addressing the key IB areas of communication, holistic learning and intercultural awareness. The Outward Bound Centre in Loch Eil, Scotland, was founded by Kurt Hahn one of the original founders of the IB. Hahn's main educational philosophies were based around the individual as a learner and the development of their civic responsibilities; he wanted active individuals “who felt responsible for the welfare and progress of society” (Knoll, 2011). As such our trip will concentrate on developing areas of the IB Learner Profile. The students will get to grips with the environment around them and participate in a range of challenging activities such as hiking, rock climbing, kayaking and environmental work.

    Please note that these residential field trips are an integral part of our holistic curriculum and that all students are required to attend.

        12. Grade 6 Live Author Skype
        Last Thursday, the Grade 6 classes enjoyed an hour-long Live Author Skype session with two prominent British Authors, David Almond (Author of the "Skellig" series) and Annabel Pitcher (Author of "My Sister Lives on the Mantelpiece").

        Both authors spoke about their inspirations and respective texts, shared tips with our aspiring authors, and promoted their new books set for release in the summer. Additionally, our students were able to pose questions to both authors such as "Why did you decide to write in the first person?" and hear them respond live.

        Halcyon has purchased copies of "Skelling: Book One" and "My Sister Lives on the Mantelpiece" for students to enjoy.
          13. Grade 7 at the National Gallery
          On Monday, Grade 7 spent the morning at the National Gallery looking at the Impressionist and Post Impressionist painters discovering how they had used a Scientific Theory for colour. This gave the students an opportunity to analyse the paintings and seek out the characteristics of these Revolutionary painters. The students will be able to use their knowledge for their presentations in which they will explore the connections between Scientific models and artistic representations of light and colour.
            14. Grades 9 & 10 French Trip
            The Grades 9 & 10 French students attended an interesting and enjoyable activity day at BFI last week. In the morning, students participated in language tasks related to several short films in french on the themes of immigration, romance and family life in Paris. After lunch, students watched 'Les Intouchables' in French with English subtitles. The film, based on real life events, follows the relationship between a wealthy paralysed aristocrat living in Paris and his flamboyant assistant. This is the most successful non-english speaking film at the box office ever taking in more than $280M across the world. Upon return to Halcyon, students completed a written piece of work including a summary of the main events, what they learned and what their own impressions were of the film.
