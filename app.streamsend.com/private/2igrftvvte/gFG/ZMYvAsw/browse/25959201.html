    ISA Testing for MYP students
    0900 - 1220 Wednesday 10 February, ISA Tests Part 1, Grades 6 - 10
    0905 - 1135 Thursday 11 February, ISA Tests Part 2, Grades 6-10
    1135 - 1230 Thursday 11 February, ISA Science Tests, Grades 7-10

    The International Schools' Assessment (ISA) tests are standardised tests of reading, writing, mathematical literacy and scientific literacy developed by the Australian Council for Education in Research (ACER). They were developed with the international student in mind and strive to be free of cultural bias. At Halcyon, we will use the results to identify any particular strengths or weaknesses of our students in those areas which are tested. The results are just one of the tools we will use to help personalise our curriculum in response to student needs. You will get a report on your child's results, but the tests do not contribute to students' IBMYP term grades and are not included in transcripts.

    Students in Grades 6-10 will take the reading, writing and math tests on the mornings of Wednesday 10 February and Thursday 11 February. Students in Grades 7-10 will also take the scientific literacy test on 11 February. Students will trial a sample of the tests during some lessons in the week of 01 - 05 February to get accustomed to the online format and style of the questions. These practice tests are not marked and do not contribute to the final results.

    The ISA tests are online tests that can be accessed on an iPad or on a laptop. Students do not need to bring any special materials but will need a fully charged device (iPad of laptop) and may like to use a keyboard with their iPad. Students whose first language is not English may use a translating dictionary and should bring a paper dictionary to the test as they will not be able to access electronic dictionaries.

    Each test lasts about 45 minutes but the tests are not time limited - students may continue working past the allotted time if they need. Students who qualify for extra time in examinations will be encouraged to use the time they need. There are breaks between each test and students will be provided with healthy snacks and drinks to refresh themselves during the breaks.

    Students should bring:
    • a fully charged device (iPad or laptop)
    • filled water bottle
    If you have any questions please email Lori Fritz, MYP Coordinator.

    Raising Bi-lingual Children
    Date: Tuesday 23 February
    Time: 1030-1230
    Location: 13 Prince of Wales Terrace, W8 5PG
    Free for FOCUS members, £25 for non-members, £15 for Halcyon parents
    Is your child bilingual or trilingual? What languages will your child need for their studies, for their work life and for their family life? Are you and your child’s school supporting your child's home language and culture? Is your child mixing English and your home language, or refusing to speak in your home language? In today’s world bilingualism is a highly regarded asset. In this workshop Susan Stewart of International School of London will share her knowledge on how to ensure children make the most of their bilingualism now and throughout their life. She will talk about some dos and don’ts and what you, as a parent, can practically do today that will have a positive future impact on your child's language development.
