1. Halcyon Community Message
Dear Parents, Students and Colleagues, 

We would like to wish you all 'happy holidays!' and hope you enjoy your break.

It has been a successful start to the year, something we hope was evidenced this week during our student-led showcase. It was hugely encouraging to see so many parents and students engaged in learning conversations, and to see the range of different media involved in everyone's learning. I heard podcasts, saw essays, read through some great displays, heard conversations about digital building design, had students show me some impressive presentations, and was delighted by the vibrance and variety of our students' art projects. This was only a sample of the experience, and we hope that your afternoon was equally enriching. Many thanks to our HPC who supported us by providing some excellent food! It was a great community event, and we would like to thank everyone for their support.

Of course, to keep improving our services and to ensure that we always focus on improving student learning, it's good to have your feedback. We would be grateful if you could take a moment to complete our feedback questionnaire.

When we return after the half-term break, Kerry Jenkins will have begun her maternity leave. We would like to wish her the very best and we look forward to hearing her news over the coming weeks. To cover Kerry's classes, we are delighted to have Fahima Khan join Halcyon. Fahima will continue the curriculum students have been given, and will take classes through the spring break. I am sure we will all join in welcoming Fahima to our learning community. 

As you may have noticed on our calendar, the school has secured the services of Linda Magnussen to provide university guidance counselling for this academic year. Linda has many years experience in this field and will be meeting with students and parents from Grades 9 - 11 on Tuesday 10 and Wednesday 11 November. We are delighted about this partnership with Linda, and are sure the students will benefit enormously from her advice. 

Before I close, we would like to end on a note of school policy, and remind everyone that we do not authorise leave for the purpose of making an early start to the holiday. Ahead of this half-term break, we had quite a few students absent on the final day and we really do hope that we will see an improvement in attendance over the year ahead. The school calendar can be seen here and I kindly ask that parents take a moment to review this and ensure that their travel plans do not conflict with instructional days. Thank you for your understanding and support. 

Have a great half-term break!

Best wishes,

Barry Mansfield

2. Halcyon Exchange Programme
Halcyon is committed to supporting globally-minded lifelong learners who are able to speak more than one language with proficiency. Therefore we are pleased to launch an Exchange Programme this year with St. Ignatius School in Argentina. The first step of this programme will be receiving Paula Cendoya in January (Grade 10 student). Paula will be part of our community and therefore we are looking for a Halcyon family to host Paula from Thursday 07 January to Wednesday 10 February 2016. 

You can learn more about the benefits of hosting an exchange student and the requirements as well as details for Halcyon students wishing to apply for the exchange to Argentina in August 2016 here. Please contact Fatima Gonzalez, Exchange Coordinator, if you require further information.

We are delighted to welcome Laura Olmedo to the Halcyon team as our newly appointed Receptionist. 5. Royal Academy of Arts Trip
On Tuesday 20 October, Grade 10 and 11 Mandarin students visited the Royal Academy of Arts to look at Ai Weiwei's exhibition. The visit linked with this year's Halcyon Student Showcase where we were focusing on the Learner Profile and how inspiring individuals can make a difference through embodying Learner Profile values. During this trip we explored how Ai Weiwei communicates his opinions in different forms of work.

New Halcyon Bookclub!
Date: Tuesday 10 November
Time: 1400
Location: Borough Barista (downstairs), 60A Seymour Street
Keen and not-so-keen(!) readers are invited to join the new Halcyon Book Club beginning next month. Our theme this year will be UK authors from any era with our first book chosen to be Almost English by Charlotte Mendelson. Listed for the Booker Prize in 2013, it is a story of living in West London mixed with the complications of family, education and social connections. Sound familiar? Should be good!

Please join our club for 'Halcyon Parents + Friends' and we ask only that we all come with the book in hand or on tablet, have made a good attempt to read it as much as possible and that we generously share our opinions over a cup of coffee!

Almost English is readily available at the moment and can be found on Amazon paperback for as little as £0.43 or for Kindle Download. Order it, enjoy the read over half term and we look forward to seeing you on Tuesday 10 November. Sign-up is not necessary but is helpful so that we know how many to expect. Any questions please contact Risa Kay or Cathy Smith.

Tours and Classes

Digital Photography and The Selfie
Date: Tuesday 10 November
Time: 0945 - 1200
Location: Art Workers Guild, 6 Queen Square, WC1N 3AT
Cost: £25 per person (incl coffee and pastries)
The London Art Salon is offering a lecture on the invention and rise of self-portraiture from humble beginnings as hidden painted faces to the obsessive documenting and displaying of our own digital images. While the "selfie" is being billed as a massive contemporary phenomenon, photographers did not wait for the invention of digital smart phones or selfie sticks in order to turn the camera on themselves. The London Art Salon brings together expert speakers in a series of talks and discussion that cover London's current art exhibitions, events and art market. Registration is essential here. For any questions, please contact Anja Beck.

City of London Architecture Tour
Date: Wednesday 18 November
Time: 1030 - 1230
Meeting Place: One New Change EC4M 9AF
Cost: £30 plus £1.26 surcharge
This is an amazing tour with the former head of planning for the City of London. On our walk around the City of London, we will look first at the immediate setting of St Paul's Cathedral and its transformation over recent decades. Our route will continue via Cheapside, the City's revitalised High Street, towards Bank and the Royal Exchange. The tour will continue through the medieval alleyways off Cornhill and take in Leadenhall Market and the Lloyds Building before visiting the cluster of new office towers which house London's insurance sector. Our walk will then turn north to visit Broadgate and Liverpool Station, finishing on the northeastern corner of the City adjacent to Spitalfields. This tour is organised by From My City. Please sign-up directly on this page for Halcyon so that we can keep track of who's coming. For any questions, please contact Stacey Ciauri.
