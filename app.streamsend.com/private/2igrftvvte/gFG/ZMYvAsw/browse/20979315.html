1. Term 2 Reports & ISA Test Results
Second term reports will be available to view in Managebac from Friday 18 April. Parents will receive an email reminder when reports are published. The reports will show student achievement against the subject criteria which have been assessed in the second term. Students in Grades 8 and 9 will also get a progress grade on a scale of 1 to 7 indicating their overall achievement level in each subject so far this academic year. Parents are encouraged to look in Managebac to see teacher comments and grades on the specific pieces of work which have contributed to the criterion grades. The reports will also include a link to a student self-reflection on their progress.

At the same time, parents will receive their child's ISA test results. The ISA tests are standardised tests in English and mathematics that were taken by students in Term 1. The tests give parents an indication of their child's general mathematics and English language skills relative to other students internationally. The results of these tests are one tool used at Halcyon to help teachers identify areas of strength or weakness so that they can plan curricula to best meet the needs of our students who have come from varied educational backgrounds.

4. Hyde Park 
Reminder for Grades 6&7
Fit for Sport will be coaching athletics in PE sessions. After the Easter break, Grades 6 & 7 will be starting weekly athletics coaching sessions in Hyde Park with Fit for Sport. Grade 6 will participate during periods 3 & 4 each Monday and Grade 7 will participate during periods 3 & 4 each Thursday.

This will not only enable the students to benefit from exercise in the open air in Hyde Park, but will also give them an opportunity to improve their athletics skills and performance in activities such as sprints, hurdles, long jump, shot put and relays.

It is important that all students bring an additional pair of outdoor shoes to school with them on the day they have athletics in the the park, as they will be required to change into their other shoes when returning to school. It is also advisable for students to bring a water bottle and to put on sun cream on the morning of the day that they have athletics in the park.

This new and exciting partnership with Fit for Sport will go some way to preparing students for the first Halcyon Sports Day event which will take place in June.

Grades 8 & 9 will also be given an opportunity to participate in athletics in Hyde Park during their PE sessions in June and we will provide more information shortly.

Should you have any questions or comments please feel free to email Oisin at: pe@halcyonschool.com

6. Bridging the Global Skills Gap 
As Halcyon sees every member of its community as lifelong learners, you may find this research interesting. Our students are considered members of Generation G, referring to Global, Gaming or Generous. To paraphrase, they are considered digital natives, globally-connected and confident users of technology; consumers who have a heightened awareness when it comes to sustainability and ethical consumption. 8. Science Museum Trip
Grade 8 enjoyed the Hadron Collider experience at the Science Museum. The experience recreated the feeling of being inside the CERN facility as the news was announced that the Higgs Boson, the missing element crucial to the working of the Standard Model, had been found. This means scientists are now much more sure about the way the universe came into being. We then walked through an exhibition which showed the enormous scale and cost of the facility and how the machinery all works. We also gained insight into the rivalry during the race to find the Higgs Boson. We then went on to observe the industrial revolution machinery that the students have been studying in Humanities. Most of the students were able to locate the original machines that they had pitched as part of their Dragons' Den assessment.

The picture above shows the Bessemer Converter which was a cheap way to produce steel and revolutionised engineering and construction.

10. Poetry Games - Our Students' Poems Published!
Recently, Halcyon entered four students’ poems in the Young Writers' "Poetry Games" competition. We are delighted to announce that all four of our students were chosen to be published in the upcoming "The Poetry Games - London Voices" book which will be sent to the British Library and further libraries across the UK and Republic of Ireland.

In her letter to the school, the editor of the Young Writers Books, Allie Jones, commented on the "perception, imagination, expression, and creative use of language" of our students poems.

Congratulations to Tanguy Guerlais (Grade 7), Siri Cervin (Grade 6), Kate Crannell (Grade 6), and Noah Melman (Grade 6) on this outstanding achievement! Your Halcyon Community celebrates with you!
