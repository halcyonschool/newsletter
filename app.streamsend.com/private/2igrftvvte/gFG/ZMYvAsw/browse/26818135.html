        MUN Conference - 09 and 10 June
        Halcyon Model United Nations (MUN) is rapidly approaching, and we hope you are as excited as we are. On Thursday 09 and Friday 10 June, three schools will be joining us at Halcyon’s third annual MUN Conference. We will be debating important and relevant issues such as Universal Healthcare and Regulation of Borders and Immigration.           1. Student Achievements
          Grade 8 Space Mission with the Challenger Learning Centre
          On Wednesday this week, Grade 8 participated in a mock space mission in collaboration with the NASA Learning Centre in Colorado. The mission was the central point of our current Science unit about the sun and sustaining life on their Space Station.

          For the past three months, we have been preparing for the mission. This involved extensive lessons on four major fields: radiation, life support, solar weather and power aboard the station.

          On the day, we had specific jobs to do, and we were divided into five teams: Radiation, Storm and Life Support dealt with the safety of the astronauts; Communication dealt with communicating to the Learning Centre; and Crisis Management were the glue, ensuring that all teams were working to solve the problem at hand - the problem being a very large solar storm that was threatening the lives of the astronauts.

          The result was successful; we managed to ensure there was no loss of life during the storm. However, what is more important than the result, is the fantastic learning experience...it was something that really shows what Halcyon is all about, and that is innovative learning.

          Written by Finn, Grade 8

          3. University and Career Guidance
          Linda Magnussen’s Visit
          For the past year we have been fortunate to have Linda as Halcyon’s University and Careers Counsellor. Linda has supported and advised Halcyon from its early days, even before the first students walked in the door. She has held parent meetings relating to the US and UK university application process and financing, workshops on university application processes for Grades 10 and 11, informal lunch meetings with both grades with Q&A opportunities, individual meetings with Grade 10 and 11 students and parents, an introductory university planning session for Grade 9 students and parents and a workshop for teachers on writing university letters of recommendations.

          As Mark Bowen will be joining our staff this summer, this is Linda’s last visit as Halcyon’s University and Careers Counsellor. She reflected on her experience at Halcyon:

          "Throughout my visits to Halcyon I was always impressed with the engagement of the students and the thoughtful questions they asked. Indeed, they were really perceptive and adept at projecting into the future, in terms of self awareness and where they might see their future and what their goals are. One of the challenges for Grade 10 is to relax a bit and let their individual DP experiences evolve; there are many paths to take to reach each of their destinations. Both Grade 10 and 11 students should continue to explore all of their options. I also thoroughly enjoyed Halcyon’s entrepreneurial spirit, which was exemplified when working with the teachers and interacting with the students. It is a very fresh and individually-centred community."

          Linda also played a key role in our interview process for our own University and Careers Counsellor, Mark. She reported:

          "His tremendous experience that he will bring from the international arena will be demonstrated through his holistic approach when developing his university and careers guidance programme for Halcyon. Mark truly understands the complexities of international students and families choosing where to study."

          We want to thank Linda for all of her hard work and support and hope to see her again in the near future.
