2. School Food Action Committee - volunteers wanted!
Halcyon is pleased to announce the creation of an advisory committee called the School Food Action Committee. In line with the mission and vision of the school, Halcyon expects the standard for health and wellbeing to be very high. We have created a committee that has all members of the Halcyon community represented to take action to promote our Food Policy.

The committee will meet and discuss all aspects of the food culture at school from how to promote it, to how to integrate it with curriculum, to how to maintain a high standard. Recommendations will be made to the Head and action will be taken. We are looking for two parent volunteers to join this group. The commitment is one academic year, meeting twice a Term.

If you are a collaborative, critical thinking team player who loves food and wellbeing this group is for you.

Please submit your name to the HPC by Monday 20 April and the volunteers will be chosen by ballot at the HPC general meeting.

    Halcyon's Student Council reminds you of: International Student ID Cards
    What are student IDs?
    ISIC (International School ID Cards) is a company which distributes IDs for international students. The cards are distributed in over 130 countries for students aged 12 and up. The cards contains a photo, first name, last name and date of birth as well as the school’s name.

    What are the benefits?
    There are many benefits of the ID card. For example, discounts in local restaurants, museums, cinemas, clothes and office supplies shops, bookstores, public transport, and more. They are also good for general ID too.

    If you would like your student to have a card, you can apply by notifying student council through this form here. As this is a student-led initiative, Halcyon's student council will be organizing these directly. Deadline for notifying is Friday 24 April.

    The cost is £7 per card (a £5 saving over doing it yourself). The Halcyon Student Council’s treasurer, Gwen (Grade 10), will be collecting the cash for payment from each advisory group next week.

              When was the last time your child backed up her or his iPad to prevent the loss school work? If or when the unfortunate event of restoring an iOS device occurs it is always reassuring to know that you have been backing up your device to the iCloud so you do not lose your data.

              This week’s post is a practical one about using the iCloud backup feature as a way to ensure your data from all of your iOS and OSX devices is being backed up to the iCloud.

              This is very important for your child's iPad as it is always a frustrating event when they students lose their work. Take a few minutes to check your devices, especially your child's iPad.

              1. Watch this video to learn more about the iCloud and iCloud Drive

              2. Watch this video to learn how to check if your iPad is backing up to the iCloud Drive on iOS 8 operating system.

              Help....I have run out of space in iCloud!
              Sometimes your iPad can't back up because there is not enough space. Learn a variety of solutions to this problem. iCloud offers you 5G of free space, but after that you would need to buy space on iCloud. See this video.

              1. What takes up a lot of space is music, photos and videos. Backing up this much data to a computer is a quick easy fix as shown in the video.
