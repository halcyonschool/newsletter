        1. Student Achievements
        Halcyon’s Student-led Debate Day was a Great Success
        Today we saw very passionate student debaters. Their debating level was considered and thoughtful and everyone was judged on an IB style criteria. We (Ellery and Emily, Grade 10) along with Ms Fritz, were the Debate Day Coordinators. We chose today’s topics from suggestions by Halcyon’s Student Council and staff, from looking at relevant topics in the news and by reviewing current educational topics.

        Round One Topic
        ‘Should Halcyon continue to support Cancer Research UK when knowing they use animal testing?’

        Round Two Topics
        ‘Should university education be free?’
        ‘Should homework at halcyon be banned?’
        ‘Should prisoners get the vote?’
        ‘Should countries with human rights violations be allowed to host international schooling events?’

        Round Three Topics
        ‘Should the internet be censored?’
        ‘To what extent do violent video games negatively impact the behaviour of young people?’

        Final Round Topic
        ‘To what extent is the death penalty a good punishment?’

        The judges were made up of a selection of teachers (Mr Alcantud, Ms Bartholomew, Mr Burn, Mr Costa, Ms Foster, Mr Gow, Ms Gonzalez, Ms Kahn, Ms Lin, Ms Rose, Ms Shillito and Mr Simpson) and students (Alex, Alexi, Dada, Ellie, Eric, Ellie, Isaac,Kieran and Lucy). The debating teams were made up of mixed grade students from Grades 6-10.

        Congrats to the winning team: Davide, Evy, Lucca and Nathan.

        Well done to all participants for their enthusiastic efforts! Thank you to the judges and Ms Fritz for helping to make today so much fun!

        We are already looking forward to next year's event.

        Thank you,
        Emily and Ellery

        Talented Writers
        The English department is always looking for authentic and exciting experiences to encourage our students to write and be creative. As such, students were encouraged to enter the BBC2 500 words competition. Although none of Halcyon’s entrants reached the final (there were over 150,000 entrants) we are very excited to say that a number of Halcyon’s MYP students who did enter the competition have been asked to take part in a random ballot to win a pair of tickets to the 500 Words Final.

        The Final will be an extravaganza of live music and sensational storytelling. It is taking place at Shakespeare’s Globe on the morning of Friday 27 May. Fingers crossed for our students and well done to all those who entered! Thank you to English teachers Hannah Shillito and Jo Ranson for finding this opportunity and supporting the students during the process.

        Grade 9 Trip to the Imperial War Museum
        Grade 9 visited the Imperial War Museum with Humanities teacher, Stephanie Cooke, and Art teacher, Sue Dance, to explore the different exhibits depicting the Cold War and a fascinating exhibition by an artist, Peter Kennard, giving his perspective on war, including the Cold War through manipulated photographic images and drawings.

        This trip gave the students plenty of source material, which they recorded at the museum, to help them with their ideas towards creating an installation about the Cold War for their interdisciplinary unit on "conflict".

        3. Halcyon Happenings
        New Staff Announcements
        Joanne Cooper, IB MYP/DP Mathematics Teacher

        Hailing from the other side of the globe, Jo grew up and then taught in Sydney for many years. As Head of Mathematics and Acting MYP Coordinator, Jo has implemented and taught the IB MYP/DP for 8 years in Australia. Inspired by the internationalism of the IB, she is currently teaching in Malaysia. Jo has MYP coordinator experience as well.

        Jo maintains that teachers must be ready to adapt the teaching pedagogy to accommodate and inspire 21st century learning. She has a degree in Mathematics, a Graduate Diploma of Education and a Masters in Educational Leadership. She is passionate about giving students the opportunity of working on the visualisation of mathematics through dynamic geometry software.

        Outside of the classroom, Jo is an advocate for social justice and has travelled with student groups through Timor Leste and Peru on immersion programmes.

        Ross McGowan, English Teacher

        Brought up and educated near Glasgow, Ross gained a bachelor degree in English and a Postgraduate Certificate in Education (English) from Strathclyde University. He has been
        teaching in schools and Further Education (FE) colleges for 13 years. His teaching career began in a state school in central Scotland before Ross moved to the Scottish Borders for three years to teach English and Communication. Ross returned to the central belt of Scotland and taught English for seven years in New College Lanarkshire.

        Internationally, Ross has taught in Abu Dhabi and Düsseldorf and has eight years’ IB teaching experience.

        In his spare time, Ross enjoys sport and used to represent Scotland at athletics. He was second best in the country!

        We look forward to welcoming both Jo and Ross to the Halcyon team!

        Grade 10 and 11 Final Exams
        Dates: Friday 20 to Friday 27 May
        In Grades 10 and 11, students sit Halcyon school examinations in English, Language Acquisition/Mother Tongue, Humanities, Sciences and Mathematics subjects. Grade 10 students also do an external Interdisciplinary exam set by the International Baccalaureate.

        The exams are an opportunity to practice revision and exam-taking skills as well as to consolidate subject knowledge. Teachers in each subject will provide structured revision in class and in homework assignments in preparation for the exams. There will be no additional assessments or homework in the exam week. The exam results will be one component of the final report grade at the end of the year. Please find a link to the examination schedule here. If you have any questions please email Lori Fritz.
