        1. Student Achievements
        The UKMT Results are in!
        Halcyon students performed extremely well in very challenging mathematical competitions.

        Around 500 of the highest scorers from previous UKMT competitions are invited to take part in the Intermediate Mathematical Olympiad papers: Cayley, Hamilton and Maclaurin. Out of this national selection two of our students were invited and each received merit certificates for the Maclaurin Olympiad. Well done Eric and Nate!

        A further 5,500 pupils are invited to sit either the grey or pink European Kangaroo papers. Out of this selection, three of Halcyon’s students received qualification certificates. Well done Martina, Mike and Nathan!

        To learn more about the UKMT challenges please visit the UKMT site.


        Young Writers Poetry Competition
        A number of Halcyon students entered the Young Writers' Poetry Trials competition and have been successful, meaning that they will now be published in a special poetry anthology, entitled, 'The Poetry Trials - The South'. This anthology of poetry will be sent to the British Library and a copy will be held with Halcyon's English department.

        The poems were selected based on perception, imagination, expression, creativity and use of language. Our successful young Halcyon writers will have received a letter with bookmark and certificate.

        If your child has received such a letter please congratulate them! The competition continues as the best poet from the anthology is yet to be selected.

        Well done and keep writing!

        2. Adventures in Learning
        French Students’ Trip to the British Film Institute (BFI)
        On Friday 15 April the Grade 10 and 11 French students attended a French film study day at BFI on the Southbank.

        The session included a series of short films on themes such as immigration, social relationships, identifying with local cultural norms and perceptions of beauty. Halcyon students completed the written language reflection tasks well and keenly offered to answer follow-up questions in front of the 250 students packed into the auditorium.

        C'était chantmé quoi!

        Grade 6 Trip to the British Museum
        On Friday 15 April, Grade 6 had a most productive visit with the Art and Drama teachers at the British Museum, investigating the amazing range of traditional masks from the North American, Mexican, Sri Lankan and African sections. This gave the students inspiration for their ideas to create their own expressive masks in Art later this term. These masks will eventually be used to perform with during their Drama lessons. The students used their iPads for annotations and spent much of the time drawing the masks in their art books, focussing on shape, pattern and expression.

        Patrick Ryan Shares Fascinating Stories about Shakespeare

        University of Cambridge, Parent Survey
        Halcyon has recently been contacted by researchers at the University of Cambridge who are carrying out a survey of parents in the UK. The study is very interesting, and we would like to encourage you to participate. The purpose of the study is to gain a better understanding of the support students and their families need during primary and secondary school.         If you would like to obtain any further information about the study, or if you have any other comments, please contact Dr. Teodora Boneva and Dr. Christopher Rauh at parental.survey2016@gmail.com.

        Halcyon’s Mentor Programme and the Cognitive Coaching Seminars, Message from Gareth Jones, Wellbeing Leader
        The first two terms of this year at Halcyon have been exciting!

        As an educator, one of the most rewarding elements of my day is empowering a student to move forward, by doing something they found challenging or did not know they could do. This year I have had the opportunity to develop a system in which we can further support our students in taking on such explorations.

        Next year Halcyon will start a new way of structuring our advisory time. Halcyon will still have vertically integrated groups, but they will meet in smaller groups of eight, twice a week for one hour. These will be known as mentor groups. During this time students will also have one-to-one cognitive coaching conversations with their mentor.

        To support the mentoring programme at Halcyon, every teacher will be trained as a cognitive coach. This method of conversation is about empowering the mentee to set self directed goals and take ownership of their learning. It allows for regular structured reflection to monitor how the student is doing and, when necessary, enables students to modify these goals. Our staff will participate in the Cognitive Coaching Foundation Workshop Part I, for four days in August with follow up workshops planned for the future.

        These conversations will support development in all areas of student learning, making Halcyon an even more effective and individualised learning environment, helping to unleash our unique potential.

        If you know of any teachers outside of the Halcyon community who would like to develop these skills, please let them know they can take part in the cognitive coaching training at Halcyon. More details can be found here.

        Become a Minecraft Savvy Parent, Message from Halcyon’s Digital Learning Leader
        Are your kids playing Minecraft? Mine are and I have been trying to find ways to get involved so I too can understand the attraction to this game and play along with them. One of the pages that lands in my Facebook feed is Common Sense Media. Recently they posted on their Facebook page a Minecraft resource for parents called ‘Minecraft: A Glossary for Parents’. I found this so helpful in gaining an understanding and language around the game when I first played because now I can use this as a way to have a common language with my child when he or she is playing the game with me.
