      Dina’s Guest Speaker Programme
      The Guest Speaker Programme is a CAS project that I am running with the help of Ms Cooke. Last year, Ms Cooke invited people to give talks to Halcyon students. We had some wonderful speakers talk about their experience of World War Two and of the reality of the internet, just to name a couple. When the opportunity arose for the project to start again, I thought it would be great if I could run it.

      The project aims to invite people to Halcyon and for them to give talks. What the speakers give talks on is partly up to the students, staff and parents that undertake the survey because it asks what you would like to hear. When the forms are complete and I know who to invite, there will be a one hour session on one day of the week. Speakers can return to address other topics, due to demand or choose a different format other than give a talk if they please.

      The first speaker is Ella Zimina who will be giving a talk of drawing the human figure, the shadows and how to do a quick sketch. She will be running the workshop for four weeks after which we will have a presentation of the work that the participants have achieved. Ella is a parent at the school. Details on her workshop to follow soon. In the meantime, if you are interested in this topic please complete this questionnaire.

      If you wish to give a talk on something you feel passionately about and think the students will want to hear, please email me and I will do my best to arrange a time for you to come in. Please also take 5 minutes to complete this form to allow us to continue this project.

      Thanks,
      Dina, Grade 11

      5. School Updates and Reminders
      Oh No! My iPad or mobile phone screen is broken!
      When a mobile phone or iPad screen cracks, it is compromised. This means that it does not take much for the rest of the screen to shatter into hundreds of pieces. The screen is made out of glass and it can cut you, possibly glass can get embedded under your skin.

      Once cracked, it is time to get it repaired and it should not be used or brought to school again until it is fixed.
