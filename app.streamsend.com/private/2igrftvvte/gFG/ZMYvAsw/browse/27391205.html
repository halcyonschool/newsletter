        3. Adventures in Learning
        Scavenger Hunt
        On the first day of term, Halcyon students took part in the annual scavenger hunt. The scavenger hunt enables students to spend a prolonged period of time with their mentors and mentor groups facilitating team building. Each year the hunt has a theme and this year we explored the 'Wonder of Hyde Park'. The scavenger hunt was split into two projects. One was searching for clues in the park, such as famous landmarks, and the other was making a promotional video to advertise the park to Halcyon Students.

        Dinis Costa’s mentor group managed to gain the most points for finding all of the clues for the first part of the project. Daphni in Grade 12 said:

        “It was fun to meet the new students and all work together in our annual scavenger hunt!"

        The video project was chosen by the team as a way to enhance students’ life-readying skills by learning how to create an engaging digital publication through team collaboration. The mentoring groups were given five guidelines they had to follow in order to create a publishable video. The guidelines were:
        • Inclusivity: students needed to ensure they worked as a team and that everyone had a role to play in the creation of the video
        • Audio: the sound had to be clear and any music used had to be licence free
        • Focus: the subjects had to be well lit and in focus
        • Legal: only Halcyon students could feature in the video
        • In context: the video needed to showcase their learning project which was to find the ‘Wonders of Hyde Park’
        The groups used video, still images, typography, different editing techniques and music apps to create their videos. Chloe McBean’s mentor group won the video project, for adhering best to the above guidelines. View the video here! Claire, in Grade 11, said on behalf of this mentor group that:

        “The Annual Halcyon Scavenger Hunt was a great way to make new friends, get to know your mentor group, and learn about all of the great things you can do in Hyde Park.”

        However, the decision for the winning video was an extremely difficult one as they were all fun and engaging collaborative pieces! A big well done to everyone for their great efforts.
        4. Model United Nations Conference in The Hague (MUNISH)
        Invitation for Grades 9 - 12 to attend
        Model United Nations Conference in The Hague (MUNISH)
        03 November - 06 November 2016
        To all budding politicians, humanitarians and those interested in current events!

        After the success of last year’s trip to MUNISH, Halcyon students will have the opportunity to attend this international conference this Autumn.

        Halcyon students will have the privilege of representing the nations of Sudan and Mexico, as well as the Palestinian National Authority and Greenpeace, and will debate in a range of United Nations committees with students from all over the world. The conference theme for this year is ‘Growth and Accountability’ and you can find out more on the MUNISH website.

        Our group will travel to The Hague on Thursday 03 November and attend the conference 04 November - 06 November, returning on the evening of Sunday 06 November.

        The cost of the trip is estimated at approximately £450-500 per student and this includes all conference fees, transport and accommodation. Additional spending money will be required. Please note that due to the short time before the conference, it will be payable in one instalment.

        The MUNISH conference is available to all students in Grades 9 - 12, though should any students in Grade 7 or 8 express an interest, please feel free to discuss this with me. The school has a minimum of 9 and maximum of 22 delegate places (in addition to the 2 confirmed Chairs) and we wish to fill and confirm all places by Wednesday 08 September.

        To secure your child’s place, the school will need to have parental consent for participation, and a commitment to meet the published costs (£450-500). If you would like your child to travel to The Hague, please use this Google Form to confirm a place on the trip. Please be aware that the form includes a commitment to pay the full amount, which covers MUNISH registration fees, accommodation, and travel. Upon receipt of parent confirmations we will book accommodation and travel. Therefore, once you submit the form, you can expect to receive an invoice for the trip. Should more than 22 students respond, priority will be given first to experienced MUN delegates, and second to Grades 10-11. We welcome younger students but also consider that they will have opportunities to travel in the future.

        Students will be required to have a passport with valid visas that do not expire within the next 6 months.

        In addition to this conference, students will have the opportunity to attend other MUN and GIN conferences, later in the academic year.

        Thank you for your support for our learning community.

        We look forward to your responses and if you have any questions please contact me.

        Kind regards
        Claire Rose
        crose@halcyonschool.com
