        Halcyon Selected for NEASC Accreditation
        Dear Halcyon Community,

        We are delighted to share with you that following a very successful Eligibility Visit in February 2016 the New England Association of Schools and Colleges (NEASC) / Commission on International Education (CIE) has accepted Halcyon’s application to enter their accreditation process.

        The NEASC’s aim is to improve education through accreditation. NEASC is acknowledged globally as a standard of excellence, attesting to a school’s high quality and integrity. NEASC is a non-profit membership organisation which connects and serves over 2,000 schools, technical / career institutions, colleges and universities in New England plus International Schools in more than 65 nations.

        As part of this acceptance, Halcyon has been invited to apply for the innovative ACE Learning Principles Review. ACE challenges the familiar language and ‘grammar’ of schooling and encourages schools to become learning communities guided by a vision of learning in our times. ACE serves as a portal to a global network of learning communities. Indeed, ACE asks learning communities to reflect on learner impacts rather than outputs and to identify the evidence required to validate the desired impact. We are particularly excited to be invited to complete the ACE Review as it aligns well with Halcyon’s ethos.

        What does this say about Halcyon?
        The Commission (NEASC) was motivated to accept Halcyon’s application, after making the following observations:
        • The overall standard of teaching and learning observed reflects quality leadership, risk taking, strong planning, collaboration, a commitment to professional training and pro-active teacher recruitment of professionals with prior IB experience
        • There is a positive learning environment that is inclusive, demanding and yet very nurturing
        • The teaching pedagogy is consistent with a quality IB World School
        • The integration of instructional technology is a key aspect of the learning environment
        • Halcyon has established modern and well-designed teaching facilities that are secure, well-maintained and clean
        • Halcyon actively recruits diverse, self-directed learners and provides a learning environment that is different
        • Halcyon is a learning community that aspires to be innovative, diverse and transformational

        The NEASC / CIE offers Halcyon additional recognition with US and Canadian universities and the ability to offer a US high school diploma.

        This is another exciting step in Halcyon’s development and we look forward to keeping you posted on what will most likely be a year-long accreditation process. For more information, please see the NEASC website or feel free to contact Barry Mansfield.

        Best regards,
        Achim Beck (Chair of the Board) and Barry Mansfield (Director)
        2. Student Achievements
        Watching Grade 6 Chicks Hatch
        This has been an exciting investigation of living egg development by Grade 6 students. We received 10 eggs earlier in the week and have been watching our new chicks join our world. The students have been studying the development of chicks by collecting and analysing data from hatching until they around eight days old. We have been live streaming the chick's development all this week and now the chicks have gone home for the weekend to be cared for by our students.

        3. Adventures in Learning
        Grade 10 Visit the Serpentine Gallery
        Grade 10 students visited the Serpentine this week, spending time exploring the two different exhibitions, making connections to the themes they are studying in "Streetcar Named Desire" during the English class. Art Students had the opportunity to discover the shapes, themes and symbols used by the artists, as an inspiration for their own artworks. The students were able to see the influences within the two shows and how the artists used different types of materials to express themselves. Back at school, students will be able to develop these themes further both in English and in Art, finding ways to express themselves creatively with words and with colour and image.

        Celebrating Shakespeare's 400th Anniversary
        Storyteller Patrick Ryan visiting Halcyon
        Date: Tuesday 19 April
        Time: 0900-1400
        Location: Halcyon
        April 2016 marks the 400th anniversary of William Shakespeare’s death. Halcyon will celebrate the legacy of Shakespeare during the quatercentenary year by hosting professional storyteller Patrick Ryan who will spend the day telling tales and sharing his love and knowledge of the Bard.

        In addition to being a wonderful storyteller, Patrick is the author of ‘Shakespeare's Storybook: Folk Tales that Inspired the Bard’, a beautiful picture book where he has retold the origin tales behind some of Shakespeare's plays.

        The schedule for his visit is:
        • 0900-0945 - storytelling in the Stern Hall for Grades 6 - 10, with Halcyon parents and family welcome to join. Parents who would like to attend please sign-up here
        • 0945-1030 - Grade 6 hearing Shakespeare's stories
        • 1050-1135 - Grade 8 hearing a Midsummer's Night Dream
        • 1135-1220 - Grade 9 hearing the Taming of the Shrew
        • 1220-1400 - Grade 11 will have lunch with Patrick and take part in a workshop on The Tempest
        Please note that Tuesday 19 April is a half-day for students who (except for Grade 11) will be released at 1220.

        Cognitive Coaching Seminars
        Halcyon is hosting a Cognitive Coaching Foundation Seminar Part I, on 15-18 Aug 2016. The mission of Cognitive Coaching is to produce self-directed persons with the cognitive capacity for excellence both independently and as members of a community. Research indicates that teaching is a complex intellectual activity and that teachers who think at higher levels produce students who are higher achieving, more cooperative, and better problem solvers. It is the invisible skills of teaching, the thinking processes that underlie instructional decisions, which produce superior instruction. Cognitive Coaching is a research-based model that capitalises upon and enhances teachers’ cognitive processes.

        Halcyon’s teachers will be taking part in this seminar. If you know any teachers who might be interested in taking part please send them this link.
