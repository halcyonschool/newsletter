              Extracurricular Activity Call for Sign-up
              Club: Mindfulness
              Time: 0800-0850
              Cost: £100 per person for 10 sessions
              A practice of greater regard for self and others. Nothing to strive for, no goal to be met, just completely aware of the moment.

              Mindfulness could be defined as paying attention to the ‘Moment by Moment experience in a non-judgemental way'. During the class students will experience a calming ambience as they learn how to observe the ’Breath' or 'Sound', both act as an anchor in bringing you to the present moment. This is what Mindfulness is about, learning how to be 'present', as our minds are ALWAYS either in the past or in the future! Surprising, but true, the past is often associated with regret and the future is associated with the unknown. The restful state students will experience prepares the mind for the day ahead, by decluttering their thought. Thus enabling them to gain a more focused perspective of what they need to do and feeling confident about doing it.   

              We currently have students signed-up but still have spaces available. Don’t miss out on this experience! If you would like to sign-up please do so here.

              G11-12 Reminder: ACT Course Preparation Registration
              The ACT is the main entrance test used by US universities to assess student's’ eligibility for admission. (You may have heard of the SAT – the ACT is now the more popular test, and it is our recommendation that students take the ACT due to recent changes to the SAT). The ACT is a standardised test of mathematics, English and science for students in Grade 11 or Grade 12.

              This course will take students in-depth into the content of the ACT, giving them an understanding of the knowledge and techniques needed to improve their score. Taught by several tutors to allow for small-groups and individualised help, the 20-hour course will teach the thinking behind all the subject areas and questions that come up on the test. Plenty of individual feedback will be given during and after the course, and all course materials will be provided.

              Halcyon does not provide tuition for the ACT but we work in partnership with UES London who specialise in the ACT and other standardised tests. Based near the school in Marylebone, UES is convenient and available to answer queries about the course and the tests. Once you have registered via the link below, Jason Smith from UES London will be in touch with you to arrange the course. In addition, if you have any questions about university admissions or standardised testing please contact Halcyon's University Counsellor, Mark Bowen.

              The cost is £700 per student inc. VAT, although this will decrease to £650 if enough students join the course.

              To register your interest, please follow this link. Please select "Halcyon ACT Course Sept 2016". The deadline for registration is Monday 19 September. If you have any questions please email Mark.

              6. Sustainability
              Become a Transport for London (TfL) Youth Panel Member
              As you might already be aware, Halcyon was awarded TfL’s Bronze STARS travel award and as a result some students will be able to attend the award ceremony. In addition, Halcyon's Eco-Committee would like to invite a Halcyon student to apply for the role of Transport for London (TfL) Youth Panel Member. The role will include:
              • As a young person, to give your views on TfL’s programmes and campaigns and share these with decision-makers across TfL’s organisation
              • Share information with other young people and champion TfL’s programmes
              • Represent the Youth Panel at events
              • Showcase the Panel’s work and help organise the annual Youth Participation Day event
              The Panel will meet every two months at TfL offices in central London. Travel expenses are covered and refreshments are provided. In order to apply, students must be:
              • Living in London or use TfL’s services frequently
              • Able to commit to attending Youth Panel meetings
              • Committed to getting young people's voices heard
              Applications will be considered by the Chair and Vice Chair of the Youth Panel and TfL’s staff that support the panel. Applicants will be told whether they have received a place by Monday 03 October and invited to attend the meeting on 14 October. The application deadline using this form is Sunday 18 September. Apologies for the short notice, we thought it was too good an opportunity to miss even with the tight deadline!

              Applications should be emailed to youthpanel@tfl.gov.uk. If you have any questions, please email Stephanie Cooke or Natalie Carman.
