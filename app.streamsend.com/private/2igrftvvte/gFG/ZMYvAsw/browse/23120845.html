1. IB Diploma Programme subjects at Halcyon
We are delighted to announce our IB Diploma Programme subject offer for 2015-2016. 

We have taken into account our students’ strengths and preferences, and designed an offer which includes a wide-range of subjects, with two or more possibilities in each subject group and the option of online Pamoja courses broadening this offer considerably.

The courses are either Higher Level (HL) or Standard Level (SL). HL and SL courses differ in scope, with students expected to demonstrate a greater body of knowledge, understanding and skills at Higher Level. Each student takes at least three (but not more than four) HL subjects, with the remaining subjects at SL.

2. Catering Update
Please note that by mutual consent, Jordi Casas will be moving on from his role as Catering Manager at Halcyon, effective immediately.

Eagle Solution Services (ESS), our catering consultants, will be overseeing the management of the kitchen on a daily basis as of Monday, supported by Theresa and Trish our kitchen assistants, as we seek a new Catering Manager. This will allow ESS's ongoing review of our catering service to continue with input from various members of the school community.

Over the next few weeks while we conduct the search for a new Catering Manager, ESS will draw on feedback received from the community and work closely with the catering staff to provide high quality catering. This interim period will allow the flexibility to consider some changes to the food service and allow ESS to better assist Halcyon in seeking suitable candidates to lead the service going forward. The aim is to appoint a new manager for early March. The school is proactively looking for a replacement and once a shortlist has been drawn up, the candidates will be invited into school to interact with us. 

If anyone knows of suitable candidates who may wish to apply, please let Julie or Rita know.

The school would like to thank Jordi Casas for his work in delivering our catering and we wish him all the very best for the future.

7. Digital Corner by Cindy Crannell
This week I wanted to share with you a digital parenting resource that I subscribed to by email over the summer called Family Online Safety Institute (FOSI) (to subscribe click here).

The FOSI mission is an international, non-profit organization which works to make the online world safer for kids and their families. FOSI convenes leaders in industry, government and the non-profit sectors to collaborate and innovate new solutions and policies in the field of online safety. Through research, resources, events and special projects, FOSI promotes a culture of responsibility online and encourages a sense of digital citizenship for all.

Their Good Digital Parenting blog covers a variety of topics for all ages and is one I have bookmarked on my Chrome browser. Looking for something specific, then filter it by age, category and type.

Feel free to contact me (Cindy) with any questions.

9. Upcoming Trip: Grade 6 to British Museum
Date: Monday 26 January
Depart: 1230
Return: 1530

Grade 6 are studying Ancient Civilisations in Humanities and in Visual Arts and we are planning to look at some of the relief sculptures and artefacts created by Romans, Aztec and Benin. Students will have the opportunity to explore the symbols that these civilisations used to represent their identity through these art-works. Students will also investigate any evidence from the artefacts that might support the ideas of what makes a society "civilised".

Students need to bring:
  • a topped-up Oyster card
  • fully-charged iPads to record photographic evidence
  • comfortable walking shoes
  • artbooks and drawing pencils.

Upcoming Trip: Grade 6 Backstage Tour at Theatre Royal Drury Lane

Date: Wednesday 28 January
Time: 0945
Return: 1205

Since 1663 the Theatre Royal Drury Lane has provided entertainment for the masses and has been visited by every monarch since the Restoration.

The theatre has not one, but two, royal boxes and it was here that the public first heard both the National Anthem and Rule Britannia. Previous buildings were managed by the great actor David Garrick and the famous playwright Richard Brinsley Sheridan and the current, fourth, building was opened in 1812.

Students must bring:
  • a topped-up Oyster card
  • coat
  • comfortable walking shoes.

    Understanding the Role of the GP
    Date: 26 January 2015
    Time: 1030 - 1230

    At the crux of the healthcare system in the UK is the General Practitioner (GP). If you have had to visit your local NHS or private practice for medical care you know that the first point of contact is almost always the GP. Join Dr Gill Bowes-Macleod, MBChB MRCP AdvDOM, Chief Executive, Roodlane Medical, HCA Hospitals, to gain a comprehensive understanding of the role of the GP for you and your family. How to register, when to contact, what to expect, when will you be referred and insurance and your GP are a few of the topics that this essential healthcare seminar will cover.

    Finding clarity in a time of transition
    Date: 25 February
    Time: 1030 - 1230

    The familiar highs and lows in life can be exacerbated when going through periods of change, for example moving to a different country. This seminar provides an opportunity for you to take stock of your life at the moment, making an honest assessment of what is going well and what needs to improve. The next step is to get clear about what you want. You should leave this seminar with a clear idea of what is getting in the way of you feeling happy and fulfilled at the moment and what you want from your life. Sometimes you find out it's not what you think it is!
