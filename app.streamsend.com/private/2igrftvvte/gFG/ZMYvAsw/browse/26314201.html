        1. Director’s Message
        Dear Students, Parents and Staff,

        I would like to take a moment to address the horrific bombings that occurred yesterday in Brussels. While we are all affected by such affronts, being part of an international school means many of us may be quite personally affected as we have friends and family all over the world. We all hope that you and your extended circles of families and friends are safe and secure. As you know, if you feel your child(ren) may benefit from extra support at school, please do not hesitate to contact me or Gareth Jones, our Student Wellbeing Leader.

        As Term 2 ends today, I am writing to thank you all for your support throughout what has been a productive and positive session. I have been really pleased to see our students making the most of all of the opportunities available to them at school, and with the success they have enjoyed in their endeavours. Well done to everyone!

        There have been so many highlights this term; for example, the inspirational Grade 10 Personal Project Exhibition; the many successes across the grade levels as a part of the UK Mathematics Trust team challenge; seeing two of our students performing so confidently at the English Speaking Union London Regional Finals; the excellent and thoughtful Theatre Writing Workshop and performance; a delightful and thought-provoking series of student-led assemblies from understanding cyberbullying to supporting homeless people; our colourful celebration of reading during World Book Week; and our Global Issues Network trip to Luxembourg (see below). I’d also like to say thank you to all our Halcyon Ambassadors - a great student team, who have generously offered their time to support our Open Houses while improving upon valuable skills such as presenting, collaborating and managing their time.

        There is much more going on at Halcyon than I can share with you here, but I hope this gives you a flavour of the exciting learning community that we celebrate at Halcyon. Thank you to our students and staff, all of whom make such a positive difference to learning.

        I look forward to all that’s ahead in Term 3.

        Have a great Spring break!

        Best wishes,
        Barry

        4. Adventures in Learning
        Grade 10 Trip, Saatchi Gallery
        Date: Thursday 14 April
        Time: Depart Halcyon at 0915, Return to Halcyon at 1315
        Location: Saatchi Gallery, Duke Of York's HQ, King's Rd, London SW3 4RY
        Grade 10 will be visiting the Saatchi Gallery for both English and Art. The Saatchi Gallery is holding an exhibition called "Champagne of Life" with a range of art-works created by a variety of artists.

        This will give the Art students an opportunity to explore the different types of materials used, and investigate the meaning and purpose of the work, which will give them sources for their own themes taken from their English play "Streetcar named Desire" by Tennessee Williams. Students will also observe the space of the gallery, which they will use to write a review on the curatorial space for the artworks.

        The English students will explore the contemporary artworks, making connections to the themes in the play "Streetcar named Desire". This will allow the students to use their art-themed inspiration to write creatively.

        Grade 10 will leave Halcyon at 0915 and return to Halcyon at 1315.

        Students need to bring:
        • a topped up Oyster card
        • money for lunch or a packed lunch
        • fully charged iPad
        • warm jacket
        • comfortable walking shoes

        Grade 6 Trip, The British Museum
        Date: Friday 15 April
        Time: Depart Halcyon at 0915, Return to Halcyon at 1315
        Location: The British Museum, Great Russell St, London WC1B 3DG
        Grade 6 will be visiting The British Museum on Friday 15 April for the Art and Drama department, being accompanied by Sue Dance and Kelly Foster. The students will have the opportunity to observe and investigate ceremonial or tribal masks from various cultures. Students will be able to make notes, take photographs and draw shapes and patterns, to give them inspiration for their own masks that they will be creating over Term 3. These "character" masks will then be used in their Drama classes for their performance, exploring the concept of transformation.

        Students will be travelling from Halcyon at 0915 to the Museum. The group will arrive back to Halcyon at 1315.

        Students will need to bring:
        • a fully charged iPad
        • a topped up Oyster card
        • comfortable walking shoes
        • warm jacket
        The school will provide a packed lunch, but students may bring money to buy an extra snack.


        Grade 7 Trip, National Gallery
        Date: Thursday 21 April
        Time: Depart Halcyon at 0915, Return to Halcyon at 1230
        Location: National Gallery, Trafalgar Square, London WC2N 5DN
        Grade 7 will be visiting the National Gallery on Thursday 21 April to study the Impressionist painters. This is part of their interdisciplinary unit "Colour and Light" with Science and Art, whereby they will have the opportunity to investigate the scientific colour theory used by these artists.

        Students will use their iPads to photograph and make annotations on the characteristics found in the paintings, which will they will continue to analyse in both classes.

        Students will be accompanied by Sue Dance (Art) and Lori Fritz (Science). The group will leave Halcyon at 0915 and return by lunch, 1230.

        Students will need to bring:
        • a topped up Oyster card
        • fully charged iPad
        • warm jacket
        • comfortable walking shoes

        Grade 9 Trip, Imperial War Museum
        Date: Tuesday 26 April
        Time: Depart Halcyon at 1220, Return to Halcyon at 1530
        Location: Imperial War Museum, Lambeth Rd, London SE1 6HZ
        Grade 9 will be visiting the Imperial War Museum with Sue Dance (Art) and Stephanie Cooke (Humanities) on Tuesday 26 April. The group will visit the Peace and Security 1945-2014 Gallery giving the students an opportunity to research and gain inspiration for their Cold War inspired art installation for an Art and History interdisciplinary unit.

        The group will be leaving Halcyon at 1220 returning to Halcyon for 1530.

        Students will need to bring:
        • a topped up Oyster card
        • fully charged iPad
        • warm jacket
        • comfortable walking shoes
        Lunch will be provided by the school. Students might wish to bring spending money for an extra snack.

        New Staff Announcements
        We are delighted to tell you about four new teachers who will be joining the Halcyon community for the coming school year. Learn about them below:

        Jon Neale, Digital Learning Leader
        Passionate about the effective use of technology to enhance learning outcomes, Jon is driven to stay on top of an educational field that is transforming daily. Jon has delivered training across Europe and in Africa, sharing pedagogical ideas as Apple Distinguished Educator and both Google for Education Certified Innovator and Trainer.

        Excited by Halcyon’s clear vision and pursuit of innovative practice, Jon joins Halcyon as Digital Learning Leader, leaving his present post as Head of eLearning at a large secondary school in Hampshire. With additional experience as Governor, Head of Faculty and Apple RTC Manager he is eager to bring his enthusiasm for all aspects of education to the school.

        Jon says “I believe that for the integration of technology to be successful, it has to be meaningful. It is crucial that learning requirements drive the technology; not vice-versa. Like Halcyon, I firmly believe that all those in education are blessed with a unique opportunity to help shape the future. It is a great pleasure to be joining such an adventurous and well respected school.”

        George Elia, Music Teacher
        George joins us as an experienced teacher of music having held positions such as Director of Music, Head of Department and Advanced Skills Teacher in various institutions across London. His experience lends itself to both classroom teaching and instrumental teaching as a violin teacher. George majored in violin performance at Middlesex University from 1998 to 2001, obtaining a distinction in his final recital.

        George is an active freelance musician and has established himself as a regular performer for a range of events and functions across popular London venues. George has a passion for current technologies in music and is always striving to combine and embrace conventional practices with innovation.

        Gabriel English, Spanish Teacher
        Gabriel English is an experienced IB educator who comes to us from the island of Puerto Rico. Gabriel has led curriculum development as an MYP Coordinator and taught both Spanish and English language acquisition as well as English language and literature courses in international schools in Switzerland and the UK. He holds degrees in Spanish and International Education with a specialisation in English language.

        Gabriel is a strong advocate of multilingualism and has worked closely with EAL students to help them achieve academic excellence, and adjust and build links in their new communities.

        Gabriel has participated in several community projects in the Dominican Republic and is passionate that students have opportunities to experience new cultures and environments.

        Chloe Macbean, Design Technology Teacher
        Chloe is a Design Technology teacher, specialising in Product Design and Graphic Products. Chloe has gained invaluable experience in teaching and developing units for MYP level, GCSE, A-Level and the DP, covering all areas of Design Technology for schools in London and Hong Kong over the past nine years. After completing a degree in History of Design with Studio Practice at Loughborough University, Chloe went into the fashion industry gaining full awareness of how the design process fits successfully into the world of business, before training as a teacher in London.

        Through her experience and understanding that the design cycle is all encompassing, she encourages her pupils to experiment with a variety of materials including the resistant materials, modern and smart materials, food, electronics and textiles. Chloe strongly believes in the application of Design Technology to benefit and enhance the lives of everyone and the world around us, promoting considered sustainable design at each stage of the cycle.

        6. Inspiring SA and CAS
        As part of our Inspiring SA and CAS (Creativity, Action, Service) feature, this week we are focusing on Service. What do we do to help others or the environment?


        Student Wellbeing Leader Gareth Jones says:
        "I donate to and I am a member of a number of charities often signing their petitions. The charities I am involved with as a member or donor are Greenpeace, RSPB, Crisis, the National Trust and English Heritage.

        In the past I have dedicated time and energy to volunteering working as a volunteer teacher in Tanzania and Rwanda. I also set up a youth centre in Zambia which I ran for three years. This gave me the opportunity to learn about culture and share my culture and skills with others. The most important element of service is that it is a two way activity in that sharing skills helps develop everyone involved."

        Spanish Teacher Fatima Gonzalez says:
        "I normally buy locally produced organic products. In the past I have been involved in two education projects. One in Western Sahara, helping to organise a school in a refugee camp of Polisario Front in 2004 while at University and another one in 2010 in North Sulawesi (Indonesia) helping to develop an orphanage school."

        Admissions Director Maria Rosengren says:
        "My husband and I are part of Brockley's Tree Society that promotes the maintenance and development of a greener Brockley, we meet once a month to discuss the Brockley trees. We also grow our own vegetables during the Summer months - carrots, squash, radishes, potatoes, tomatoes - the best part of Summer is spent in the garden."
