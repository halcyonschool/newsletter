3. Mother Tongue Language Programme
We are pleased to announce that our Mother Tongue Programme has started. Initially we are offering German and Italian with seven students participating. However, as the school grows we will be expanding the programme and more languages will be added based on demand.

Students will have the opportunity to improve literacy in their first language and with that their level of linguistic competence in other languages will improve as well.

Halcyon’s goal is to offer students the chance to strengthen their sense of cultural and personal identity through mother tongue instruction.

If you are interested in your child participating in our Mother Tongue Programme or require further information, please contact Noelia Zago.

5. Global Issues Network (GIN) Club Follow-up
A big welcome back to members of Halcyon GIN club, who last Thursday presented at their first international conference. They presented in front of students and teachers from all over the globe. The student-led 45 minute session educated and raised awareness of sanitation issues. The students have participated in sessions by other schools and listened to a range of interesting presentations. Keynote speakers at Euro GIN included Cassandra Lin, who at the age of 10 started to provide biofuel to struggling families in Rhode Island and UNICEF discussing their work with child soldiers in the Central African Republic. At the end of the conference they worked towards creating an action plan for the next year. The plan includes making the club more professional, sustainable and impactful.

This was a valuable experience for all involved including the teachers. We will be publishing a video soon so you can get a taste of what Halcyon's first ever residential trip was like.

7. Learning to Crawl: Robotics
Students in Grade 9 have been working on the inquiry statement 'Through the development of relationships between man and technology we can create models that can simplify our lives'. This has been an interdisciplinary unit between technology and mathematics. Students have looked at the historic applications of coding and ciphers, including the Caesar cipher, Vigenere cipher and the One-time pad. The class followed this up by looking at modern day coding. To do this, the students have built a Crawlbot and manipulated Arduino code. This was made possible by the innovation of Michael Margolis who developed the Crawlbot and has supported us on the project. Michael is the author of “The Arduino Cookbook”.

9. Google Cultural Institute
Cindy Crannell and Michelle Jones were invited to attend the launch of the Google Cultural Institute on Wednesday 26 March at the London offices in Soho.

The Google Cultural Institute "brings together millions of artefacts from multiple partners, with the stories that bring them to life, in a virtual museum", including artefacts from previously private collections.

Aside from a wonderful networking opportunity, Cindy and Michelle were given the opportunity to interact directly with Google representatives currently working with educators in London and Paris. This latest Google Education project offers numerous opportunities for student learning and global engagement, and both of these educators are excited to share this with their colleagues in an upcoming Professional Development session at Halcyon.

For more information, or to try this exciting platform, please visit the Google Cultural Institute.
