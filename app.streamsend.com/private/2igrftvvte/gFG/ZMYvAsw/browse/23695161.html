3. Reminder about attendance and punctuality
As we embark on a new Term, we ask that all families ensure their children arrive on time at school. Punctuality is vital in ensuring the smooth running of the school and late arrivals impact negatively on all students’ learning.

It is Halcyon’s expectation that students will attend school every day unless they are ill or there are exceptional circumstances. Any absences not related to illness require prior written explanation and clearance with the Head.

If your child is unwell and not able to come to school, please contact Reception by phone 020 7258 1169 or email reception@halcyonschool.com before 0830 to confirm the absence and provide a reason. You can also inform us through Managebac.
4. Staff Announcement - Sonia O’Dell
We’re delighted to introduce you to Sonia O’Dell, the new Food Service Manager at Halcyon, who will be joining Elizabeth and Trisha, our existing catering assistants. Some of you would have already met Sonia while she was on campus doing her trial day. Sonia brings a great deal of experience and passion to her work and she is looking forward to collaborating with students, parents and staff to integrate and develop the food culture at Halcyon.
Sonia is excited to work at an international school because she believes that “being internationally-minded extends one’s mind, knowledge and understanding beyond the immediate and the familiar so that your views and experience may be expressed to people of different cultures. International-mindedness opens minds to new ideas as part of the process of developing socially responsible citizens”.

Sonia’s focus on customer service and a passion for food will make her a valued member of the Halcyon community. Please extend her a warm Halcyon welcome.
    5. IB Diploma Programme Verification visit, 16 & 17 April
    Halcyon will be welcoming two visitors: Naheed Bardai and Roz Trudgon on Thursday and Friday of next week for the final stage of the IB Diploma Programme authorisation process. They will be meeting with members of the school community to verify the progress we have made in our implementation of the IBDP. As part of the process they are meeting with staff, students and parents. If you happen to be at school when they are here please give them a warm welcome. Thank you for your support in this exciting final step on the way to being an authorised IB World school.
    6. Kids' Company
    On Friday 27 March, Halcyon students wore their pyjamas to school and donated £2 to raise money for the charity, Kids' Company. This day was organised by Grade 6, and they raised £79! Kids' Company visited Halcyon in January and explained that their organisation provides practical, emotional and educational support to vulnerable children, young people and families. They reach 36,000 people across London, Bristol and Liverpool, including the most deprived and at risk children whose parents are unable to care for them due to their own practical and emotional challenges.

      vi. The Radio Play - Look What A Wonder
      Thursday 23 April 1545-1645
      Halcyon Room of Prayer

      This event is brought to us by Drama teacher, Julia Watson. This is a rare opportunity to listen to a radio play that may one day become a West End musical. Look What A Wonder is a one-hour adaptation of a gospel folk opera, based on the life of the ex-slave and free Black, Denmark Vesey. Look What A Wonder has already been immensely successful as a narrated concert performance. For reviews, along with pictures, synopsis and music clips, please go to www.lookwhatawonder.com. Please sign up here to attend. Questions? Contact Julia Watson.
