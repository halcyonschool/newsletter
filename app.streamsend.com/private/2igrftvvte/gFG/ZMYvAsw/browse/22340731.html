3. REMINDER: ISA Testing 6 & 7 October
Schedule:
Monday 6 October 0900-1200
Tuesday 7 October 0900-1200

The ISA English and mathematics tests are "paper and pencil tests". Students will need to bring:
  • a pencil
  • an eraser
  • a blue or black pen
  • and students whose first language is not English may use a translating dictionary (book form, not digital).
Students in Grades 8 and 10 will also take ISA science tests. These are online tests and students in Grades 8 and 10 will also need:
  • a fully charged iPad.
Each test lasts approximately 45 minutes but the tests are not time limited - students may continue working past the allotted time if they need. There will be breaks between the tests. Please note that no special preparation or revision is necessary for these tests.

This letter gives information about the privacy of data on individual students when test results are reported and used for research. The personal data provided by Halcyon is limited to name, date of birth and whether or not English is the first language of the student.

If you would like any further information about the tests, please contact Lori Fritz.

5. Halcyon Community Field Studies Day
Thursday 16 October   
Departure: 0900 at Marble Arch
Return: 1535 pick up at Marble Arch

On Thursday 16 October, all Halcyon teachers and students will be involved in field trip activities related to their units of study. The activities will take place in various locations in and around London as detailed below.

All students will meet at 0900 at Marble Arch monument where we will take a school photo before setting off to our destinations by public transport. Students will also be dismissed from Marble Arch. Parents meeting students at the end of the day should arrive at Marble Arch by 1530.

Lunch is not provided so all students should bring a packed lunch or money for lunch, as detailed below.

Grade 6: Science and technology in Legoland
Students will explore the versatility of Lego as a modelling tool in workshops on computer coding and underwater habitats. There will also be a break where students will have time to enjoy a ride or two. Students will travel to Legoland in Windsor by train from Paddington.

Students need to bring:
  • topped-up Oyster card (for travel from Halcyon to Paddington)
  • comfortable walking shoes
  • waterproof jacket
  • packed lunch or money for lunch.
*They will not need ipads*

Grade 7: Art and natural history studies at the Victoria and Albert Museum and the Natural History Museum

Students will walk from Marble Arch to Hyde Park where they will carry out practice fieldwork using transects and quadrants, followed by investigations of biodiversity in the park. They will then walk down towards the Victoria and Albert Museum where they will visit the Islamic section to explore the use of geometry in ceramics and relief carvings.

Moving across the road to the Natural History Museum, students will have the opportunity to use their drawing skills for recording and classifying extraordinary extinct species.

Students need to bring:
  • charged ipad
  • their art journal
  • comfortable walking shoes
  • topped-up Oyster card
  • packed lunch or money for lunch.
Grade 8: Geography River studies

Students will explore the geography of a local river.

Students need to bring:
  • tape measure
  • string
  • oranges (ask the students!)
  • wellies/ rain boots (they may need to go in the river)
  • sensible outdoor clothing (+ a spare set!)
  • waterproof jacket
  • packed lunch and water bottle as there may not be an opportunity to purchase food and drink
  • charged ipad
  • topped-up Oyster card
  • small backpack for their belongings as the activities will be “hands on”.
Grades 9 & 10: Conflict examined through exhibitions and art

First stop will be the Imperial War Museum of London at which the Grade 9 and 10 students will explore the way in which modern conflict has been documented throughout history and the issues that surround such. Leaning on their inter-disciplinary English and History units, this field day will seek to explore modern stories of survival, heroism, and memory through a variety of exhibitions.

Moving on from the Imperial War Museum, students will visit the Tate Modern Museum. Studies at this venue will include Alexander Brodsky and Ilya Utkin modernist perspectives on city landscapes (in connection to Aldous Huxley’s “Brave New World”), Thomas Hirschhorn and Germaine Richier hybridisation of the body pieces, and the “Conflict, Time, Photography” exhibit concerned with the relationship between photography and sites of conflict over time.

Students need to bring:
  • charged ipad
  • topped-up Oyster card
  • packed lunch or money for lunch.
6. US university admissions process
Many thanks to Jason Smith of UES London who made a very useful presentation to Grade 9 and 10 students and parents earlier this week on the North American College admissions process and the standardised ACTs and SAT tests used for applications.

9. Guest Speaker Susan Pollack
Some Halcyon students are pictured here with Susan Pollack, who related her personal and very moving history of what it was like to experience anti-semitism in Hungary; to survive Auschwitz and Bergen Belsen camps; and to try to move on with her life after the loss of her family and country. Susan's prominent message to our students was to stand up wherever you encounter any form of bullying or injustice because without justice you have no humanity.
