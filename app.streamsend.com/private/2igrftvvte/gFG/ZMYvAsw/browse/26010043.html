Director’s Message
Dear Students, Parents and Staff,

I would like to thank you for your energy and enthusiasm in supporting our learning community for the first half of Term Two! As we get ready to depart for half-term break, I have been reflecting on the many successes we have enjoyed across our thriving community...and we are only half way through this term!

Today was an exciting time for the school as students, staff and some of our parents got to experience the new Google Expeditions Programme. Halcyon was chosen to be part of the Pioneer Programme for this new virtual reality platform. Teachers led students (and parents!) on a number of different expeditions, and we had a representative from Google in class to help lead the workshops. To name a couple of the expeditions, Grade 9 students went to Antarctica with Stephanie Cooke and Grade 7 visited the Gettysburg National Military Park with Claire Rose...all without leaving the classroom! We would like to thank Peter Falcone, from Google, along with Cindy Crannell and Claire Rose for organising this virtual reality experience. What a great day!

    Another example of our drive to provide our students with the best possible opportunities is seen in the growth of our Service As Action and CAS programmes. Students are developing and working on amazing projects such as Daphne’s Happy Thoughts project and Ethan’s Seeds of Growth project...to name only a couple. All of our students have shown great commitment to Service As Action and CAS. Thank you! I would also like to take this opportunity to thank our CAS Coordinator, Stephanie Cooke, and Service As Action Coordinator, Natalie Carman, for their tremendous efforts in supporting the students and community.

    Our first Exchange Programme student, Pauli, from St Ignatius in Argentina had her last week with us this week. Pauli was an exemplary student who received nothing but positive feedback from both her peers and teachers. We were sorry to see her go. We look forward to developing our Exchange Programme by sending three Halcyon students to St Ignatius in August 2016 for what we’re sure will be an enriching experience. Many thanks to our Exchange Programme Coordinator, Fatima Gonzalez for all of her hard work in developing this amazing opportunity for students.

    There is much more going on at Halcyon than I can cover here so please do enjoy reading about all of the happenings below.

    I hope you all have an enjoyable half term break and look forward to seeing everyone on Tuesday 23 February.

    Best wishes,
    Barry

    新年快乐! Xin Nian Kuai le!
    This Chinese New Year brings us the Year of Monkey, which is the ninth of the 12 recurring Chinese zodiac cycle. The Chinese New Year festivities began on Monday 08 Feb. There are a series of activities in London, such as the parade and the first lantern festival, all happening during the half-term break. Visit TimeOut London for more details. Mandarin students are also doing different activities to welcome the new lunar new year. Some of the activities have included, a Grade 11 trip to China Town, Grade 7 students made Monkey-shaped lanterns, Grades 8 and 9 students helped to prepare a celebratory Chinese lunch today, including wonton soup, Moo Shu pancakes with vegetables, vegetable stir fry, Chinese New Year themed cupcakes and Chinese custard buns.

    Exchange Programme: Pauli’s Last Week
    Pauli was our first student of the Language Exchange Programme between St. Ignatius College in Argentina and Halcyon London International School in the UK. As a pilot project, Pauli has helped to develop and define the basis of what will be an international experience for prospective students moving forward.

    Pauli's stay at Halcyon has been endearing and exemplary in all aspects: academic, social, and human. She was fully integrated from the first week although her first language is not English; she has attended all her chosen subjects, actively participating with very good feedback from teachers; her character and charm has made all of the students at the school welcome her with open arms by creating bonds of friendship from the first week; and also she has contributed in the Spanish department by supporting her peers who are studying Spanish as an additional language. It is worth noting that Pauli could have chosen to study an additional language (Mandarin or French) however, she decided to help her schoolmates, the Spanish department and the School, as a gesture of gratitude to the Exchange Programme.

    It has been a real pleasure to have Pauli at Halcyon and we wish her all the best in the future. She looks forward to welcoming Eric, Kieran and Nate from Halcyon for their August exchange.

    Fatima Gonzalez
    Exchange Programme Coordinator

    A Message from Claire, Grade 10, Personal Project JoyJ Outreach
    JoyJ is an amazing homeless charity that I came to love by doing an outreach in Atlanta. For my Personal Project, I decided to bring it to Halcyon. I have put together an outreach that is going to happen on Friday 26 February. Here is the link to my funding website. The link also contains much more information on learning about JoyJ as well.

    The main message of JoyJ:

    "JoyJ provides essential support to local homeless populations through direct outreach programmes. These unique programmes provide volunteers with opportunities to engage in ‘spontaneous philanthropy’. This differentiating aspect of our organisation creates a powerful effect on both volunteers and those on the receiving end. The distribution of ‘comfort bags’ provides not only basic items that can be used in everyday life but equally powerful intangibles, such as a mutual feeling of trust and respect."

    With such a great message comes a price - I am looking for funds to complete my project. If you could either please donate or share this with other people you know that would be greatly appreciated by myself, the charity, and the homeless people who receive help from this outreach.

    Thank you so much for your time and support.
    Claire

    Date: Friday 26 February

    Raising Bi-lingual Children
    Date: Tuesday 23 February
    Time: 1030-1230
    Free for FOCUS members, £25 for non-members, £15 for Halcyon parents
    Is your child bilingual or trilingual? What languages will your child need for their studies, for their work life and for their family life? Are you and your child’s school supporting your child's home language and culture? Is your child mixing English and your home language, or refusing to speak in your home language? In today’s world bilingualism is a highly regarded asset. In this workshop Susan Stewart, Head of Languages at International School of London will share her knowledge on how to ensure children make the most of their bilingualism now and throughout their life. She will talk about some dos and don’ts and what you, as a parent, can practically do today that will have a positive future impact on your child's language development.
