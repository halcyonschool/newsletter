3. Match Report: 
Girls Football v Dwight
The girls' football team had their second match of the season against Dwight on 20 November. Despite a strong defence, and a contentious goal, Dwight won 2-0. Jeffrey did an excellent job as Assistant Manager, coaching the team from the sidelines. Frida and Sabrina had a good midfield double-act which supported Gwen and Kate in their continuous attempts on goal; between them, they made 12 shots, but unfortunately, Dwight's high-quality keeper and a series of unavoidable left-footed strikes meant no attempts made it over the line. Anne Marie proved herself in her first game for the school, supporting the ever reliable Alexi in midfield. Cloe remained a feisty and assertive defender for the team alongside Dina who was a solid goalie and saved a number of powerful shots from the opposition.

Overall, an excellent game, in which both sides were constantly pushing forward on the offensive, but in which Dwight were the deserved winners.

5. Our Future, Our Rights
Grades 9 and 10 attended the Our Future, Our Rights event at the Southbank Centre run by the Overseas Development Institute (ODI). This event had incredible speakers including children's rights activist and Nobel winner Kailash Satyarthi; former Prime Minister Gordon Brown; alongside Malala Yousafzai's school friends Kainat Riaz and Shazia Ramzan; Camfed founder Ann Cotton; ODI Executive Director Kevin Watkins. It was hosted by Ade Adepitan, the British TV presenter and Paralympian. We also heard stories from several children across the world and had a video message from Ban Ki-Moon, Secretary-General for the United Nations, and Anthony Lake, Executive Director of Unicef.

The event was on the eve of the 25th anniversary of the Convention of the Rights of the Child and celebrated the great success of this, but also pointed out where it had fallen short. The event was aimed at getting people to sign a petition to help put pressure on governments to make changes to fulfil the plans of the Convention.

This was an incredible event - students were engaged and enthused by the speakers and it sparked some fantastic conversations. We would like everyone to sign the petition #UpForSchool to ensure that children across the world have access to education. You can sign this petition here: http://www.aworldatschool.org/upforschool
