          1. Student Achievements
          Grade 11 Visual Arts Exhibition
          The purpose of the Grade 11 IBDP Visual Arts exhibition was to give students an experience of exhibiting their work publicly, as a lead up to their final exhibition next year. It was also an opportunity for Halcyon families, students and staff to engage with the work and talk to students about their ideas, concepts and intentions.

          The pieces that were exhibited represent studio areas students have explored this year. Therefore, the audience experienced photography, drawing, printmaking, painting and sculpture. Each student has her own unique concepts, styles and media, making the show diverse and energetic.

          The exhibition provides an important learning opportunity for students. Firstly, it allows students to articulate ideas to an audience and helps them develop concrete insights about their own work. Secondly, it gives students an experience of putting a show together and provides an introduction into curatorial considerations. Finally, it provides an important forum for constructive feedback and support. Well done to the Grade 11 students: Alex, Anne-Maria, Charlie, Dina, Gwen and Isla.

          Grade 9 “Conflict” Installation Exhibition
          Global Context: Personal and Cultural Expression
          Concepts: Expression, Audience and Perspective
          Grade 9 created an interdisciplinary installation based on the Cold War, a topic they were studying in History. During their Art lessons, Grade 9 researched artists who depicted conflict and found aspects from these paintings to use for their inspiration.

          Students also visited the Imperial War Museum and explored the artefacts from the Cold War era and had the opportunity to observe Peter Kennard “Unofficial War Artist” allowing them to make further connections to other conflict art-works.

          The topic of conflict formed an interdisciplinary unit with Art and History where students spent time planning and collaborating ideas. They used the installation to express themselves through the perspectives of the East and West.

          See a 360 perspective of the installation here.


          Grade 11 & Eivor Martinus, Esteemed Playwright, Novelist and Translator
          On Monday 13 June, playwright, novelist, translator, and theatre director, Eivor Martinus worked with our Grade 11 students, who are currently studying August Strindberg's 'Miss Julie' as part of their Language and Literature Diploma course.

          Martinus has adapted several Swedish classics for BBC Radio and has translated 15 of Strindberg's plays into English for stage productions in London and New York. Here is what DP student Rachel had to say on working with Martinus:

          “It was a great privilege to hear an interesting lecture from such an esteemed fan of "Lady Julie" (as she argues it should be). The comments and knowledge that she brought to the table during our discussion helped guide us to make connections we may not have necessarily noticed about the novel before and will be invaluable to us as we begin a more in-depth study of the play.”

            Sports Day Reminder
            Date: Monday 20 June
            Time: 0900-1300
            Location: The Running Track at Paddington Recreational Ground (PRG), Randolph Avenue, London W9 1PD
            We kindly ask that students make own their way directly to Paddington Recreation Ground on Monday morning, instead of going to school. We will be convening at the running track.

            The closest tube station is Maida Vale. The buses that serve the area are 6, 16, 98 and 414.

            Students will take part in a series of track and field events and team sports during the morning. It is compulsory for students to wear their PE kit (either version). Please note nutritious snacks will be provided to sustain our athletes.

            Bring
            Due to the unpredictability of the weather, students should prepare themselves accordingly, either bringing:
            • sunscreen
            • a waterproof jacket
            • full water bottle
            • topped up Oyster card
            In case of Rain
            Please Note: Sports Day is weather dependent and we will forge ahead if there is just a chance of rain. Parents will receive a text message at 0730 on Monday confirming if Sports Day is going ahead at the PRG.

            Please Don’t Bring
            iPads: Sports Day will be a technology-free event so students should not bring their iPads. There is no need to have an iPad during the day, and there is no secure storage at the recreation ground. Please be aware that students will have no secure place to store their phones and other valuables at the recreation ground.

            At 1300, students will return to school by public bus (topped-up Oyster card required) accompanied by teachers. Upon arrival, lunch will be provided and students will remain at school until the usual time,1535, and after school clubs will run as normal.

            Facing History and Ourselves Benefit Dinner June 2016: Rita Halbright Receives Award
            On 07 June, Facing History and Ourselves held their annual benefit dinner with special guest speaker, the Rt. Hon. David Lammy, MP Tottenham, making a moving and thought-provoking speech about the importance of teaching critical thinking skills and inclusion throughout our schools. Halcyon students, Eric, Ethan, Kieran, Nate and Scott volunteered throughout the evening to assist the charity in pulling off a flawless event while Halcyon’s Rita Halbright was the recipient of Facing History’s Upstander Award, an award given to individuals who dedicate a great deal of their time and effort to supporting the important work of Facing History.
