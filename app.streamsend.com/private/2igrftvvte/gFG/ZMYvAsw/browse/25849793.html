This Week at Halcyon
BETT Conferences at Halcyon
We had two great BETT conferences at school this past Wednesday and Thursday where we hosted Google staff and delegates from other schools across the globe. We had three of our amazing Student Ambassadors (Sabrina, Lucca and Mike) who gave the delegates tours of our school. Three students also gave presentations on how Halcyon's digital integration enhances their learning experience (Rachel, Eric and Caroline). Many attendees congratulated the school on our students for being articulate, confident, intelligent and friendly ambassadors. Well done to everyone involved!

BETT Conference: Halcyon Students Present on the Google Stand
Today three of our students presented at the BETT conference at ExCel London on Google’s stand. The conference was an excellent opportunity for our students to showcase how they feel digital integration has helped to transform their learning, and also a chance for them to learn about student's experiences with technology from other schools and to explore new cutting edge technologies.

Read about the student presentations here where we have also included one of the presentations for you to watch.

Halcyon Lifelong Learning Seminars, From Child To Adolescence – The Changing Teenage Brain
Time: 1900-2030
Date: Thursday 28 January
Where: Halcyon
An informative and explorational parent seminar around the emerging themes of managing the emotional and developmental changes in our children as they move from being a child into adolescence, focusing primarily on their emotional and mental health and wellbeing. This event will be facilitated by Emma Dawes, Halcyon School Counsellor.

Emma is a drama therapist (MA) and school counsellor and works with children and young people of all ages in schools, health care settings and in organisations. These have included primary and secondary schools, pupil referral units, eating disorder clinics, NHS and private clinics for mental health, addictions, and many other areas of clinical work. Emma also provides staff and parent training seminars and workshops, raising awareness and support around the themes most commonly explored within counselling and therapy surrounding the mental health and emotional wellbeing of young people.

If you are interested in attending, please sign-up here.

      United Kingdom Mathematics Trust (UKMT)
      The UK Mathematics Trust (UKMT) is a registered charity whose aim is to advance the education of children and young people in mathematics. The UKMT organises national mathematics competitions and other mathematical enrichment activities for 11-18 year old UK school pupils.

      All students in Grades 6, 7 and 8, and some students in Grades 9 and 10, will be competing in the UKMT individual challenges:
      • Grades 6 and 7: Thursday 28 April 2016
      • Grades 8, 9 and 10: Thursday 04 February 2016
      The participating students will have to complete 25 multiple choice questions. Of these, the first 15 are more accessible whilst the final 10 will provide more food for thought. Gold, silver and bronze certificates are awarded to 40% of participants nationally in the Junior and Intermediate Challenges, and 60% of participants nationally in the Senior Challenge. The most successful participants at each level are invited to enter follow-on rounds; Kangaroos (multiple choice questions) or Olympiads requiring full written answers.

      We will be running lunchtime sessions to aid students with their mathematics competition technique.

      For more information please see the website or email Natalie Carman.

      The Parent Factor: The Role of a Parent in their Child’s Education
      Date: Monday 25 January
      Time: 1030-1230
      Free for FOCUS members, £25 for non-members, £15 for Halcyon parents
      There is a big debate raging in education about how children learn, and the best strategies to affect “good learning”. There are many theories and beliefs about this question and how best to educate children to maximise their potential both at school and beyond. Join Ed Richardson, Director of Education at Keystone, for a presentation that will help us understand better the role parents can play in their children’s learning. Ed’s talk will encourage parents to think critically about different approaches to learning, to challenge a few basic assumptions about the school-student-parent paradigm and to consider the parental role in a child’s education.

      The talk is suitable for parents with children of all ages but will be particularly applicable for school age children.


      Raising Bi-lingual Children
      Date: Tuesday 23 February
      Time: 1030-1230
      Free for FOCUS members, £25 for non-members, £15 for Halcyon parents
      Is your child bilingual or trilingual? What languages will your child need for their studies, for their work life and for their family life? Are you and your child’s school supporting your child's home language and culture? Is your child mixing English and your home language, or refusing to speak in your home language? In today’s world bilingualism is a highly regarded asset. In this workshop Susan Stewart of International School of London will share her knowledge on how to ensure children make the most of their bilingualism now and throughout their life. She will talk about some dos and don’ts and what you, as a parent, can practically do today that will have a positive future impact on your child's language development.
