7. Grade 8’s trip to Tate Britain
Grade 8 visited Tate Britain on Tuesday to look at Phyllida Barlow's massive and fascinating installation created from recycled materials. The students also had the opportunity to look at Bill Woodrow's sculpture, who also works from recycled materials. This gave the students a chance to compare and contrast these two artists and build on their analytical skills for the unit later in the year called "Junk Art". Students will be using the app "voicethread" to make comparative comments about these pieces.

Upcoming Trip: Grade 8 to Thames Explorer Field Studies Centre
Monday 27 October
Depart: 0845
Return: 1535

On the very first day back after October break (Monday 27 October), Grade 8 will be going on their Humanities field trip to the Thames Explorer Field Studies centre in Chiswick.

The trip will involve walking on the foreshore (mud and shingle) and so old, comfortable footwear is essential.

Students will need to bring:
  • topped-up Oyster card
  • waterproof clothing
  • spare change of clothing (just incase!)
  • wellies, if possible.

Upcoming Trip: Grade 10 to BFI
Tuesday 28 October
Depart: 1030
Return: 1530

As part of Grade 10’s unit on Mass Media & Communication, students will be going to the British Film Institute (BFI) for an interactive study day on Media Regulation. During the session, students will be exploring the question: "Why do we need media regulation in a digital age?". This interactive study day will cover mass media in film, television, print, online and advertising and will offer students an invaluable opportunity to hear and question guest industry speakers.

Students will need to bring:
  • topped-up Oyster card
  • their charged iPad.
Packed lunches will be provided by the school, but students may want to bring some money for snacks.
