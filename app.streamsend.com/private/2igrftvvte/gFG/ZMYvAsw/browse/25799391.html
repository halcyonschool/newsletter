Director’s Message
Dear Parents and Staff,

We have ended the second week of Term 2 on a high. We have had two successful three way conference sessions which we hope were informative and offered an insight into your child's learning. Your feedback about the conferences is most welcome so please don’t hesitate to contact me. This is also a great time for me to say thank you to all the staff who went above and beyond, to prepare and deliver these conferences. And a big thank you to all of the students and parents who attended.

On a similar note, we are currently progressing well with our strategic planning committee. We are looking forward to tomorrow's workshop, to consider the responses received from both the survey and the focus groups, and to share ideas and proposals for Halcyon’s continued growth and success over the coming years. Thank you in advance to students, parents and staff who are taking time out from their weekend to attend, your participation is invaluable.

Yesterday we held one of our Open House mornings. We would like to say a big thank you to Scott, Ellie, Antonia, Emily, Allegra and Maga for representing Halcyon as our Student Ambassadors at the morning. They dedicated their time to help us present the student voice to prospective parents. Each student gave an excellent self-written presentation on a variety of topics: MUN, school trips, Student Council, course curriculum and GIN. In addition, they answered questions from attendees with confidence, maturity, and ease. We would also like to thank our previous ambassadors: Kit, Lucca, Christina, Sabrina, Manuel, Jaden, Lucy and Kieran. All of our Open House guests have expressed their admiration for the Ambassador's public speaking skills, polite manners and enthusiasm for the school. Once again thank you for supporting our school!

We now look to next week and are delighted to tell you that we are a Google Reference school, which means that other schools look to us as a benchmark for Google applications for education. As such, we will be hosting Google staff and delegates from other schools around the world on Wednesday 20 January and Thursday 21 January 2016. In addition, three of our students will be presenting at the Bett conference on the Google stand on Friday 22 January 2016. The conference is an excellent opportunity for our students to demonstrate how they feel digital integration has helped to transform their learning, and also a chance for them to learn about student's experiences with technology from other schools and also to explore new cutting edge technologies.

In finishing I would like to thank all of our community for their continued support of Halcyon!

Best wishes,
Barry

    Halcyon Lifelong Learning Seminars, From Child To Adolescence – The Changing Teenage Brain
    Time: 1900-2030
    Date: Thursday 28 January
    Where: Halcyon
    An informative and explorational parent seminar around the emerging themes of managing the emotional and developmental changes in our children as they move from being a child into adolescence, focusing primarily on their emotional and mental health and wellbeing. This event will be facilitated by Emma Dawes, Halcyon School Counsellor.

    Emma is a drama therapist (MA) and school counsellor and works with children and young people of all ages in schools, health care settings and in organisations. These have included primary and secondary schools, pupil referral units, eating disorder clinics, NHS and private clinics for mental health, addictions, and many other areas of clinical work. Emma also provides staff and parent training seminars and workshops, raising awareness and support around the themes most commonly explored within counselling and therapy surrounding the mental health and emotional wellbeing of young people.

    If you are interested in attending, please sign-up here.

    ESU Performing Shakespeare Competition
    Well done to Mia Ciauri and Matilde Sanchetti who have been invited to perform at the ESU London Region Performing Shakespeare finals! The competition offers a chance for students to gain confidence in performing Shakespeare.

    The ESU Performing Shakespeare Competition allows students of 11 and 14 to enhance their understanding and enjoyment of Shakespeare through theatre and performance. The ESU is excited to enter the 5th year of this growing national competition.

    Mia and Matilde submitted a short video of themselves performing a monologue from Romeo and Juliet. The best entries are sent forward to the Regional Finals, a day which will include educational workshops and a reprise of the video entries in front of a live audience. Winners from each region will be invited to the National Grand Final in London, in March 2016.

    The finals will be on Thursday 25 February 2016. We wish Mia and Matilde the best of luck!

    The Parent Factor: The Role of a Parent in their Child’s Education
    Date: Monday 25 January
    Time: 1030-1230
    Free for FOCUS members, £25 for non-members, £15 for Halcyon parents
    There is a big debate raging in education about how children learn, and the best strategies to affect “good learning”. There are many theories and beliefs about this question and how best to educate children to maximise their potential both at school and beyond. Join Ed Richardson, Director of Education at Keystone, for a presentation that will help us understand better the role parents can play in their children’s learning. Ed’s talk will encourage parents to think critically about different approaches to learning, to challenge a few basic assumptions about the school-student-parent paradigm and to consider the parental role in a child’s education.

    The talk is suitable for parents with children of all ages but will be particularly applicable for school age children.


    Raising Bi-lingual Children
    Date: Tuesday 23 February
    Time: 1030-1230
    Free for FOCUS members, £25 for non-members, £15 for Halcyon parents
    Is your child bilingual or trilingual? What languages will your child need for their studies, for their work life and for their family life? Are you and your child’s school supporting your child's home language and culture? Is your child mixing English and your home language, or refusing to speak in your home language? In today’s world bilingualism is a highly regarded asset. In this workshop Susan Stewart of International School of London will share her knowledge on how to ensure children make the most of their bilingualism now and throughout their life. She will talk about some dos and don’ts and what you, as a parent, can practically do today that will have a positive future impact on your child's language development.


    Introducing Kensington Chelsea Women’s Club (KCWC)
    The HPC would like to introduce organisations in London that may be useful to our parent community. The KCWC is an organisation of international and British women who want to enjoy everything London has to offer. They have an activity based club where members can attend events organised by fellow members who follow their passion and use their skills to organise over 30 activities including: history, culture, art, design, sports, crafts and hobbies, special events, languages, book and lecture groups, leisure pastimes, travel and tours in the UK and Europe as well as volunteering. Daytime and evening events are available. If you are interested in learning more about the KCWC or becoming a member, please see here. This is a great opportunity to meet people if you are new to London, or just to get involved in activities you enjoy!


    How Do I Deal With Being a Parent of an Adolescent?
    A new Halcyon Discussion Group is forming to share our collective knowledge of how to be effective parents for our London teenagers.
