        1. Director’s Message: Halcyon Student MUN
        A key component of a Halcyon education is that we promote student advocacy and agency; the ability to express oneself and for that expression to be realised - that student voice also means student action. Not only do our students feel comfortable sharing what they would like to change, but they also know how to make that change happen. Nowhere is that more apparent than in our student-led Model United Nations (MUN) conference, an event wholly organised by our student MUN Board of Directors. The conference coordination involved managing facilities, materials, budgets, catering, programme details, communications, registration, health & safety issues, risk assessments, web design and inviting and organising students from different schools. All this had to be brought together, communicated and delivered - a huge task, even for those who do this professionally. For two days this week, all this happened like clockwork - a fabulous student-led MUN conference that showcased just how intelligent, engaged and thoughtful our students are about the world, and how aware they are of all the challenges they will inherit.

        I would like to thank our MUN Board of Directors - Alexi, Ellie, Eric, Eveline, Evy, Kieran, Nate, Nathan, Rachel and Scott - for all of the above. It has been a magnificent effort and all your hard work has given the opportunity for so many others to have a voice and to have the ability to lead change. Thank you!

        Best wishes,
        Barry Mansfield
        Director
          2. Student Achievements
          MUN Conference

          “Halcyon’s 2016 MUN Conference has been an amazing opportunity to expand my communication with the London community by meeting new students from other London based schools and become a more active global thinker from participating in engaging debate on current global issues."
          Charlie Sears, Chair of ECOSOC (Economic and Social Committee)

          This year our MUN academics team researched and chose a number of topics for debate, including:
          • Reducing dependence on nonrenewable energy resources
          • Implementing Universal Health Care
          • Regulation of borders and immigration
          • Increasing internet security
          The overall theme for the conference was ‘Our Changing Climate’. The topics and theme were selected after much insightful research because we believed that they were most relevant to our current global environment.

          We were delighted to have guest speaker Moraig Henderson present on her experience working in the global sphere from the UN to UK Parliament. Hearing about her hands-on experiences and insights was a fantastic way to close the conference. Moraig has experience as:
          • SNP Head of Office for the Westminster Foundation for Democracy | UK Parliament
          • Deputy Director - Policy & Communications | UNDP
          • Deputy Chief - Peace Division - UNV | UNDP
          • Head of Office - UNAMI - Kurdistan | UNAMI
          • Strategic Planning Specialist | UNDP-BCPR
          • Special Asst. | UNAMI

          We asked Halcyon’s new Math teacher, Ean Alleyne what he thought of his first Halcyon MUN Conference:

          "Halcyon’s MUN Conference was a fantastic two days. I got to witness a refined and restored faith in the next generation. Their chosen debating issues are of critical importance that will impact the way the world spins. It's been great to see young ladies and gentlemen dressed for success and I have loved the protocols that they put in place for the conference such as not using personal pronouns and restoring oratory back to academia."

          The Halcyon MUN team as a whole has felt inspired by the passionate and engaging debates that have taken place over the past two days. The team collaboration in the build up to the conference and throughout the two days was really motivating...we can’t wait for next year’s already!

          London Wildlife Trust
          On Tuesday after school nine Halcyon students and two parents visited the London Wildlife Trust site at Camley Street Natural Park in King's Cross. There the group saw the work done by the London Wildlife Trust to help ensure natural diversity and a 'wild' area in the middle of central London to help support bats, birds and insects. They looked at the site’s food digester which takes waste from local restaurants, digests it to produce biogas, which runs their kitchen. The fertiliser produced is used to grow the fruits and vegetables they use in their café. The site is free to walk around and they are always looking for support though signing up for membership. The London Wildlife Trust is one of Halcyon's chosen charities so some of our fundraising events are to support the important work they do in educating on sustainability and providing a haven for wildlife.

          Halcyon’s Sustainability Club Student-led Assembly
          On Wednesday morning Halcyon Sustainability Club members Ethan, Eveline, Julia, Nate, Noah, Mike and Vicky held an assembly to raise awareness in the school community about the work that is taking place in the club and also to increase sustainability awareness of all members of the school community. As Nate said: "We wanted to educate school students about some issues related to sustainability and demonstrate the impacts of their actions and encourage actions in the school to help protect the oceans, reduce food waste, and increase recycling."

          As the assembly was held on 08 June, which is World Oceans Day, Nate and Julia spoke about the threats of devastating overfishing, acidification, and bleaching of the oceans and gave students the advice of reducing fish consumption or making more ethical fish consumption choices. Reducing plastic waste and recycling will also help solve the problem of plastics, which some fish now prefer to consume over their natural food sources. A key goal of the club is for all Halcyon students to bring their own reusable water bottle to school so we can eliminate the use of the plastic cups. Watch this space for Halcyon water bottles, which the students are currently researching, and please encourage your child to bring one to school if they already have one at home.

          Eveline, Noah and Mike spoke about the importance of recycling and have already set up a recycling bin system in school. Reduce, reuse and recycle is their mantra.

          Vicky spoke about the environmental benefits of vegetarianism to help students understand why this is one of Halcyon’s commitment. Reducing animal product consumption uses far less water, land and CO2 which will have a big impact on our environment. Ethan spoke to students to remind them to reduce their portion sizes if they were throwing away food. This has a huge impact, not only on the resources used to produce excess food, but also to fly it around the world to reach us.
