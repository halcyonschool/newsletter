1. Yearbook update
MONDAY is the deadline for students to update their profiles on our Yearbook via YearbookMachine.com. Remember this is a student-led Yearbook, so all students need to contribute content.

All students should have:
  • uploaded photographs (including photographs of work they are proud of, trips, events - they can even upload a baby photograph of themselves)
  • filled in profile questions on the left hand side of their profile page
  • left comments on friends' and teachers' pages, including for students in other year groups - they can leave comments or fill in questions about their friends on the right hand side of their profile pages
  • taken part in the polls – we've added new ones to the site
  • ticked the sections of their profile page they want printed
  • selected the three photographs they want printed on their page [one with just them, then the other two they could choose one with friends, a piece of work they like...]
Lenny Dutton has created this video to talk students through doing this.
2. Introducing Abigail Jean-Baptiste
For part of our summer term, Abigail Jean-Baptiste will be interning with us. Abigail completed the IBMYP while living in London and is now finishing secondary school in the United States. 5. Halcyon’s first choir performance
On Thursday 8 May, after the student-led conferences, the Halcyon Choir performed their musical debut for family, friends and the Halcyon community. The performance consisted of three songs, including a lovely solo by Alex Wiklund and two beautifully harmonized pieces by the entire group. The after school choir has been a great place for students from any grade to explore their musical interests and collaborate with a group. This first-ever performance was a great success, and a lovely culmination of their hard work. We can’t wait to see what they perform next!

Choir Members: Sabrina Ciauri, Allegra Beck, Siri Cervin, Clarissa Haile Selassie, Andrea Wiklund and Kate Crannell.

8. Halcyon students making the world a better place
Halcyon’s IBMYP C&S programme aims to identify and espouses behaviours that help generate internationally-minded citizens who “help to create a better and more peaceful world.” (IB 2009)

Every student has to be involved in a “Helping Others” project, and to make this significant, Halcyon is working closely with one particular international charity: UN TECHO. The charity works to provide sustainable solutions to poverty across the Latin American continent. UN TECHO works with young people and our students are the first in Europe to work with them. More >>
9. Upcoming Trip: Grade 6 British Museum
Date: Monday 12 May
Depart: 1235 
Return: 1535

After a quick lunch, we will travel by Tube, taking the Central Line from Marble Arch to Holborn and will then walk up to the British Museum. We will be looking at traditional ceremonial masks from Africa, Mexico, Sri Lanka and Japan. This will give the students opportunities to study the shapes, textures, patterns and colours on these masks and investigate what they were used for and where the shapes came from. Students are planning on making plaster masks based on characters from the Legends they are studying in English.

Students will need:
  • a fully-charged iPad
  • jacket
  • topped-up Oyster card
  • art book
  • drawing pencils
Trip leader: Sue Dance.
10. Upcoming Trip: Grade 8 Tate Modern
Date: Thursday 15 May
Depart: 1235
Return: 1535

After a quick lunch on Thursday, Grade 8 will depart school to take the Central Line from Marble Arch tube station to the Tate Modern. This trip will introduce us to the Surrealist art-works. These paintings should give the students inspiration for their ideas in creating a design for a backdrop of "Midsummer Night's Dream" that they are studying in English. They will also have the chance to write a review of the work in relation to the concepts of the play, through the lens of Surrealism.

Students will need:
  • a fully-charged iPad
  • jacket
  • topped-up Oyster card
  • art book
  • drawing pencils
Trip leader: Sue Dance.
