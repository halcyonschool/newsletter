4. Student Feedback from the Grade 7 Trip to Oak Lodge
On Monday as part of Halcyon's Community Day, Grade 7 students had an exciting opportunity to visit Oak Lodge School for the deaf. This is the only specialised deaf school in London. Halcyon's Grade 7 worked with students of Oak Lodge by looking at ways of feeling sound or experiencing sound, rather than hearing it and also learnt how we can communicate without using words. As a follow-up, Grade 7 will produce an iBook about sound and hearing which they will share with the students of Oak Lodge. The trip was a great experience for Halcyon's students to learn different modes of communication.

One Halcyon student gave us the following feedback on her visit to Oak Lodge:

"I had a lot of fun at Oak Lodge School. I learnt a lot and I had an amazing time! I learnt many things about the deaf students. For instance, usually if they have learnt to speak before the age of six, it is easier for them to learn how to speak; some can't speak, and won't be able to for the rest of their life. Most of the students have difficulty reading and writing. For this reason, when we make our iBooks, we should focus on keeping the language simple, and on keeping the text big. I would like to be able to have a full conversation with a deaf student. I would like to fully learn sign language, so that I can have a conversation with a deaf person, and they can see that I care about them, and that I am interested. Thank you Oak Lodge School, I will never forget this day!"

Shoreditch Street Art
Tuesday 13 October 1000 - 1230
Meeting Place: Urban Street Experience, Outside exit 3 of Old Street Station
£30 plus £1.26 surcharge
Shoreditch is the epitome of London's creative community and has been home to street art from all over the world. This is a great tour to experience a part of London that is constantly brimming with creative talents and just a funky part of town. Even if you have been on a street art tour in the past, it's worth going again as the art is constantly changing. This tour is organised by From My City which is a trusted tour organiser and we have had great tour guides from them in the past. Please sign up directly on this page for Halcyon so that we can keep track of who's coming. For any questions, please contact Stacey Ciauri.

Digital Photography and the Selfie
Tuesday 10 November 0945 - 1200
Art Workers Guild, 6 Queen Square, WC1N 3AT
£25 per person (incl coffee and pastries)
The Landon Art Salon is offering a lecture on the invention and rise of self-portraiture from humble beginnings as hidden painted faces to the obsessive documenting and displaying of our own digital images. While the "selfie" is being billed as a massive contemporary phenomenon, photographers did not wait for the invention of digital smart phones or selfie sticks in order to turn the camera on themselves. The London Art Salon brings together expert speakers in a series of talks and discussion that cover London's current art exhibitions, events and art market. Registration is essential here. For any questions, please contact Anja Beck.

City of London Architecture Tour
Wednesday 18 November 1030 - 1230
Meeting Place: One New Change EC4M 9AF
£30 plus £1.26 surcharge
This is an amazing tour with the former head of planning for the City of London. On our walk around the City of London, we will look first at the immediate setting of St Paul's Cathedral and its transformation over recent decades. Our route will continue via Cheapside, the City's revitalised High Street, towards Bank and the Royal Exchange. The tour will continue through the medieval alleyways off Cornhill and take in Leadenhall Market and the Lloyds Building before visiting the cluster of new office towers which house London's insurance sector. Our walk will then turn north to visit Broadgate and Liverpool Station, finishing on the northeastern corner of the City adjacent to Spitalfields. This tour is organised by From My City. Please sign up directly on this page for Halcyon so that we can keep track of who's coming. For any questions, please contact Stacey Ciauri.
