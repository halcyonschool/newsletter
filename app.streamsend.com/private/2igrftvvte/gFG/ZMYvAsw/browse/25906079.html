    ISA Testing for MYP students
    0900 - 1220 Wednesday 10 February, ISA Tests Part 1, Grades 6 - 10
    0905 - 1135 Thursday 11 February, ISA Tests Part 2, Grades 6-10
    1135 - 1230 Thursday 11 February, ISA Science Tests, Grades 7-10

    The International Schools' Assessment (ISA) tests are standardised tests of reading, writing, mathematical literacy and scientific literacy developed by the Australian Council for Education in Research (ACER). They were developed with the international student in mind and strive to be free of cultural bias. At Halcyon, we will use the results to identify any particular strengths or weaknesses of our students in those areas which are tested. The results are just one of the tools we will use to help personalise our curriculum in response to student needs. You will get a report on your child's results, but the tests do not contribute to students' IBMYP term grades and are not included in transcripts.

    Students in Grades 6-10 will take the reading, writing and math tests on the mornings of Wednesday 10 February and Thursday 11 February. Students in Grades 7-10 will also take the scientific literacy test on 11 February. Students will trial a sample of the tests during some lessons in the week of 01 - 05 February to get accustomed to the online format and style of the questions. These practice tests are not marked and do not contribute to the final results.

    The ISA tests are online tests that can be accessed on an iPad or on a laptop. Students do not need to bring any special materials but will need a fully charged device and may like to use a keyboard with their iPad. Students whose first language is not English may use a translating dictionary and should bring a paper dictionary to the test as they will not be able to access electronic dictionaries.

    Each test lasts about 45 minutes but the tests are not time limited - students may continue working past the allotted time if they need. Students who qualify for extra time in examinations will be encouraged to use the time they need. There are breaks between each test and students will be provided with healthy snacks and drinks to refresh themselves during the breaks. Students are encouraged to bring a filled water bottle to the tests.

    If you have any questions please email Lori Fritz, MYP Coordinator.

    InterQuest: Educational and Careers Assessment for Grades 9, 10 and 11 Students
    We are delighted to offer your child(ren) the opportunity to take part in a unique assessment from a well-established educational and vocational guidance company: Step One who have worked successfully with International students for many years.

    Your student will discover how to make their best educational choices (now and at University or College), analyse future career opportunities and their natural aptitudes and abilities. The assessment considers:
    • what universities require in subject choices and levels so the right choices are made now
    • the demands of future professions that are of interest. Updated with even more careers from around the globe and capable of appreciating subtle differences between the emerging roles
    • how your child is fulfilling their potential – a valuable indicator of academic potential gained through validated psychometric assessments of their abilities and aptitudes
    They will receive a personal report and action plan based on their answers and assessments, which gives all this valuable information and is completed on-line.

    The InterQuest test, detailed report and interview with a StepOne advisor is £180. Payment is via PayPal, which once completed, allows the student to access the InterQuest programme.

    The Programme will be active from Monday 07 March to Monday 14 March 2016.

    Please follow the instructions here.

    United Kingdom Mathematics Trust (UKMT)
    The UK Mathematics Trust (UKMT) is a registered charity whose aim is to advance the education of children and young people in mathematics. The UKMT organises national mathematics competitions and other mathematical enrichment activities for 11-18 year old UK school pupils.

    All students in Grades 6, 7 and 8, and some students in Grades 9 and 10, will be competing in the UKMT individual challenges:
    • Grades 6 and 7: Thursday 28 April 2016
    • Grades 8, 9 and 10: Thursday 04 February 2016
    The participating students will have to complete 25 multiple choice questions. Of these, the first 15 are more accessible whilst the final 10 will provide more food for thought. Gold, silver and bronze certificates are awarded to 40% of participants nationally in the Junior and Intermediate Challenges, and 60% of participants nationally in the Senior Challenge. The most successful participants at each level are invited to enter follow-on rounds; Kangaroos (multiple choice questions) or Olympiads requiring full written answers.

    We will be running lunchtime sessions to aid students with their mathematics competition technique.

    For more information please see the website or email Natalie Carman.

    Raising Bi-lingual Children
    Date: Tuesday 23 February
    Time: 1030-1230
    Free for FOCUS members, £25 for non-members, £15 for Halcyon parents
    Is your child bilingual or trilingual? What languages will your child need for their studies, for their work life and for their family life? Are you and your child’s school supporting your child's home language and culture? Is your child mixing English and your home language, or refusing to speak in your home language? In today’s world bilingualism is a highly regarded asset. In this workshop Susan Stewart of International School of London will share her knowledge on how to ensure children make the most of their bilingualism now and throughout their life. She will talk about some dos and don’ts and what you, as a parent, can practically do today that will have a positive future impact on your child's language development.
