        1. Director’s Message
        Dear Students, Parents and Staff,

        Yesterday’s Grade 10 Personal Project exhibition at Halcyon demonstrated a wonderful way for our community to gain a deeper understanding into the project-based work our students learn to master.

        The Personal Project is designed as an independent learning experience. The project formally assesses students’ ATL (Approaches to Learning) skills for self-management, research, communication, critical and creative thinking, and collaboration. It encourages students to practise and strengthen their ATL skills, to connect classroom learning engagements with personal experience, and to develop their own interests for lifelong learning.

        The afternoon demonstrated the creativity, energy and profound sense of inquiry in our learning community It was inspiring, not least because the students were so successful in directing their own learning, and the variety of projects on show - from clock-making and documentary filmmaking through to a benefit dinner for a charity and building a climbing wall - were all so thoughtfully presented.

        I would like to say ‘well done!’ to every one of our Grade 10 students for their excellent work. It is always wonderful to hear the students talk about their projects, and there was a great deal of innovation on show yesterday.

        I would also like to say thank you to Halcyon’s teachers and staff for their support of our Grade 10 students through the Personal Project. The students would not have had this opportunity without all the support from our team, and I very much appreciate all the additional time and effort involved. A big thank you to Lori Fritz for leading this so well!

        Many thanks to both the Grade 10 parents, who have supported their student(s) throughout the process, and all those students who gave up their time to come to the exhibition.

        The refreshments went down extremely well yesterday, so a final thank you to the HPC for providing such an excellent array of treats!

        Have a great weekend.

        Best wishes,
        Barry

          7. Inspiring SA and CAS
          As part of our Inspiring SA and CAS (Creativity, Action, Service) feature, this week we are focusing on Creativity.


          English Teacher Hannah Shillito says:
          "I am a freelance photographer and writer for various British women's magazines. I have been lucky enough to have had a couple of public photography exhibitions in Milan where I lived before coming to London last year and I also have an online portfolio with Vogue Italia. I am in the process of adding to my website and presenting my latest work which has focussed on Vintage London. I have just completed a Fiction writing course with the University of Stratford which has helped me in my writing...I mainly have published lifestyle articles about fashion and travel so far. There is no better feeling for me than seeing my name in print and knowing that I have created something that perhaps other people will be interested in or even hopefully be inspired by. It always fills me with pride to know that editors like my work enough to publish it."

          Science Technician Tewolde Hagos says:
          "I believe in recycling and highly depend on my surroundings. In my early years as a son of a traditional dress maker, I was able to learn embroidery works, designs and for some time wood-work as well. This was on top of my studies, during school. The tools I learned while I was a child (I have to admit I was not a fan of these as they used to take most of my play time) have helped me to solve puzzles inside and outside of work regarding recycling unwanted items.”


          Art Teacher Sue Dance says:
          "I work in my own tiny studio making paper, lino-block printing, drawing and ceramics, using my kiln for firing the clay pieces. I have designed a ceramic mural for Chiswick House, Kitchen Garden."
