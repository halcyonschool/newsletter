1. Halcyon Chickens!
It has been a very exciting week at Halcyon: 10 chicken eggs arrived on Tuesday early afternoon and by Thursday morning all had successfully hatched. This investigation was part of Grade 6's The Circle of Life unit, and is in conjunction with learning about reproduction in Science. The lab has been full of students, teachers, staff and parents! The whole process was live-streamed to our community, which has been very exciting. You can watch the live-stream of our chickens here:
http://new.livestream.com/accounts/7324873/events/2795053
To see a highlight of a chicken breaking through it's shell, watch this: https://new.livestream.com/accounts/7324873/events/2795053/videos/43549733
2. Learning Through Coding
Grades 8 & 9
On Tuesday 4 March, Periods 1 & 2, Grade 8 will get the opportunity to teach Grade 9 coding.

Coding is the language of the future. It is an important skill which will provide people with a better understanding of technology and will help them in developing programmes. Coding can be a hobby, a career, or both. The apps which Grade 9 are going to use are: Hopscotch and Khan Academy.

Grade 9 students are requested to download Hopscotch app and create a Khan Academy account. Both are free of charge.

Grades 6 & 7
This week, Grade 7 taught Grade 6 how to code, using the app Light-Bot and the website Touch Develop. Students not only learnt why it was important, but even coded their own games.

Throughout Grade 7's previous unit they have been preparing teaching resources, writing lesson plans and even learning about different preferred learning styles. It was wonderful to see all of their hard work come together into a great lesson, and both Grade 6 and Grade 7 working wonderfully together.

4. UKMT - Go Halcyon!
A big congratulations to all those who took part in the UKMT Individual Challenge before half-term. The results are in and the following students received certificates:

Katie B - Gold
Eric O - Gold
Andrew G - Silver
Rachel G - Bronze

Katie B will now go on and compete in the Cayley Maths Olympiad and Eric O in the Maths Grey Kangaroo. Both of the competitions consist of the best mathematics students in the country. So well done to them and good luck on March 20!

A big congrats to Halcyon’s mathematics team for Grades 7 & 8 who finished 6th place in the regional maths final held at City of London School. The students participated in 4 mathematical rounds consisting of relays, cross numbers and shuttles. They were up against over two dozen of London’s top schools. An amazing achievement for such a young and small school!!

6. Halcyon and Un Techo Working Together
On Wednesday 26 February three representatives from a Latin American charity named TECHO, meaning ‘roof’ in Spanish, came to present the organisation’s work to Halcyon students, as part of Halcyon’s Community and Service Programme.

Halcyon students participated in the presentation by sharing their impressive knowledge of Latin America and even the urban slums for which TECHO’s charity work focuses on. TECHO presented some of the vast educational and construction programmes they have completed in some of the most deprived urban areas in 19 Latin American nations.

8. Visiting The British Museum and Chinatown
On Thursday, Grades 6-9 Chinese classes went on a field trip to The British Museum where the students had a guided tour led by the China Education Manager. The one hour tour included Chinese culture from the timeline of world history and students learned how to understand the importance of learning about the culture while studying the language. After the Museum, the class went on to explore the Chinese writings and signs in Chinatown. The trip was a great success!
9. Celebrating International Mother Tongue Day
In celebration of International Mother Tongue Day and the culmination of the Chinese New Year celebrations, Halcyon was visited by a troupe of 11 Chinese performers from The London Confucius Institute. The impressive performances featured the Traditional Chinese Musical Instrument Suona solo, the Chinese Folk Dance Dai and Xingjiang Dance, and both Group and Individual Martial Arts sequences. It was great to see some of the students dancing too. Thank you to all parents who came!
