1. Lunch Survey
For our catering team, this past year has been one of reflection, change and improvement. Our overall commitment has been to provide our community with a delicious and nutritious morning snack and lunch every day. We encourage our students and staff to provide us with live feedback to our menu and service through our daily feedback form. Our Food Action Committee - an advisory committee with student, parent and staff representation - was established this Spring to respond quickly to parent, student, staff feedback to ensure that standards for health and well-being remain high.

Creating and developing the food culture at Halcyon is an ongoing process, and we can only continue to develop if we get your feedback and input. Please take a moment to reflect with your child on this Term’s Menu and fill in the following survey. 

A number of parents and students have mentioned wanting to submit recipes to be considered for Halcyon’s lunch menu. We would gladly review either savoury or sweet recipes for our vegetarian menu. Please consider the seasonality of the ingredients and how practical it may be to produce the dish for 100 people at a time. Please submit your recipe here.
2. Service as Action Award
This year’s Service as Action award goes to Emily and Daphni. The Service as Action Award is awarded to the student/s who:
  • contribute most to building Halcyon's community and promote the school's reputation.
  • care for other people less fortunate and for the environment and take action to improve our world
  • show initiative and independence to carry out his/her own projects
  • become a role model for other students to follow
  • are most involved in the wider life of the school.
We would like to recognise both Emily and Daphni’s for their outstanding Service as Action work this year. Congratulations!

8. Discovery Day: Shoreditch Street Art
As part of Discovery Day, several students chose to explore Shoreditch, home to some of the best street art London has to offer. Students explored narrow streets filled with vibrant, colourful culture of street art and murals. Techniques ranged from stencil art, paste up art, freehand painting, sculptural art, and political and environmental art. Highlights included seeing several Banksy and Shepard Fairey works, as well as, chewing gum “art”!

10. Sports Day
Thanks to Hyde Park and great weather, Halcyon students, staff and HPC pulled off a fantastic Sports Day with tons of participation in a wide range of activities from sprints to mid-distance to hurdles, from the javelin to the shotput. Last, but not least, there was refreshing ice cream to cool off afterwards. 

There were a number of outstanding performances by students from all Grades with several students winning in more than one event. The overall sportsmanship award went to Kieran, Halcyon’s football captain who, Oisin Byrne explained, is so sportsman-like that he even stops playing football to help a fallen opponent up from the ground or happily volunteers to switch teams to ensure sides are even. Best athletic ability went to Jonah for his amazing ability in athletics and other sports and to Dada for for her endless energy and strong ability in all the sports she plays. Both were weighed down by the medals they had won at Sports Day.

Special thanks to Oisin Byrne who came off Paternity Leave for the day to ensure all went swimmingly (plus he didn’t want to miss out on the performances!). Thank you also to parent, Gary Dunkin, for photographing the event. You can view more photos here.
11. Grade 7 Art
Here is Grade 7’s group painting, reflecting their understanding of the Impressionist's techniques with scientific theory of colour. Each student painted a section of Monet's painting of Haystack and explored ways to re-create the colours and brush marks.
12. Grade 6Y Art
Here are Grade G6Y presenting their character masks made from plaster and paint. Both groups visited the British Museum to get ideas from traditional masks. They then used these ideas and created their own character mask from a story of their own and their imagination.

i. Buddy Programme
A big thank you to the many families who have already volunteered to buddy up with new families starting at Halcyon in September. This is hugely important and helps families and students get off to a strong start.

We are still looking for a few more families to volunteer to be a 2015/16 Buddy Family. We invite existing Halcyon families to become a buddy family for one of the many new families joining our school community each year. We would like to pair each new family with a “local” family who will help provide a warm welcome to them. A little effort can go a long way for those families moving from both near and far.

As a buddy family, we ask you to do the following:
  • Call or email and welcome your buddy family to the Halcyon School community and to London (if from elsewhere)
  • Set up a get-together with the children and/or parents
  • Answer any questions about the school, HPC or living in London.
If you are interested in volunteering as a buddy family, please contact Jenny Garcia. Thanks for helping to welcome our new families.
