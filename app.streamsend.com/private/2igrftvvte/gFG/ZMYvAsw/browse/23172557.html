1. If a student feels unwell - at home or at school
As some of us battle the bugs and viruses that are common in the winter months, we would like to take this opportunity to remind you of the School’s procedures in case of illness.

At home:
If your child is unwell and not able to come to school, please contact Reception by phone 020 7258 1169 or email reception@halcyonschool.com before 0830 to confirm the absence and provide a reason. You can also inform us through Managebac.

At school:
If a student starts to feel unwell whilst at school, the student must first inform his/her teacher, who will refer the student to Emily or Mia. If they feel unwell during break or lunch the student must inform a staff member or they may go directly to either Emily or Mia. An assessment will be made and if necessary Emily or Mia will contact the parents. 

Students should not contact their parents first instead of following this procedure. PLEASE do not pick up your child from school unless you have first spoken to either Mia or Emily. We understand that children may wish to first contact their parents when feeling unwell so please help your child by directing them to see their teacher, Mia, or Emily, first.

For our statutory obligations with record keeping, we would be grateful if you could forward us a doctor's certificate relating to any absence of your child if the illness exceeds more than 5 school days. Your doctor will be able to provide you with a note that includes an expected date of return to school.

4. Halcyon’s first assembly
On Monday 26 January, Halcyon held its first whole school assembly. This was organised by the Student Council's Events committee, with the help of Claire Mills. The assembly began with an introduction from Duncan Partridge, who spoke about the importance of working together as a community, just like Emperor penguins! Kate Crannell, Secretary of the Student Council read out the Morning Notes. We then heard an update from Kieran Schmidt-Das, Student Council President, on the progress of the Student Council since elections. There was a lot to celebrate, as Gareth Jones handed out certificates to the students who were MUN delegates to MUNISH. To round off the assembly, Rachel Green spoke about the IBMYP Personal Project, explaining the basics about the Personal Project (PP) and what she was doing for her PP, so that younger Grades could learn more about this process. Students really enjoyed having a whole school assembly and being able to share their experiences and celebrations in this way. 

We hope to use assemblies as a way of bringing the Halcyon Community even closer together, and would like it to be student-led. If students have any ideas or would like to contribute in some way (performance, talk, sharing of good work in school), they should email Claire Mills.

10. Grade 6 visit The British Museum
Grade 6 had a very productive trip to the British Museum looking at the three ancient civilisations that they are researching in both Art and Humanities. Students had an opportunity to make notes on the artefacts that reflected their use of scientific knowledge as well as their creative skills in recording their way of life and using symbols to indicate their culture.

Pictured here, is Ethan looking at the brass-casted plaques by the Benin civilisation from West Africa. The students are making notes and drawing ideas that they may like to use in their own clay pieces back in the art room.

12. Upcoming Trip: Grade 6 and 7 Spanish student to the BFI
Date: Tuesday 10 February
Time: 0945
Return: 1615
Location: BFI-Southbank

At Halcyon we try to enhance our language provision by exposing students to cultural events to deepen their learning.

In this language immersion trip, Grades 6 and 7 will spend a day at BFI-Southbank enjoying a day full of activities. Using a selection of short films such as Emperrado, La Huida and El Letrero, the interactive morning session will focus on themes of interest to young people while offering glimpses into Spanish life and will include films that represent culture in other Spanish-speaking countries - all of which will be explored and discussed.

There will be plenty of interactive activities in the target language and vocabulary extension related to the films with discussion in the target language. The afternoon comprises an introduced screening of a feature film, Zipi y Zape, 2013, an enjoyable Harry Potter-esque film adventure. A teachers’ resource pack will also be included with plenty of follow-on activities. 

The event will be conducted predominantly in Spanish and students are encouraged to speak in the target language.

Students must bring:
  • a topped-up Oyster card
  • fully-charged iPad
  • coat
  • comfortable walking shoes.
For any questions, please contact Noelia Zago.
