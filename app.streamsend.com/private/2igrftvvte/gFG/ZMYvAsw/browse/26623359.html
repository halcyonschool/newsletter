        1. Student Achievements
        Through Both Eyes
        The film group of Halcyon's GIN Club has been working in collaboration with FilmAid on the project "Through Both Eyes". The students are creating a short viral video which expresses the comparison between a refugee living in a Kenyan refugee camp, and a financially comfortable first world citizen. The video aims to raise awareness of the life of a refugee, and will also encourage people to donate to FilmAid's campaign. The students wanted to reach out to everyone in the Halcyon community to visit and like their Facebook page to learn more about their project and keep up-to-date with upcoming events. Please like and share to support our students in this fantastic cause!

The International Orienteering Federation would like to see as many people as possible, of all ages, from all around the world, taking part in some sort of orienteering event to set a new Guinness World Record.         Halcyon’s Collaboration with St John’s International School, Belgium
        On Friday 29 April, Maggie Adams from St John's International School, Waterloo, Belgium visited Halcyon. St John’s runs the IBPYP and IBDP, but does not currently offer the MYP. Instead, the school chose to remove the MYP 15 years ago and create it’s own “world” curriculum. St John’s is now looking to bring back the MYP to replace the one they created for middle school. Maggie’s role at St John’s is MYP Evaluator; she is tasked with the huge role of evaluating best practice MYP and how to implement it at St John’s. After meeting Barry Mansfield at the International School of Paris, Maggie reached out to Barry to get an introduction to Lori Fritz, Halcyon’s MYP Coordinator, to learn about Halcyon’s best practice with the MYP. Lori and Maggie have been collaborating this academic year, to offer Maggie guidance and insight into running the MYP framework. MYP teachers demonstrating collaboration at its finest!

        Maggie described her visit to Halcyon as “inspirational” and when asking Halcyon’s students on what they liked best about Halcyon’s practice of the MYP they told her: “(The) interconnectedness of projects” - they think it’s “cool” to learn one topic across a number of units and use London as their learning laboratory.

        Grade 10 and 11 Final Exams
        Dates: Friday 20 May, Wednesday 25 May, Thursday 26 May, Friday 27 May
        In Grades 10 and 11, students sit Halcyon internal examinations in English, Language Acquisition/Mother Tongue, Humanities, Sciences and Mathematics subjects. Grade 10 students also do an external Interdisciplinary exam set by the International Baccalaureate.

        The exams are an opportunity to practice revision and exam-taking skills as well as to consolidate subject knowledge. Teachers in each subject will provide structured revision in class and in homework assignments in preparation for the exams. There will be no additional assessments or homework in the exam week. The exam results will be one component of the final report grade at the end of the year. Please find a link to the examination schedule here. If you have any questions please email Lori Fritz.
