1. Next Year
One of Halcyon’s founding goals was to create a school community with a higher number of internationally-minded families who were committed to staying in London for the foreseeable future. Our aim was to reduce the usual rate of international school student turnover from 20% to as close to 10% as possible. This would put us more in line with local school turnover. We are pleased to announce that we have achieved that this year, while doubling in size for the 2014-2015 academic year; plus, all of our wonderful teachers and admin staff are staying with us.

More exciting information about next year is that we have appointed an Interim Director. We will be making an announcement shortly - so look out for the news very soon.

3. Community & Service (C&S) Award
Halcyon’s first C&S Award was presented to Katie Buckminster. Choosing a recipient was a tough task, but Katie was selected for meeting the following criteria:
  • the student that contributes most to building Halcyon's community and promoting the school's reputation
  • the student who embraces the attributes of the IB Learner Profile inside and outside of the classroom
  • the student who also cares for other people less fortunate and for the environment and takes action to improve our world
  • the student who shows initiative and independence to carry out his/her own projects
  • the student that becomes a role model for other students to follow
  • the student who is most involved in the wider life of the school.
Congratulations Katie and thank you to all students who have participated so enthusiastically in the C&S programme this year.

As always, students are being encouraged to read beyond these texts. Lenny Dutton and Michelle Jones would like students to move beyond their reading comfort zones by exploring non-fiction works such as biographies or essays, as well as books in a series.

Most of all, we would like students to foster a true love of reading. In light of this, students are being encouraged to enhance their reading experience with audiobooks and iPad apps specific to their texts. To find these resources, type the book title into the iTunes Store on your mobile device.

Avoiding the "summer slide":
It is well documented that students who continue to read over the break return to school more prepared and motivated. So, to avoid these learning losses termed the "summer slide", we would like to suggest the following strategies to keep your child reading:

1. Encourage your child to log their reading on GoodReads.

2. Set a reading challenge with your child on GoodReads, or by following a list such as GoodReads Best Young Adults Books list or GoodReads "The Book Was Better Than The Movie" list.

3. Visit your local library: The library is a great place to discover new and exciting books to read. Let your child choose a topic that interests him or her, and challenge him or her to find five books on that topic. Find your local library here.
