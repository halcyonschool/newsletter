Grade 6 Drama Trip
Date: Thursday 10 December
Time: Depart Halcyon at 1300, Dismissed from Halcyon at 1630
Location: Theatre Royal Drury Lane, London
On Thursday 10 December the Grade 6 students will be taking a trip to the Theatre Royal Drury Lane, Covent Garden, for a backstage tour. Since 1663 the Theatre Royal Drury Lane has provided entertainment for the masses and has been visited by every monarch since the Restoration.The theatre has not one, but two, royal boxes and it was here that the public first heard both the National Anthem and Rule Britannia. Previous buildings were managed by the great actor David Garrick and the famous playwright Richard Brinsley Sheridan and the current, fourth, building was opened in 1812.

Students will depart Halcyon at 1300 and will return to Halcyon by 1630 where they will be dismissed from school.

Students need to bring:
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Topped up Oyster card
- Filled water bottle

REMINDER: Grade 10 Causes and Consequences Conference
Date: Monday 07 December
Time: 0900 - 1230
Location: Halcyon
For parents who have signed up to attend this conference, please note that changes have been made to the format of the sessions. There are only a few spots left so if you have not yet RSVP’d please do so to see if there is still room.

The role of sexual violence in conflict is an issue which has long been ignored, despite its rampant occurrence in conflicts throughout history. Halcyon's Grade 10 students are researching and preparing 'Ted Talk' inspired presentations based on a study of historical examples of conflict marked by sexual violence. Student presentations will be incorporating Facing History Scope and Sequence, such as identity, membership and belonging, case history, judgement, memory and legacy and choosing to participate in fully examining these tragedies while making connections to today’s world. The students will demonstrate how this approach helps to develop a deeper understanding of historical events, critical thinking skills and making connections to the moral choices we face today, in line with the IBMYP curriculum.

We are also delighted to welcome award winning photojournalist and documentary filmmaker Fiona Lloyd-Davies. As part of her work with Pulitzer Center, Fiona has made a documentary film called 'Under the Shadow' about the sexual violence which has been prevalent in the on-going Congo conflict. This documentary covers perspectives from the women who have been victims of sexual violence, the perpetrators and the children born through rape. The documentary also examines the justice system as well as a community based project which aims to tackle some of the repercussions of sexual violence. Fiona will be screening her documentary and taking questions from the audience.

Halcyon students are keen to share their knowledge and concerns about sexual violence in conflict.

Given the content matter, this event is open to students of Grade 9 and above only. We also see this as an opportunity to showcase student learning and welcome parents of the Halcyon community to attend the conference as well based on availability. We are excited to have a couple of schools visiting for the conference as well.

The day will be structured as follows (we have slightly tweaked the structure from last week’s note):

0900 - 0915: arrival and refreshments
0915 - 0930: introductions
0930 - 1000: student talk one
1005 - 1035: student talk two
1035 - 1050: break and refreshments
1050 - 1215: film and discussion
1215 - 1230: plenary

Please RSVP to Stephanie Cooke to see if there are still spaces available.

Introducing Kensington Chelsea Women’s Club (KCWC)
The HPC would like to introduce organisations in London that may be useful to our parent community. The KCWC is an organisation of international and British women who want to enjoy everything London has to offer. They have an activity based club where members can attend events organised by fellow members who follow their passion and use their skills to organise over 30 activities including: history, culture, art, design, sports, crafts and hobbies, special events, languages, book and lecture groups, leisure pastimes, travel and tours in the UK and Europe as well as volunteering. Daytime and evening events are available. If you are interested in learning more about the KCWC or becoming a member, please see here. This is a great opportunity to meet people if you are new to London, or just to get involved in activities you enjoy!


The HPC is an all-volunteer group that works to support community life at Halcyon for all students, parents and staff. We welcome any and all support.
