Community Day Trips, Tuesday 04 October
Learning at Halcyon has no boundaries and with London on our doorstep, we can stretch beyond the confines of the school to learn from the wonderful array of museums, tours, theatres, green and urban spaces that London has to offer. Being part of an international school community we appreciate that we have the opportunity to educate out students about the global community in which they live and study.

Our Community Days afford students the opportunity to learn more about London and the fundamental British values and diverse cultures that our capital classroom has to offer. We continue to deepen our awareness of a variety of British based institutions and their valuable contributions. 

Grade 6
Location: Education Centre at Hyde Park and the Natural History Museum
Students will visit the Education Centre at Hyde Park to complete a range of activities based on animal adaptations. They will then walk from Hyde Park to the Natural History Museum where students will explore various exhibits and review what they have studied so far in their Ecosystems unit by identifying different animals, the abiotic and biotic features of the ecosystems that they can be found in and their particular niches.

Students should bring their:
  • charged iPad 
  • topped up Oyster card
  • rain gear incase it rains
  • money for lunch or a packed lunch
  • filled water bottle
  • any spending money for the gift shop at the Natural History Museum (optional)
If you have any questions about the trip, please contact Kerry Jenkins.


Grade 7
Location: Vicente Cañada Blanch
This Spanish secondary school is managed by the Ministry of Education in Spain through the Spanish Embassy in London. Students follow the Spanish national curriculum from primary all the way through secondary. Halcyon’s Grade 7 students will experience a cultural exchange for one day discussing art, customs and history of Spanish speaking countries and sharing a tapas lunch with their Spanish peers. 

The aim of this trip is to further advance the harmony and understanding of different cultural traditions and practice their Spanish in an authentic way.

Students should bring their:
  • charged iPad 
  • topped up Oyster card
  • rain gear incase it rains
  • filled water bottle
If you have any questions please email Fatima Gonzalez.


Grade 8
Location: Science Museum
The visit to the Science Museum will consist of interactive exhibitions:
  • Who am I? Investigate some of the characteristics that make humans such a successful species, such as personality, intelligence and language
  • Engineering Your Future - discover what engineers do
  • Our Lives in Data - how big data is transforming the world
  • Workshop - construct your own vintage-style radio devices
These exhibitions link to the science and maths curricula (body systems / nutrition and digestion / electromagnetism / statistics).  

Students should bring their:
  • charged iPad
  • topped up Oyster card
  • comfortable walking shoes
  • money for lunch or a packed lunch
  • filled water bottle
If you have any questions please email Miguel Alcantud.


Grade 9
Location: East London and the Tate Modern
Grade 9 will be exploring East London on a street art tour and seeing how artists have expressed their social views at the Tate Modern. This will be a great opportunity to look at artistic expression, visual communication and contemporary graphics. The day will culminate in preparation for a debate that students will have in their Thursday PSHEE class. The debate will be entitled ‘The rule of law vs individual liberty' where students will discuss how law and liberty can both complement and contradict each other.

Students should bring their:
  • charged iPad

  • rain gear incase it rains
  • money for lunch or a packed lunch
  • filled water bottle
If you have any questions please email Gareth Jones.


Grade 10
Location: Tate Britain
Grade 10 will spend time exploring how British artists have expressed themselves through the use of the figure, and how this has been interpreted in both paintings and sculptures. This supports students in appreciating and understanding British Artists from 18 - 20th century. 

Art students will investigate how identity has been expressed through the body, making annotated notes on photographs of images using their iPads. They will further develop their understanding of the figure through observational drawings in their art books.

English students will use the figure as a starting point for creative writing, making connections to themselves and the British artists. They will then write a creative piece in response to one of the figurative art works.

Students should bring their:
  • charged iPad 
  • topped up Oyster card
  • rain gear incase it rains
  • money for lunch
  • filled water bottle
If you have any questions please email Sue Dance.

Halcyon Students Attend USA College Day 2016, Fulbright Fair on Saturday 24 September.
USA College Day offers students and parents the opportunity to meet with representatives from US universities in London, speak with educational service providers and attend a seminar about applying to US universities. In its 39th year, USA College Day is the largest American university fair in the UK and not to be missed by ay student considering undergraduate study in the US.

Last weekend a number of Halcyon Diploma Programme students attended the fair to speak with the 170+ exhibitors. Two of our Grade 11 students, Antonia and Ethan, spoke with us after the fair about their experience. 

Antonia: “The Fulbright Fair was amazing! There are five US universities that I am interested in and I got the chance to speak with each of them one-on-one. They offered me top tips on what research I should be doing on the universities before I apply and offered to meet and give me a tour when I go out to the US to do my university tour. One of the best aspects was that alumni members were present who gave a fantastic insight into life at the university. Overall I spoke to about 10-15 different universities and left the fair feeling excited about my next step in higher education.”

Ethan: “The fair gave me the opportunity to learn more about prospective universities and studying abroad in the US...it was awesome! I spoke to 15-20 universities. I went to the fair knowing that there were a couple I wanted to approach, there were some that I wanted to learn more about purely location wise, universities that I heard about but wanted to learn more and others that I stumbled upon. I would definitely recommend the fair to any student interested in studying in the US...it is a good opportunity to speak on a personal level with the representatives and ask lots of questions.”
Reminder: Lice Check
Last week we flagged a couple of reports of possible lice from parents. Please be aware that your child(ren) might have lice and not know it. So, how do you find out if they do?

Check their head when their hair is wet. Lice can only move very slowly when they are wet and therefore cannot hide. A thorough combing will remove the lice...however not their eggs.

How do I get rid of lice?
  • Wash hair with shampoo and rinse
  • Apply a thick conditioner and detangle with a wide toothed comb leaving the conditioner in the hair
  • Carefully comb through the hair with a special lice comb to remove all adult lice, moving from the base of the hair close to the scalp through to the hair end
  • Then while rinsing hair, comb through again with lice comb to remove any lice that have been dislodged
  • If lice are found, repeat the above steps on the following schedule: twice a week for the next two weeks. This should remove the lice that hatch from the eggs, which may have been laid
  • Remember to check at regular intervals to make sure that lice have not re-infected at some later date
