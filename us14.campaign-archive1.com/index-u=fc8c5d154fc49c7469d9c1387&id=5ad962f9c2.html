Visit Halcyon’s New E-Safety website
At Halcyon, we see digital integration as a way to leverage learning on a variety of levels. We get very positive feedback from students and staff with regard to improved levels of organisation, creativity, collaboration, access to resources and more. 

That said, as this is still considered a newer medium, we all can benefit from the latest information on how to best manage our devices and make the most of the amazing resources available. 

In addition to our digital citizenship classes in PSHE and our Digital workshops throughout the year for parents and staff, Halcyon has made e-Safety information readily available to our whole community by developing our own e-safety website, accessible here and via the footer on the main Halcyon website. Its aim is to provide useful information for students, parents and staff to help keep everyone informed of latest thinking with regard to digital safety. 

Being responsive to our community is a priority here at Halcyon. We trust this resource will inspire and support everyone’s digital usage.  If you have any questions please email Jon Neale.

Spanish Immersion trip to Madrid and Cuenca (Grades 9 to 12)
There are still a few places left  on Halcyon’s Spanish immersion trip to Cuenca (Spain) from 15-23 April 2017 (second week of Spring break).

This trip offers Halcyon students the opportunity to experience nine days of Spanish language and culture by:
  • living with a Spanish host family
  • enjoying a great number of leisure activities and cultural excursions
  • working on an archaeology field project with students from a local secondary school (Lorenzo Hervás y Panduro)
  • conducting research in geology and humanities and visiting two active excavations
This trip will be a joint academic project that will let our students collaborate in Spanish with local peers as part of an interdisciplinary unit that will offer a practical learning experience.

We intend to book flights next week so we recommend you sign-up here by Friday 11 November. 

Cost of the trip: £923.

If you have any questions please email Fatima Gonzalez.

Mission and Vision Workshop 
Date: Tuesday 08 November
Time: 1830 - 1930
Location: Halcyon London International School
Halcyon’s School Board sent a note regarding this workshop to parents, staff and students before the break. In case you missed it:

As a part of our strategic plan, we are committed to reviewing Halcyon’s vision, mission and core objectives. To facilitate this process, we would like to invite you into school to join a discussion asking what it means to have a Halcyon education. Our school’s purpose (what we intend to achieve) should be expressed in our vision and mission and we feel that the current version should be reviewed with the goal of ensuring it expresses our central values succinctly and with great clarity.

The forum is open to all members of our community and the evening will be conducted following a workshop format. We will work in small groups, with some guiding questions, to consider the essential purpose of our school and how best we can put that into words. The discussions and ideas will be collected and taken forward by a working committee, who will report a final recommendation to the Board. The Board has also heard recommendations and ideas from an external consultant who supported the school during our strategic planning last year. 

If you are interested in joining our discussion, do please RSVP here. We look forward to seeing you then - your input is valuable.

HPC: Exhibition The Vulgar, Fashion Redefined
Location: Barbican Art Gallery
Date: Wednesday 23 November
Time: 1000-1200
Meeting Point: the Barbican (more details to follow upon confirmation)
Cost: £36 (including exhibition entrance ticket)
This new exhibition conceived by exhibition-maker Judith Clark and psychoanalyst Adam Phillip will look at the notion of vulgarity in fashion inviting the viewer to think again about exactly what makes something vulgar and why it is such a sensitive and contested term. Drawn from major public and private collections worldwide, the show will include contributions from leading modern and contemporary designers such as Walter van Beirendonck, Manolo Blahnik, Christian Dior, Marc Jacobs for Louis Vuitton, Stephen Jones, Christian Lacroix, Karl Lagerfeld for Chloé and Chanel, Alexander McQueen for Givenchy, Prada and Vivienne Westwood.
