Dear Parents, Students and Colleagues,

The winter break is an important staging-post in the school year. It is the end of the long journey from August - from the warm, late summer, refreshed as we return from our vacation, to mid-winter and the shortest day of the year. There is a sense that the hardest part of the year is behind us, and we now look forward to the New Year, to the spring, and to the successful completion of all our endeavours!  

And this school year, the success we are thrilled to celebrate is Halcyon’s first graduating class! On the afternoon of Saturday 27 May, we will have our first graduation ceremony, and we would please ask you to make a note of this date in your diary; we would like our community to come together, to mark the occasion for the school and to honour our graduates who have been such bold ambassadors for Halcyon. These graduates are already receiving offers and acceptances from universities, in the UK, US and Japan. I would like to congratulate them all on completing what is maybe the hardest term in the Diploma Programme.

We can reflect on the successful changes we have made this year. Most importantly, we have extended the IB Diploma Programme into Grade 12, and appointed our first in-house university guidance counsellor, Dr Mark Bowen. We have pioneered the ‘Explorations’ Programme, which we will showcase for parents on Friday 20 January. We have also developed our Wellbeing Programme by adding mentoring this term. We have a new reporting system (four times a year) and we have re-styled our 3 way conferences to support learning.  Finally, we have also sought to expand our extracurricular services, before and after school. Lots of positive and exciting changes to our wonderful learning environment.

This term, Halcyon teachers have completed all the necessary evaluation documentation for an accreditation visit, in February 2017, by the New England Association of Schools and Colleges (NEASC). This will be another important milestone in the school’s growth, and it recognises our expertise, our institutional strength, and our commitment to learning. I would like to thank all my colleagues for their dedication to improving learning, every day, at Halcyon.

The Halcyon Parent Community has always been wonderfully supportive to our community, and I would like to thank all our parents for their engagement and feedback. I would like to extend a special thank you for the International Showcase - a fantastic way to bring everyone together and to celebrate Halcyon. Thank you to everyone who donated their time so generously to support Halcyon.

To round off, I would like to say a big thank you to all of our students who continue to showcase Halcyon’s values and aspirations. They continue to be leaders, innovators, thinkers, and creators; to support each other; and to challenge themselves and extend their learning. Well done!

Best wishes, and have a great vacation!

Student Voice: Grade 10 Conference
Article Written by Maggie in G10
On Tuesday 13 December Halcyon G10 history students held a conference discussing sexual violence in conflict. Ms. Cooke's class has been studying sexual violence and its use as a weapon in times of war. Each of the four groups of students presenting studied one conflict where sexual violence was a feature, the focusing on:
  • Guatemala
  • Mau Mau
  • Nanjing
  • Rwanda
We were fortunate enough to have guest speakers present. The first guest speaker, Rebekah Read, works with a law firm that seeks for compensation from the government for victims of sexual torture. The second speaker, Kevin O'dell, works with a charity that sends people to Guatemala for education and awareness. Finally, Ellie, Fra and Scott from G11 joined the conference to speak about everyday sexism and how one can go about combating it. 

A student attendee said:

“The conference was a very enlightening experience, I cannot believe (that talking about) sexual violence is still taboo and goes unmentioned in most school curricula, my classmates and the guest speakers did a great job and I cannot wait to attend this conference again next year.”

Overall, the conference was a huge success, thank you to all the students, Ms. Cooke and supporting staff for making it all possible!

‘Re-imagine Learning’ - Halcyon's new speaker series - inspiring speakers for students, parents, staff and friends.
 
We are delighted to launch our speakers series with a special guest, David Benigson. David is founder of Signal, a media firm that uses Artificial Intelligence ( AI ) to help companies and brands make more informed decisions by turning down the noise and turning up the relevant signals.
