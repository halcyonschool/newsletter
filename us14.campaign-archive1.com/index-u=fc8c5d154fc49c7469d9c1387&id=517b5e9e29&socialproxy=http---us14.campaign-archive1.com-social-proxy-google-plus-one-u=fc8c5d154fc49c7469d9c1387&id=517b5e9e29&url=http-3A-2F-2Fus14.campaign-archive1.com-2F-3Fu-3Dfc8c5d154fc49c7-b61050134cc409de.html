IBSCA*: The University Admissions Officers Report 2016
IBDP gains highest marks again as best university preparatory curriculum from UK/US institutions.

“As a diploma student, I have been able to explore and flourish in my selected areas of interest through both the core elements of the Extended Essay, CAS and Theory of Knowledge, but also in my subjects. I strongly believe that without my experiences of the IB Diploma, I would not be the independent enquirer I am today.” - Halcyon Student Rachel, Grade 12

In both the United Kingdom and the United States there is widespread agreement from universities that the IBDP is overall the best qualification to prepare students to excel at university. IBSCA proposed the following question to a broad sampling of university admissions officers:

Overall what score out of five would you give these different qualifications, for how well each prepares the student to thrive at university? A score of one is low, does not prepare well and five is high, prepares extremely well overall.

MUNISH 2016
Members of Halcyon’s Model United Nations (MUN) jetted off to the International School of the Hague’s MUNISH conference in the Netherlands last weekend to discuss Growth and Accountability. 26 Halcyon students participated as delegates, debating a range of issues, such as, the question of promoting universal healthcare for all citizens, the question of religious freedom versus freedom of speech, and the question of establishing measures to ensure the reintegration of child soldiers. 

Additionally, two of our most experienced MUN members took on the role of deputy Student Officers. Kieran (Grade 11) as Deputy Student Officer and Rachel (Grade 12) as Deputy Student Officer of Historical Security Council (1978). 

Alexi (Grade 11), an active member of Halcyon’s MUN club summarised her experience of MUNISH:

“I have attended the [MUNISH] conference for three years in a row and have had a wonderful experience at each of the conferences. The structure of debate allows for each delegate to engage as much as they want, with opportunities to speak in front of up to 250 like-minded students." 

The conference was a resounding success with students able to share their opinions on highly relevant, engaging topics whilst learning about the procedures of the UN as well as hearing inspiring speakers and meeting students from schools across Europe.

Halcyon students were commended by other schools at the conference for their “professional manner”. 

Pat Kyle fed back:

“Education is a road to peace and the delegates’ insight, agency, and empathy in MUNISH was wonderful."

Written by Ellie, Grade 11

HPC: Exhibition The Vulgar, Fashion Redefined
Location: Barbican Art Gallery
Date: Wednesday 23 November
Time: 1000-1200
Meeting Point: the Barbican (more details to follow upon confirmation)
Cost: £36 (including exhibition entrance ticket)
This new exhibition conceived by exhibition-maker Judith Clark and psychoanalyst Adam Phillip will look at the notion of vulgarity in fashion inviting the viewer to think again about exactly what makes something vulgar and why it is such a sensitive and contested term. Drawn from major public and private collections worldwide, the show will include contributions from leading modern and contemporary designers such as Walter van Beirendonck, Manolo Blahnik, Christian Dior, Marc Jacobs for Louis Vuitton, Stephen Jones, Christian Lacroix, Karl Lagerfeld for Chloé and Chanel, Alexander McQueen for Givenchy, Prada and Vivienne Westwood.
