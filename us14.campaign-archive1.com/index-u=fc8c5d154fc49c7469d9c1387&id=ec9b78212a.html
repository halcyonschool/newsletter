Mark Your Calendar: 3 Way Conferences
Date: Monday 14 November AND Thursday 17 November
Time: 1600-1900
Location: Halcyon London International School
Please mark your calendar for our upcoming innovative 3 way conference (student, parent, teachers):
  • Monday 14 November is for families with surnames A - K
  • Thursday 17 November is for families with surnames L - Z
We will send out a link shortly for the booking system.

The sessions are designed to give all of our parents the opportunity to speak with their child(ren)’s teachers about interim semester reports. Please note that students are expected to attend so they can be a part of any reflection about how to improve their learning. The discussion should be as student-led as possible, so we encourage students to discuss their successes and targets for improvement and to outline what they feel they have achieved over the first quarter of the school year. Students and parents are encouraged to read through reports together and consider any questions they might have about the student progress. Reports will be issued on Friday 11 November.

Grade 10 Theatre Trip
Date: Friday 14 October
Time: 1900-2200
Location: Trinity Laban Centre
Grade 10 Theatre Arts students will be visiting the famous Trinity Laban Centre in Greenwich to see a dance theatre production of Company Wayne McGregor's Entity. The show has been described as 'awe-inspiring' and 'cutting edge'. The Times goes on to say:

“(Wayne McGregor's) keen embrace of science and technology, of pop and cyber culture, has made one of Britain’s top-­ranked choreographers” 

This show will help the Grade 10 Theatre Arts class in their study of Rudolf Laban's Movement Analysis, a technique that they will be applying in an original performance to an invited audience at the end of the school term. More details to follow shortly!

HPC: Take a Historical Tour Around Greenwich
Date: Wednesday 19 October
Time: 1000-1230
Cost: £15.00 per person to be paid on day of tour

Meet: at the Thames Clipper ticket booth opposite Embankment Tube Station at 1000 for a 1012 Clipper boat departure to Greenwich

Return: at the end of tour on the DLR
Halcyon parents are invited to come check out Greenwich with a professional elite tour guide and UK Historian Philippa Owen (she makes every tour worthwhile and fascinating).

Philippa says: "Greenwich, the gateway to London! A beautiful oasis on the mighty bend of the river, this World Heritage site looks over at Canary Wharf where once ships not banks traded their wares. Always a royal place, Henry the VIII and his two daughters were born there. The Queen's House and Mary II began the story of the Royal Naval College.Time is measured from there and its story is linked to the history of exploration and empire. A fascinating place, we will arrive by boat along the Royal River as so many have done before."

Please sign up here. Contact Stacey Ciauri 07557520442 with any questions.
HPC: Exhibition The Vulgar, Fashion Redefined
Location: Barbican Art Gallery
Date: Wednesday 23 November
Time: 1000-1200

Meeting Point: the Barbican (more details to follow upon confirmation)
 
Cost: £36 (including exhibition entrance ticket)
This new exhibition conceived by exhibition-maker Judith Clark and psychoanalyst Adam Phillip will look at the notion of vulgarity in fashion inviting the viewer to think again about exactly what makes something vulgar and why it is such a sensitive and contested term. Drawn from major public and private collections worldwide, the show will include contributions from leading modern and contemporary designers such as Walter van Beirendonck, Manolo Blahnik, Christian Dior, Marc Jacobs for Louis Vuitton, Stephen Jones, Christian Lacroix, Karl Lagerfeld for Chloé and Chanel, Alexander McQueen for Givenchy, Prada and Vivienne Westwood.

Please RSVP here. For any questions contact Stacey Ciauri 07557520442.
