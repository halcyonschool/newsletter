Personal Projects: a Glimpse of Olivia’s Project

Our G10 students are working away on finalising their Personal Projects. Yesterday, as part of Olivia’s Personal Project, she organised an exhibition of her paintings with philosophical questions:

"For my personal project I organised an art exhibition that was a form of self expression. The art pieces reflected what I’ve learnt from self studying the subject of philosophy and my intention was to get my community to ask themselves and each other philosophical questions and have discussions. I really appreciated all the feedback I got in the guest book. Thank you”

A Warm Farewell to our Argentinian Students!

Wednesday 15 February was the end of the exchange programme with our two Argentinian students Mati and Dioni. During one month, they had the chance to immerse themselves in Halcyon and London. For example, the transportation. As Mati explained ”when we separated from the group we took the Tube. This was a horrible experience, there were a lot of people and it was noisy, but it was something different and that is what I was expecting from this trip.”

To share their experience, Mati and Dioni had the brilliant idea to create a Google Site and detailed everything they discovered and how beneficial this exchange was for them. 

We would like to thank again our generous hosts, the Bodaghis and Schmidt Das families. We are delighted that this international experience is always a wonderful opportunity for everyone to grow, share and learn from each other!
Halcyon House of Horror - Selection of Scenes

Halcyon Students from G6 - G10 worked together to build a drama ensemble for the first part of the year. Then, they began working on their actual performance scenes. The theme of the overall show was based on horror, to look at the dark side of life. The students performed in a Traverse Theatre space, where the audience sits on both sides of the stage. This type of stage was challenging, but also interesting. Audience member, Beckett commented, 'The play was extremely impressive and interesting to watch.' Many thanks to students, staff and parents for attending. By Lucy G10

CyberFirst : An exciting competition to inspire and encourage girls to consider a career in cyber
“CyberFirst is designed to open up and introduce talented students to the possibilities of a career in cyber. We need young people from all backgrounds. In the UK currently only 10% of those working in cyber are women. This means the UK is missing out on an extraordinarily talented group of people. So CyberFirst is running a girl’s competition to inspire and encourage future generations of young women to consider a career in cyber."

If you want to learn more about this competition, follow this link.
