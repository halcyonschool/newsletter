Grade 7 Canned and Perishable Food Drive

The Grade 7 Students would like to collect canned/perishable foods.


There are 13 million people live below the poverty line in the UK.

Every day people in the UK go hungry for reasons ranging from redundancy to receiving an unexpected bill on a low income. Trussell Trust food banks provide a minimum of three days emergency food and support to people experiencing crisis in the UK.


In 2013-14 food banks fed 913,138 people nationwide. Of those helped, 330,205 were children.

Milk (UHT or powdered),

Upcoming Trip: Grade 6 Backstage Theatre Tour at Drury Lane Theatre

Wednesday, Jan. 28th, 9:45am-12pm

Catherine Street

Since 1663 the Theatre Royal Drury Lane has provided entertainment for the masses and has been visited by every monarch since the Restoration.


The theatre has not one, but two, royal boxes and it was here that the public first heard both the National Anthem and Rule Britannia. Previous buildings were managed by the great actor David Garrick and the famous playwright Richard Brinsley Sheridan and the current, fourth, building was opened in 1812.


Students will need to bring:

Book of the Week

Title: Holes

Author: Louis Sachar

Summary:Stanley Yelnat's family has a history of bad luck going back generations, so he is not too surprised when a miscarriage of justice sends him to Camp Green Lake Juvenile Detention Centre. Nor is he very surprised when he is told that his daily labour at the camp is to dig a hole, five foot wide by five foot deep, and report anything that he finds in that hole. The warden claims that it is character building, but this is a lie and Stanley must dig up the truth.


In this wonderfully inventive, compelling novel that is both serious and funny, Louis Sachar has created a masterpiece that will leave all readers amazed and delighted by the author's narrative flair and brilliantly handled plot.
Available: RM Books
