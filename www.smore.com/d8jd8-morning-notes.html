Celebrating Shakespeare's 400th Anniversary

Storyteller Patrick Ryan Visiting Halcyon

Date: Today

Time: 0900-1400

Location: Halcyon

April 2016 marks the 400th anniversary of William Shakespeare’s death. Halcyon will celebrate the legacy of Shakespeare during the quatercentenary year by hosting professional storyteller Patrick Ryan who will spend the day telling tales and sharing his love and knowledge of the Bard.


In addition to being a wonderful storyteller, Patrick is the author of ‘Shakespeare's Storybook: Folk Tales that Inspired the Bard’, a beautiful picture book where he has retold the origin tales behind some of Shakespeare's plays.


The schedule for his visit is:


  • 0900-0945 - storytelling in the Stern Hall for Grades 6 - 10, with Halcyon parents and family welcome to join. Please share this form for parents who would like to attend

  • 0945-1030 - Grade 6 hearing Shakespeare's stories

  • 1050-1135 - Grade 8 hearing a Midsummer's Night Dream

  • 1135-1220 - Grade 9 hearing the Taming of the Shrew

  • 1220-1400 - Grade 11 will have lunch with Patrick and take part in a workshop on The Tempest


Please note that today is a half-day for students who (except for Grade 11) will be released at 1220.

Halcyon Drama Scene Studies Presents...

Date: Today

Time: 1700 - 1800

Location: Halcyon, Council Room

The Drama Club will present a series of short scene studies of plays from the late 20th and early 21st century. These scenes have been directed collaboratively with a student director, Ellery, and teachers Ms Watson and Ms Foster. The whole cast has been involved in the production of the scenes from stage managing to costume design. Sets are influenced by the minimalist design of Bertolt Brecht so the audience can focus on character. All are welcome to attend the performance, which we are sure will be a show to remember!

Street Art As a Growing International Art Business: Talk for Parents and Students

Date: Thursday 21 April

There are two sessions available please sign-up here for one: 1545 - 1715 OR 1830 - 2000 that same evening

Location: Halcyon, the Hub

Have you ever wondered about the art on London's city streets? The spray painted walls of East and South London? How street art is an international art business? Then come to our latest special parent, student, teacher education evening where we will be hosting a street art expert on the topic and one of London's own street artists, Rich Simmons who will do an actual street art demo for us. We will cover all these areas and a lot more. Rich's demo is of one of his most recognised art pieces.
