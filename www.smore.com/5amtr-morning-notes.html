Grade 8 Rivers Trip

Monday, Oct. 27th, 8:30am-3:30pm

Thames Explorer Field Studies Centre

On the very first day back after October break (Monday 27th October) Grade 8 will be going on their Humanities field trip. We will be going to the Thames Explorer Field Studies centre in Chiswick.
You need to bring with you a fully topped up Oyster card, waterproof clothing, a spare change of clothes (just in case!) and wellies if possible. It will involve walking on the foreshore (mud and shingle) and so old, comfortable footwear is essential.
Packed lunches will be provided by the school, but you may want to bring some change for a snack or ice cream."

REMINDER: Upcoming Trip: MUNISH

Thursday, Oct. 30th, 12pm to Monday, Nov. 3rd, 6:15pm

The Hague, The Netherlands

The Hague, ZH



Depart:

Thursday 30 October by public transport at 1200 from Halcyon to London St. Pancras. The group will take the following route to The Hague:


London St. Pancras to Brussels Midi 1258 - 1608

Brussels Midi to Rotterdam Centraal 1652 - 1802

Return:

Den Haag Cs to Rotterdam Centraal 1253 - 1317

Halcyon has been invited to the Model United Nations International School of The Hague Conference (MUNISH). Thirteen Halcyon students from Grades 9 and 10, who were chosen by a lottery, will have the privilege of representing the nations of South Africa and Sudan at the conference and will debate in a range of United Nations committees with students from all over the world. The conference theme for this year is "Security & Development". You can find out more on the MUNISH website.


Good luck to all participants!

INVITATION: IB Diploma Programme information evening for Grade 9 and 10 parents

Wednesday, Nov. 19th, 6:30pm

33 Seymour Pl

We would like to invite Grade 9 and 10 parents to join us for an information session about the IB Diploma Programme, which students in Grade 10 will begin in September 2015 when they enter Grade 11. This information will be useful for all Grade 9 & 10 parents as the students start to think about their subject options in Grade 10 and 11. This format will provide the important opportunity for Grade 10 parents to ask questions and learn about the IB Diploma Programme and how it works. Grade 9 parents are encouraged to attend to begin the learning process of fully understanding the IBDP and how course choices affect work load, IB requirements and university options.
